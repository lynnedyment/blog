Blah Blah Blah

Riku is learning new things every day and today was a red letter day, with three "awww moments".

I was speaking on the phone with my friend Tay, and finished off a sentence by saying "blah, blah, blah"... Riku looked up at me and said "blah, blah, blah" and after hearing me laugh out loud continued to say "blah, blah, blah" for another two or three minutes whenever I walked by him.

Today Riku called out to me for the very first time; I was in the kitchen and he was wandering in the hallway and his room (or so I thought). Then I heard "Nana?", then a pause, another "Nana?' and another pause followed by a third one. I looked around the corner and there he sat on the fourth stair up, waiting to be given permission to continue up to our bedroom... which of course we did.

As usual, while we were sitting at the table we were flipping through magazines looking for something interesting. Riku has been quite fascinated with some pics of women athletes from the weekend magazine and stopped at one showing a swimmer's back. I told him that was her back, and then patted his and said "Riku's back" and then pulled up my shirt to show him my back. A few minutes later I asked him where his back was and he shrugged sideways with his head cocked over his shoulder to indicate that he did indeed now know where his back was!

I finished off my day by taking Amelia out for a stroller ride/sleep while her parents did some unpacking. She showed her absolute pleasure by blowing some wonderfully loud raspberries at me, accompanied by lots of liquid bubbles as well; it's our favourite thing to do right now and makes me laugh each time.

Amelia and Nana Go to the Park

Amelia is cutting new teeth and napping during the day is just not part of her agenda right now. 7 June 2004

Tuesday with Riku

Riku is learning so much so quickly these days; I write these new skills down each day, as I know I would soon forget just when most of them took place.

Today Riku was playing in the kitchen with a set of five stacking/nesting cups that Gail bought him for his bath last year. For the first time, he brought them into the kitchen and started stacking them. Then he decided to nest them and it was the most amazing thing to watch... he put them all in a circle and then attempted to put one inside the other; he got two right immediately but twice put a bigger one on top of a smaller one. Riku knew that this wasn't right and so picked up the next one to try. He was very methodical about it and I was amazed at how quickly he figured it all out. There was no question that he knew how it should look when it was done, as he sat back with quite a look of satisfaction on his face when he had finished with it. Wow!

Tuesday is ironing day around here, so I set up the board and went off to get the clothes. When I came back Riku had started to place his cars on the board, and thought it was just a great little platform for one of his favourite activities, playing with his cars. The ironing was done after he moved on to something new.

Riku knows his Grandpa is missing (he's in Phoenix) and a few times today would look over at Doug's chair at the computer and say "Bompa?" They are quite a pair those two... a mutual admiration society for sure.

This lovely day ended with okonomiyaki prepared by Akane; Riku sat at the table with us, eating his tomato slices, spinach with sesame seed paste, and long slim noodles with seasoning, oh-so-nicely with his spoon.

Actually the day ended with my losing at the first game of cards we three have played in too many months, but I thought it wasn't worth mentioning...

Susan told me last night that Amelia was a very happy girl in her bath that night and had a very good night's sleep, despite our concern that she would be wide awake due to that late nap. I slept well myself... :)

He truly loved walking on the sand, stooping to pick up rocks, feathers, tiny crab shells, and at one point a long dry piece of seaweed which blew along behind him as he stolled. It was a lovely hour and I took lots of pictures, reminding me of the days with wee Bryn at Ocean Beach when he was this age.

Back to Nana's house for some sitting-on-the-bed-time going through a tiny box that either his Auntie Kelly or his Daddy gave to me many many years ago. It holds a broken string of pearls which fascinates him, and two pairs of clip on earrings which he has me put on and then pulls off, saying "ouch".

The day ended later than usual with story time on the sofa and a couple of rounds of "ring around a rosy" whilst checking himself out in the mirror under the mantle. What a ham! as my Mom would have said.

p.s. Riku is missing his Grandpa. Both yesterday and today, each time he started to climb the stairs up to the main floor, he stopped and asked "Grandpa?"... and I had to tell him that no, his Grandpa wasn't home yet.

24 June 2004

Zoë, Amelia, and Riku ... and Doug too

Today Gail and Glenn celebrated their fifty-seventh birthday! Candy, Beth, and I all sang "Happy Birthday" to Gail on paltalk this morning, one at a time, but alas, Glenn missed out as he didn't join us today.

It was a bonus day today with a phone call from dear Zoë in England. It has been far too long since our last phone call, and both of us have been neglecting email correspondence, due to our very hectic and busy social lives! We talked on her dime for a good while and then I called her back to continue catching up on all our news. What a perfect way to end the week.

I had my weekly chat with Bryn and Caillie as well today. Bryn is sounding very grown up to me; I think his voice is deeper. He is a very good conversationalist and told me that the photo album, organized by Kelly as an end-of-the-year gift for his very special teacher, with accompanying letters and a photo of each pupil in the class, was a bit hit.

Caillie sounds as chirpy and chipper as ever; Caillie doesn't like to spend much time on the phone, but is polite and answers a few questions before suggesting that I talk to someone else! However, she does like to be the last one on the phone; recently we have started counting backwards from a number which she chooses, ending with "I love you" and hanging up.

Amelia and I had a very nice long walk in the sunshine today... two and a half hours from 4:45 on. Before we left the house though she showed me how she can pull herself up to a standing position; she is as steady as can be and Susan tells me she can stand there for a good twenty minutes or so. As I was walking her the last ten minutes of our trip, I wiggled my fingers at her which made her smile and then reach out to touch my hand. We continued doing this for the rest of the trip home. I would reach out, grasp her hand and let go... and then she would reach out giving me the biggest smile when I interlinked our fingers. What a doll she is!

Susan sent me home with take-out Chinese food from our favourite vegetarian spot in Berkeley... a wonderful tofu and vegie dish and lemon "chicken"... yum! I didn't realize how hungry I was until I started heating it up! What a thoughtful and perfect thing to do, and that too was a wonderful way to end my week.

Bryn called to see if I would like to come over to say goodnight to a certain little boy. I did indeed, and we played with blocks in the kitchen, emptied out the plastics drawer and then "ceaned up" which I sing to him as he does so. Riku is very good at cleaning up after himself; what a good habit to begin so early and he enjoys it too.

I helped him brush his teeth with the new toothbrush and children's toothpaste that I bought him a couple of days ago. Oh, does he enjoy that! I helped get him into his pajamas and then walked home, awaiting the phone call from Doug to pick him up from BART... home from the last of his classes for the summer possibly.

Riku will be a happy boy tomorrow to see "Bompa".

25 June 2004

An Afternoon Break... with Amelia

After a fairly mundane Monday doing household chores -- laundry and vacuuming -- it was time for a walk. Amelia was game too, so at 3:50 p.m. we left her house; at 4 p.m. she was sound asleep, and remained so for two solid hours!

Her mom told me she had a two-hour nap in the car this morning as well (Susan got in some unexpected reading time) so she is a well rested girl today. Sleeping is not Amelia's favourite way to pass the time these days.

It was very breezy and cooler on the other side of the city, but we did what is quickly becoming "our walk": to Stow Lake and back, a good long walk indeed. Today I sat for ten minutes on a bench overlooking the lake, hoping to see one of the six great blue herons, two adults and four babies, nesting there, but to no avail. They may have been napping too.

The fog was rolling in on the far side of the lake and it was so thick it looked like smoke, and then disappeared on the breeze... lovely!

28 June 2004

Riku Time

Riku arrived nice and early this morning, which was great, as it gave him time to explore all his favourite spots in the house before his second breakfast and morning naptime. We went upstairs together and I dug out some well-buried toys. Riku was delighted with a new-to-him tin plane, a tiny bear from way back in his Dad's and Auntie Kelly's childhoods, and a boy doll named Robin. Robin was bought when Kelly was expecting a new sibling to join our family, and it was a boy doll just in case she had a brother... which she did! Riku is quite taken with him; they have the same parts... very interesting for Riku.

Grandpa had installed a new program on the computer for Riku, called BabyType, which Riku was just delighted to see. He already knows that each push of the slash key will give him a rumbling truck noise, along with a picture of a truck. The space bar produces a big coloured ball, and lots of noise; his other favourites are a car and a key, with a revving engine noise. A half hour of this a day will do just fine as there are many other things on his nineteen-month-old agenda!

New word today... "vacuum"... and he also pointed out which clothes belonged to whom as I was ironing. It was another gorgeous day here so Riku spent some time playing in the yard on his own, but mostly wanted Grandpa to pay lots of attention to him. He was happy to have me sit with him while we ate together, and put him down for his two naps, but in between he wanted his Grandpa to pick him "up, up" whenever there was a little break between other activities.

Last week I allowed Riku to pluck away at the (Chinese) "moon instrument" hanging on the wall of the living room, as well as a mandolin that originally belonged to Doug's grandfather. Today he wanted Doug to show them to him, and was as pleased as could be to be sitting on "Bompa's" knee strumming away.

We all went off for a walk, hoping Riku would nap a bit, said goodbye to Doug at the BART station an hour and a half later (no nap), and then Riku and I spent an hour in the playground. He loves being up in the playhouse now, although it's a little nervewracking for me as there are two places he could fall from too high a height!

Home for a quick splash in the tub and off he went home with his Daddy to see Mommy. We had a great day.

 

29 June 2004

Amelia on Potrero Hill

Susan booked a well-deserved massage and bath at the Kabuki Hot Springs for noon today, and so Amelia was delivered to us here at 10 a.m. What a treat! After a little nursing time Susan left us; it was plain that Amelia needed a long nap, so off we went in the stroller, all the way through the Mission District on the quieter streets and up to the very top of Noe Valley.

Amelia napped for just one hour although it was obvious she needed more. However, if there is anything at all going on, Amelia is determined not to miss out on it, so what with random motorcycle noises, and petitioners against Bush chatting us up, Amelia decided to just stay awake for the rest of the trip.

We walked for two and a half hours and by then it was lunch time for Amelia... squash, followed by her favourite applesauce. It was such fun to feed her, as she was ready for it; she looked so cute sitting in Riku's chair at the table. She is strong enough that she can push her hands against the table and get the front legs of the chair off the floor!

After that it was play time (and photo time too!)... in the living room on the sofa with Raggedy Andy, standing up against the sofa, and in Riku's room, sitting and/or rolling on the bed. Grandpa also showed her some interesting designs on the computer and walked her around the house showing her the same things that interested Riku at this age... the big clock, the chimes on the counter, and all the various folk art chickens.

Finished the day off with a chat on the phone with Riku, promising him a train trip tomorrow. He didn't say much, but Bryn said he was listening intently, and so we are off tomorrow on a 10:14 a.m. train trip to Palo Alto.
