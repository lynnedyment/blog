Amelia and Nana Go to the Park

Amelia is cutting new teeth and napping during the day is just not part of her agenda right now. So off we went for a lovely long afternoon stroll to Golden Gate Park, walking through the Haight Ashbury District on our way, where one aged hippie told Amelia she was the cutest baby girl in the whole wide world! I agreed wholeheartedly with her, of course.

It was a breezy day, but a perfect one as well, with lots of sunshine and a beautiful blue sky. We spent most of our time in the children's playground, where Kelly and I spent so many hours with Bryn and Caillie on their visits here. There is a fabulous set of curvy and very long slides, where the children use cardboard boxes to make their trip down even faster. One day Riku and Amelia will enjoy these as much as Bryn and Caillie did.

However, today we just sat on a glider, while Amelia slept, and then we held hands all the way home. Once in a while Amelia, while sitting and watching the children play, would reach up to touch my cheek; she was in a Baby Bjorn carrier on my chest, facing outwards, instead of in her stroller, as usual. It was such a perfect way to spend a few hours; Susan got some unpacking done and we had exercise and playtime outside. Hardly seems fair!

I also got to feed Amelia her first applesauce ever. She liked it a lot and leaned forward for more, but once in a while would do a little shudder and make a funny face... these new foods are sometimes a challenge, but I would say I got three stars out of four (for my organic homemade applesauce).

Later in the evening I called Riku for a little chat on the phone. He doesn't say much right now , but does love to listen, and rewarded me with a few "Nana!" s... so dear to my heart. He practices quite a bit on his own play cell phones and gets to do all the chatting on those.

I am a very lucky woman, having these two children here in the city with me. How I would have loved having Bryn and Caillie so close by.

7 June 2004
