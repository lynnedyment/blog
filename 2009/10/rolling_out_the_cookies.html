Mirei Rolls Out the Cookies

In the hour and a half between celebrating Amelia's birthday at the restaurant, and here with a cake, Mirei and I got busy rolling and baking the gingerbread cookies. Mirei has had lots of practice rolling "dough" with Play-Doh but this was even more fun as she got to taste a little bit of it too! The dough is quite hard when it is cold right out of the fridge and we both had hold of the pin at one point, rolling together. But I told Mirei it would soften up soon, and it did, so she got to roll it all by herself. She did a great job placing the cutters so that they filled up the space, taking care that the edges fit in too, and then she would press down hard, saying "wiggle, wiggle", wiggling not only the cutters but her body too! I tried hard to get this on tape but didn't manage to; perhaps I will the next time. We had agreed not to make gingerbread men, but she did cut out two or three little tiny girls and a couple of angels too, and they required eyes, noses, and a smiling mouth, as well as buttons. Using a chopstick and a spoon Mirei managed to get the decorating done too. Here are a few of the photos from this afternoon.
