Decorating a Tree!

Susan, knowing how much I enjoy decorating Christmas trees (I had mentioned this to her the other day, lamenting not having one to decorate here, and hoping I would be helping with the one in Toronto), kindly invited me over to help with theirs today! They bought a lovely slim tall tree from the local lot on Sunday, getting it home on the children's big wagon, and now it had fallen and was ready for some attention.

I arrived about 4:30; Sheena and Amelia had done their homework, so we set about untangling the lights and getting the decorations out of the boxes. Susan took care of the lights, with a little help from Amelia and me, and the twinkling tiny white lights were lovely once she had all four sets on. She went off to bath Finn, and the girls decided to have some play time. Susan put on some lovely Christmas music, and I very happily took my time and hung up each ornament in the perfect spot. Amelia and Sheena came in to help with their favourite glittery openwork stars, and all the soft fabric ornaments as well.

Susan had ordered four pizzas from Amici's for dinner, as well as a big salad, and invited Sheena's mom, Katherine, her partner Greg, and Sheena to stay for dinner. The British Isles were well represented, as Katherine hails from Ireland and Greg from the southwest coast of England. I sat reveling in the accents! They're both lovely friendly people. The pizza was as good as always, the company "brilliant", and the wine delicious. Kevin was with us too, and it was just a grand time sitting and relaxing 'round the table, while the children played happily in Amelia's room. Cheesecake topped off this dinner ... delicious!

I got home about 8:30, and Doug and I went upstairs to watch one segment of Billy Connolly's tour of Ireland ... such good timing, and it just reminded us of how we would love to go back to that lovely green isle.
