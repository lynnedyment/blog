Mid-Week

Another warm sunny day today, and I appreciated it even more after IM-ing with Doug's sister, Sandy, this evening, and hearing how she and Corey stood for an hour in the cold this morning in Hamilton waiting to see Prince Charles and Camilla. When they did arrive, it was raining icy pellets on them all! Brr-r-r! She did take some amazing close-up shots of the Royal Couple; her grandchildren may be very impressed one day at how close their grandmother got to them. I was, and she took great photos too, as always.

We spent the day at home today, and I was busy clearing out lots of paper, making good use of the electric shredder I don't use nearly often enough. If and when we do move, I would like to have all the excess files and folders empty of all but the essentials. Not a very exciting day, but productive for certain.

Kelly is still home, feeling worse than ever, since she is now coughing up nasty stuff from her lungs.

Doug and I are certainly having quieter days together than we have in a long long time. The days disappear quickly, though, despite not having such a busy household.

A lovely photo arrived today from Bryn ... a darling picture of Riku and Mirei dressed in traditional Japanese kimono, for a "coming of age" ceremony. I am not sure when this took place, or where, or what it signifies, but it is one of the cutest photos we have seen of them! I do hope to get more information from Bryn about it, although there doesn't seem to be as much free chat time lately. One day though I will add it in here.
