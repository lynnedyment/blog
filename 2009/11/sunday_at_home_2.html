Lazy Sunday at Home

Many years ago, when visitors came to San Francisco, one of our favourite brunch spots was "The Crepevine" in the Haight. They made the most delicious "scrambles", a basic fried potato dish with a huge blackboard-full of suggested additions. They all had clever California/San Francisco names, none of which I can remember right now, but one of my favourites had tofu and avocado in it. It was always hard to choose, though, as there were so many options, all sounding delicious, but there was almost always a lineup, so that gave me time to make that final decision.

It was cold in the house when we awoke, with the heat down to 62ÂºF, and on days like that what I like best is a big breakfast with lots of hot tea. Doug came downstairs a little later today and I suggested that I make a scramble. I had cooked up extra potatoes last night, as there is always a way to use them up for a meal at any time of the day or night.

I fried up the coarsely cut potatoes with green onion and a crushed garlic clove and then added ... small cubes of a tasty hickory-baked tofu that Doug had picked up the other day, a fresh tomato (well drained), grated old cheddar cheese, a really big handful of chopped spinach, and three well-beaten eggs. Stirred it all up until the eggs were just cooked, added salt and pepper and voila ... my homemade version of a "Crepevine" scramble, along with two slices of the vegetarian "bacon" we like so much. We both thoroughly enjoyed every bite of it!

Doug went off to a double feature today, taking the bus, picking up a vegetarian Vietnamese sandwich on the way for his lunch. I stayed home to work on emails and photos: did a little of both, but ended up spending most of the day reading a "thriller" that Doug had recommended to me. It was a page turner for sure, and I was halfway through its over 400 pages when he arrived home.

Lovely day, it was!
