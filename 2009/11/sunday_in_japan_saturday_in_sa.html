Saturday in San Francisco; Sunday in Japan

It's always lovely to know what is going on in Japan as the distance between us are too far, and the hours between us too many ... seventeen.

I find myself several times a day checking my watch to see what time it is in Kyoto, and what our little family might be doing at that time ... sleeping, getting ready for school, in class, at piano for both children, Mirei on the bus on her way to or from school, or karate practice for Riku.

When we are just getting up in San Francisco, they are sound asleep with that day behind them. About 2:30 P.M. here, during the week, I know the children are getting ready for school, and Riku leaves about a half hour later. At 10 P.M. here I know that Riku is close to being home from school, but Mirei won't be for another two hours.

And so go my days; if I'm not thinking about the family in Japan I am checking to see if the four children in Toronto are still in school, or if Kelly is close to getting home from her day at school.

The only children I can easily keep track of are Amelia and Finn. Thank goodness someone stayed put and are in the same time zone.

Now I know how my mother must have felt when Doug and I moved to Vancouver with her grandchildren!

Susan is so good about sending me two or three photos a day letting me know what she is up to with the children, and so I see Finn at Baker Beach, or munching on grilled cheese sandwiches, riding on a carousel, or scooting along on his wooden bike. And I get to see Amelia at her circus class, getting her hair cut, or playing on a beach with Sheena, her best friend.

Now that Bryn has an iPhone too, I get lots more photos from him, and so sometimes at the end of my day, in comes one like that below; I get to find out that their meal tonight was saginaki... taken at dinner time at home ... and I get to see that there is a little manger in the background and wonder whether Mirei plays with the baby and the animals. It all helps.

p.s. Bryn sent a short IM saying Mirei had done a little skateboarding today!
