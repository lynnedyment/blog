Travelling to Kyoto... and Arriving!

Leaving San Francisco ...

My flight to Kyoto was at 11:30 A.M., a perfectly reasonable time, as I do loathe getting up at 5 in the morning to start a trip! Doug packed up my final bag this morning and I arrived at the airport with two bags to check, both weighing just under the 50 pound allowance. Anything that was overweight I was going to carry on the plane with me, but instead I was able to add some magazines to one of the bags.

Easy flight with lots of room. I had a window seat and sat with a friendly, chatty Japanese man on the aisle, and a seat between us. Over the course of the flight we talked several times; he drew up a small map showing me where Kobe, his hometown, is in relation to Kyoto and Osaka. He strongly urged me to do a day trip there, as it's beautiful and on the water. He kindly wrote up the names of a couple of restaurants in Kyoto where I can find vegetarian food, and told me to be sure to try the long white potatoes which are one of his favourite dishes. I slept perhaps an hour, watched one movie, and finished off a book.

I had two vegetarian meals on the plane, both small lunches. The first was a grain dish, possibly bulgar, with peas, corn, and green onion, served alongside stewed fruit, apples, pineapple, and peaches. It was hot and delicious. I had taken along a tuna sandwich, which I ate right after takeoff. I get hungry when I am on a trip, and was ready for my hot meal at almost 2 o'clock. We got snacks at the mid-point ... a tiny KitKat bar, a package of delicious oatmeal/pecan cookies, and some SunChips.

About an hour and a half before landing we got a second lunch. At first they thought they didn't have a meal for me, but that would have been fine as there was a vegetable selection along with chicken or beef, and it looked delicious, with noodles and veggies. But they found mine after all, and it was another grain, a mound of perhaps cornmeal, and the same hot fruit served along with it. It was filling and tasty, and I filled it out with carrot sticks, grapes, and apples. I had eaten the tiny mandarins earlier. I would have been hungry without my extras for sure!

Our flight arrived a half hour early, at 11:10 P.M. my time. It took a long time to get through immigration, as there were several planes in at once. There was a large sign reading "For Preventive Measures Against Acts of Terrorism. You Will Be Required To Be Fingerprinted and Photographed. (Facial Photograph)." This process slowed things down quite a bit, but I am now in the system. The luggage was slow coming down too, and by the time I had it all on a cart and was out in the main area, Bryn was right there to meet me.

5 April 2009

On to 59-4 Nishigamo!

It was wonderful to see Bryn waiting for me when I finally got out the door into the main area! We had quite the struggle with the big square box (fully loaded), my big blue suitcase (equally stuffed), plus my own carry-on bag with most of my clothes in it, and a small knapsack. The box was not just heavy but extremely awkward to carry, but getting to the train itself was fairly easy ... an elevator down to the platform and a twenty-minute sit while we waited.

When each train comes in there is an announcement made that the train will be cleaned before boarding is allowed. It's more than just going through and picking up any trash, but the floors get cleaned, the window ledges dusted, and the seats automatically turn to face the opposite direction.

There were two very loud and fairly young American males sitting in front of us. They had been drinking on the plane, and were loud while waiting in the station, as well as on the train. One of them was particularly loud and using foul language, and the conductor spoke to them right at the beginning of the trip, asking them to keep their voices down. They did and we were glad of it. On most trains, in most countries I believe, one would just have to grin and bear this kind of behavior.

We took a more expensive but faster express train to Kyoto, with just three stops, and arrived in at the Kyoto station at 6:15 P.M. We realized that we would not be able to get on the bus with these large and cumbersome bags, so headed for the taxi queue, a long and arduous process. Too late I realized I should have taken along the portable wheels, as it would have made this whole process so easy!

Taxi cabs here have seat backs covered in quite "fussy" white lace-like coverings, not just on the passenger seats but on the driver's seat as well. They look like something a very old-world style grandma at home would have on her chesterfield set. It was good to sit back and take in the scenery along the way, as it was a good twenty minutes ride "home". We passed through the busy downtown area, with lots of shops, then through a more industrial-feeling section, and finally into the more suburban area with homes, and more trees, heading toward the mountainous hills Bryn had described so well. They are at the very most northern tip of Kyoto, and as we drove along a river, the banks were lined with blossoming cherry trees. I decided I would take a long walk one day, once Riku is in school for a full day.

There were a few twists and turns to reach the house, and there we were, just as Doug and I had seen it on the computer at home (Google maps). I could have picked it out myself. We came in with me expecting to have two children running to greet me, but instead they were in the bath with Akane, and so while we were waiting for them, Bryn gave me a tour of the house. It's a lovely house, with bright spacious rooms, especially the living room/dining area, my room, and the children's room.

For now there are still lots of boxes around, waiting not only to be emptied, but for places to put it all. There are no closets in the bedrooms, two of which are traditional tatami rooms where dressers aren't allowed to be placed. Once the room now housing most of the boxes is cleared, a large wall unit will be used for Akane's and Bryn's clothes. So I can see there is lots to be done and, with three of us, it should all come together eventually.

It seemed a long time before those children came bursting out of the bathroom in their PJs! They climbed up on the sofa with me, both chatting at top speed, and each of them trying so hard to get my attention. Riku was showing me a new book, and wanting me to find hidden shapes with him, while Mirei was determined that I would talk only to her. It was so good to see those faces again! Riku looks so different with his missing tooth, and now there is a second one, close to coming out, but sticking out from his mouth on a funny angle. It looks hilarious!

He was most anxious to show me some "special presents!" he had for me, and Mirei wanting to show me their bedrooms, so up we went, with Mirei telling me to "be verrrry careful on the steps Nana; they are verrrry dangerous!" They have one long room on one side of the house which they will share eventually. Except for beds, it already looks settled, as Mirei has her stuffed animals, her tiny kitchen, a wee bench and her dolls on her side of the room. Riku has a bookcase with books and a neat array of small toys, plus a bench, Lego parts, and his dresser dividing the room at the moment.

On his window ledge was a small cup with neatly rolled pieces of paper in it, all for me. He had drawn some pictures and of course had his usual love note for me, with "Riku loves Nana" neatly written. He promised to draw me lots of pictures when I am here too,

I tucked Riku in fairly quickly, as both children were very tired from a late night the night before. Riku drifted off very fast, as he usually does, and was happy to have our familiar routine still holding ... a snuggle, a kiss goodnight, and then he rolls onto his left side, holding on to my hand.

Mirei, on the other hand, was not nearly so co-operative. It was harder for her to settle down, as she naps at her French nursery school, making it tough for her to go to bed as early as would be ideal. Instead of sleeping, she was lying beside me stroking Riku's hair and lacing her fingers through his; he was sound asleep, but I came downstairs and let Akane take over, hoping that would make it easier for this little girl to sleep.

By this time, it was close to 8:30, so I decided to settle in, unpack, and get organized, wanting to stay awake until a more reasonable bedtime for an adult. There is a very large shelving area in a cupboard in my room, but no hanging space (which is true throughout the house), but there was plenty of room to get my bag unpacked and tucked away on the base of that cupboard. I also pulled out the cream cheese, cheddar cheese, veggie "bacon", Acme sourdough bread, and the bagels, leaving the rest of the box and the blue bag for tomorrow. I made up the single futon, put the new cover on the duvet, and was tucked into bed at 11 P.M., twenty-six hours after waking in San Francisco yesterday. Off to bed. Yawn!

6 April 2009

First Full Day in Kyoto

The sun was shining through the rice-paper shaded windows when I woke at 6:15 today. I slept very, very well, and was awake when Riku burst into the room at 6:30. He snuggled right in with me, warming up his cold feet on my warm legs. Now I know how Doug feels! Today is the first school day for Riku, although he was there with his parents and Mrs. Goda yesterday for a first-day ceremony, and did go to his classroom and sit at his new desk.

It was a busy morning, as both children had to get ready for school, and Mirei and Akane were going to accompany us to Riku's school just in case there were instructions for Riku that Akane needed to know. It was very cold in the house, so the warm sun when we stepped outside was such a treat ... a perfect beginning to this day for Riku!

Mirei rode on Bryn's shoulders and I tried to pay attention to just how to get to Riku's school. It's a twenty minute walk at a normal pace, and I will describe the turns we make at some later date. The cherry trees I saw in the dusk on the way in last night were just lovely along the riverside, and many people smiled and bowed to us as we passed them by. We heard loud voices across the river, and Bryn explained to Riku that it sounded like grown men exercising before going into work.

The school is very large with several buildings in all. The many steps were lined with flowers in individual pots, which look as though the children last term may have planted. There is a huge outdoor area with a high fence along three sides, where a vigorous game of baseball was being played, and soccer at the other side. We walked around to Riku's area, where he stepped into a corridor and put on his new "indoor shoes", a lightweight basic tennis shoe.

He was very confident and walked right in, checking to see how the other children had hung their knapsacks on the side of their desks. There was no teacher in sight when we left him sitting in his seat, with his female seatmate. The children were all just sitting quietly at their desks when we left at 8:15.

Akane and Mirei left us halfway home to continue on to the bus for Mirei's school. Bryn and I came home; I made some breakfast, and then started in unpacking some clothes. I made neat stacks of them and now they are neatly lined up on the window sills in the children's rooms, while Bryn's and Akane's had to stay downstairs awaiting a dresser.

Bryn and I left to meet Riku at 11:15, as he has early dismissal for the next three days. The gate we had walked through this morning was closed, and so we headed off in a new direction and met Riku in a neat line of students, two by two, holding hands, with his teacher at the front of the line and a helper at the back. They were with us all the way to the main road, and we learned that they will do this for a couple of weeks until the children are more comfortable with finding their own way home.

Riku was full of stories on the way home: he liked his school, he liked the teacher, and the children are nice. So far, so good.

After lunch and some play time at home, Bryn and I headed off to a nearby playground, just four blocks from the house. Riku decided he would take his new school backpack with him, complete with the photo of Skylar from Starr King! His plan was to draw while we were there, something new and different for him. There was a serious baseball game going on when we arrived at the lower part of this big area, and a swing set, a sandbox, and a slide, on the upper area.

We sat on a bench near the sandbox, and he worked away completely engrossed in it. After a while some older boys from the sandbox came over to talk to us, trying out their English, and being so sweet with Riku. When they were ready to leave, I asked if I could take their photo, and they posed readily for me, making sure that Riku was included. I hope he sees them again, as he was quite pleased with their attention.

Bryn and Mirei joined us about 5 P.M. Mirei was her usual beaming self, telling me that she did cry at school, but was happy now! Bryn was running up and down the slide, so both children tried imitating him. Riku can get about halfway up but I bet it's not long before he makes it to the top. Bryn was balancing on the teeter totter, walking up and down, and encouraged Riku to do the same. He was nervous about it, but did it with more and more confidence each time. I know he likes knowing he can do these things, and just needs some assurance that he can. Mirei was wise enough to know she still needed a hand. Just wait 'til next year though! We all got a chuckle watching Bryn stand on one leg juggling on the teeter totter... that Bryn! The walk home was lovely, holding hands while noticing all the things on the way... a very tall grouping of bamboo, a private (we think) garden, complete with a tiny wooden house inside the iron railings, a tiny kitten decoration in a flower pot, etc. I took a photo of them all standing outside their new home.

Dinner was almost ready when we walked in and after eating I gave Mirei one of her tiny baby dolls, along with a cotton receiving blanket that was always on her pillow at nap time at our house. Riku played with the bulging green "Hulk" Doug found for him in Vermont, so they had some quiet time before their tub, Mirei's first in the house. They have all been showering, but of course it is easier for me to bath them. The tub is deep and stainless steel, and even though initially Mirei didn't want to bath, at the end of it she didn't want to get out!

Riku is going to start sleeping in my room tonight; there is lots of room for both of us on two futons.We read a "Henry and Mudge" book, new to us both, and found it hard not to drift off with him when I was tucking him in.

I did the usual email check, some blogging, Facebook time, and then bed at 10:30; I'm getting lots of fresh air and exercise and sleeping like a baby.

7 April 2009

Getting to Know the Neighbourhood

Riku and I had a lovely long sleep-in this morning, waking at 7:15 and reading a couple of stories while snuggling under the twin duvets. The house is cold in the mornings since the heater is only in the living room. Akane and Bryn just keep the living room/dining room/kitchen area closed off with doors, and once we are in there it's cozy. Getting out of bed is a challenge with this warm little body cuddled in beside me, though! I made porridge for the children and sat at the table with them while they ate. Riku was all set to walk out the door at 7:45 but poor Mirei was so upset about having to go to school. I left the house with her crying, not wanting to get her coat on, and it's so hard to hear.

(I'm just not good at that part of childhood. I wasn't when Brynnie was a little boy, and cried at nursery school, junior kindergarten, and even kindergarten, and it didn't get any easier with Riku at nursery school either. And the older I get the harder and harder it gets, I think, as now I just find it heartbreaking, not just horribly upsetting! It doesn't even help knowing that a happy little girl gets picked up at the end of the day; I just want to let her stay home and play. I'm a softie, and it's not even in Mirei's best interests, I know. Which is why I just don't say a word, but get out of hearing! )

I walked Riku to school on my own today, and found our way down to the main road with no trouble at all. I took along my camera and snapped a few photos on the way back to the house ... a lovely private home, a little shrine, and a ramshackle building in the midst of mostly well kept up homes. This isn't unusual though, and I shall continue to snap photos of the various incongruities.

Once at home, I had breakfast, including my usual big comforting mug of hot chocolate, checked my email, and then started going through one of the many boxes piled in the living room. It wasn't long before the 11:30 pick-up for Riku, and Bryn and I walked over together to get him. It was a treat to see all those children, hand in hand, two by two, walking toward us over the bridge. Of course Riku was all smiles when he spotted us and skipped along home.

Riku and I sat together at the table while he ate a big lunch, I went up with him to his room to see all his Lego projects, and we played several games of a very unusual tic-tac-toe game, one in which we each get to be winners many times over. It's fun for both of us,but most especially Riku who. like his dad, doesn't much care for losing. Riku likes a little quiet time on his own, so once Akane left to pick up Mirei, I got a little more unpacking done, mostly tossing out packing material and/or tidying it up into one big box that had already been started.

Once Mirei got home and had a little snack, she, Riku, and I walked to the local playground, just a five or six minute walk up the road. There was a group of girls there playing on the slide, standing at the top, and down along to the bottom, being "tunnels" and they were more than happy to let Riku and Mirei join in too.

Home, tub, dinner, story-time with Riku, bedtime by 7:30, and at 10 o'clock bedtime for me too, still on San Francisco time! I've done well with this jet lag though, by not sleeping during the day. It was a bit of a fight not falling asleep with Riku tonight though. Yawn!

8 April 2009

Our Little French Miss!

My days are settling into a routine, with the mornings helping with the children as they prepare for their school day, and then my walk to school with Riku, my walk home, and some puttering about the house. Akane is off taking Mirei to school, and usually stays out for most of the day. Bryn is working, we have some breakfast, he washes the dishes and I dry them, and the morning slips by.

I took a photo of Riku and his classmates being walked to the crosswalk today. Riku spotted me and gave me a big smile. We hold hands and chat all the way home. He doesn't talk about school so much, but about what he wants to do or what he was thinking about. It's hard to get any information out of his about what he does with his time there!

We do pass some interesting houses on the way home, and over the course of my time here, I will include some of them in my blogs. Today we passed a shrine outside a house, and Riku quite liked the little apron-like cloth covering most of it.

Riku is always ready for lunch, even after eating his little cheese cracker snacks (brought from San Francisco) on the way home. Then he's ready for some quiet time in the house for a while. Today that didn't last long and he was most anxious to get to the playground. Bryn and Akane had gone out for a noodle lunch, so I did up the few dishes, made myself a small thermos of tea, and off we went. It's hot in the afternoon, and Riku had his new hat on to keep the sun off his face. Our usual bench was busy with moms and toddlers so we chose a bench at the side of the play area today, a good spot for Riku to watch the baseball players, rescue a couple of balls for them, and water the dry bushes with a bottle he found. He had some big drinks of water, went down the slide a few times, and picked me some wild flowers.

He had a piano lesson scheduled for 4:30 today, a try-out session to see if it would be a good fit between him and the teacher. So, Bryn and I left the house to pick Mirei up from her Ecole Francais today. It was my first time on the bus here, and the drive further south into Kyoto was interesting, with the usual mixes of ugly new buildings tucked behind tiny wooden structures, lots of overhead wiring, beautiful blossoming cherry trees in varying hues of pink/white, and a lot of Caucasian tourists here for those blooms. It's about a five minute walk to the bus stop, and we had to wait five minutes for the bus, but we arrived at the school at five minutes past four, despite our having left a bit late.

I had heard so much about Mirei's handsome male teacher, and he is indeed. Mirei came out onto the porch smiling her big smile and Bryn had some time to talk to him about their routine. It's a busy day with playtime, outdoor play, painting/drawing, and a one hour nap following lunch. They do waken the children at 2, but that hour makes it hard for Mirei to settle in as early as she should. Akane had told us she would be hungry when she got out, so we stopped in at "Petit Pâtisserie" for a snack for her. She chose a round "pizza" and amazed us both with how quickly she ate it at the bus stop!

Of course she gets much attention on the bus, as she is so vocal, very inquisitive, and not always the quietest little girl. Women especially respond to her though in a very positive way, smiling at her, and a few of them spoke to her as well. The bus was crowded, but a kind woman offered us her seat, which meant Mirei could see out the window and comment on ever so many things. There was a woman sitting next to us, who asked where we were from. I told her Mirei was born in Japan but had been living in SF until last month. She volunteered that her daughter lives in Washington, and that she had been there to see the cherry blossoms in that city last year. Another daughter is pregnant and living nearby and she is most anxious to be a grandmother.

Tonight while Mirei was sitting on my bed, playing with her dolls, I heard her counting in French to them ... un, deux, trois.

Riku's piano lesson went very well and he will be going for classes every Thursday right after school. He liked the teacher, and seemed happy about his class.

It was late by the time they got home, so Akane made udon noodles with veggies and fish cake for the children, I tubbed them both, and Riku was sound asleep at 7:45, after one chapter. I woke at 8:10, having decided to just lie beside him for a minute, but awoke thinking it must be an hour later. I like these long lazy days!

Akane tucked in Mirei, and that was the last we saw of her for the night! We could hear Mirei chattering away just as she did for Bryn the night before, when he fell asleep with her!

Eventually, Bryn and I did the dishes, and after answering just a couple of emails, I decided to work on my family tree for a while. It was very relaxing and I happily turned in to read at 10:30.

9 April 2009

It's The Weekend!.. well, Friday, so Close!

That's what Bryn told the children this morning; their last day of school for this week and lots to look forward to.

The children are very excited about Easter coming up, with only two more days to go! I brought Easter decals for the windows for them from SF, and two days ago we stuck bunnies and eggs and chicks up on the living room, dining room, and bedroom windows. They are so looking forward to colouring Easter eggs tomorrow; Auntie Ayako will be joining them too, as she is coming to visit for the weekend. They will be able to give her some pointers, as she has not likely coloured eggs before.

We had our usual morning here getting ready for school. Riku and I both woke at 6 A.M. but I thought it was 7! So back under the covers we went, with time for another chapter in our book "The Phantom Toll Booth".

It was quite a leisurely morning with that extra half hour, so Riku had time to draw; Mirei drew and wrote some letters... M, X, and O, as well as Xs and Os for kisses and hugs for me. While saying goodbye to us at the door at 8 A.M. she told me she wasn't going to cry at school. She has said this, I believe, each day since starting, but today she meant it! We were so excited when Akane told us she had a tear-free beginning to her day!

I asked Riku to lead the way to school this morning, and even though he told me he didn't know which way to turn, each time I asked him, he always chose the right way. Eventually he will be walking to school either on his own, or with some neighbourhood children, so we want him to start being aware of where he is and where he is going.

After leaving Riku at his classroom, I stopped to take some photos at the school... boys- double-Dutch skipping(!), girls lined up doing single skipping, the children playing baseball and soccer, the bright flowers in their bright pots, and the school itself from the main road. On my way out of the school grounds, a group of five boys walked up to me and one of them said "Hello!" This has happened occasionally since I arrived, with a child wanting to speak English to me, but it was interesting to have five boys in a group do so. They all took turns greeting me and then one boy told me his name, pointing to his chest. The others followed after I told them my name. One asked me where I was from, and I said "Canada" which elicited oohs and ahhs. It was a very sweet encounter and I hope to have many more of them.

The three hours at home passed quickly today, as I started sorting through the big boxes in the living/dining room to get some order established. Soon there were piles of clothing for each person established, then boxes for storage, children's games/books/toys, wrapping materials, etc. Four boxes later it all looked much organized, even while we wait for permanent homes for most of it. A nice lunch of egg and toast and tea, and it was time to pick Riku up. Today was the first pick-up at 1:30. I thought this was the regular day's end from now on, but this is a second transmission period and eventually he will be going until 2:15, a half-hour shorter day than in San Francisco.

They were later than usual crossing the bridge today, so I had some time to stroll along the river bank. As the group came closer I could see that Riku looked quite sad. He had one of those days that children inevitably have at school; a boy copied his work (which he didn't like at all), the girl who sits beside him and with whom he has to hold hands on their way to the bridge insisted on going too fast, which had them walking too closely behind the children in front (at least for his taste!), and he said an adult — not clear who it was — told him he couldn't cry when he bumped his head. So we talked about it all on the way home and then he talked about it with his dad, and that seemed to get it out of his system.

He had his first lunch at school today (pasta, bread, spinach, and an apple in some form ... perhaps applesauce) and he enjoyed it all very much. Nice for Akane not to have to make his lunch too!

Riku had a snack at home, and then we went off to the playground for a very short visit. Today he wanted to do some Chinese writing there! So off he went with his backpack packed full again, and chose a different bench to work on. He was quite pleased with his full page, and then had some time to practice his balancing walking up and down the teeter-totter.

Bryn had gone out for some time on his own, and we met at home to pick Mirei up from school again.She was a happy girl, happy to see us, and so proud of herself for not crying today. Bryn treated her to another round of pizza from the patisserie, and we were lucky enough to catch a bus immediately. Today Mirei chatted up an elderly man, who had a red feather in his hat. She called out "Hello!" (in Japanese) to him in a very loud voice, and then when Bryn reminded her to speak more quietly, called out to him using a few different phrases trying to catch his attention. He was trying hard to not pay any attention at all (I find men here in general most passive when it comes to interacting with anyone; I often feel invisible as they often refuse to meet my eyes, even when it would be so easy to acknowledge one another's presence). So, Mire, being her persistent self, refused to give up, and he finally gave a little wave to her as he walked by the window after exiting the bus. This is one determined little girl!

Riku had a follow-up visit to a dermatologist today, and the clinic hours were 6–9 P.M. He, Akane, and Bryn left before 6, hoping to get in early, and Mirei and I walked to the playground. It was hot, but she enjoyed some time on the slide and the teeter-totter, and she enjoys the walk there and back as well. At home she spent quite a bit of time being a "mommy", busy with her babies in both beds and strollers! and had a cool drink, waiting for everyone to arrive home. Today she wore a little Japanese jumper to school, and she just looked like a tiny Japanese grandma to me.

Today on his hour away, Bryn had met the owner of a restaurant/bar, and found him very friendly. The fellow was quite chatty, had been to SF and especially liked Chinatown, and has a young family as well, a boy and girl exactly two years younger than Mirei and Riku. So, Bryn suggested we all go for dinner there tonight. It was a little late by the time they arrived home — 7:30 — but we went anyway. The children are always either in bed by that time, or very close to being tucked in.

Service was quite slow, unfortunately, but the children were very well behaved as they always are in restaurants. Riku drew, of course, and Mirei chatted away to us, asking lots of questions about the nearby patrons. The food eventually arrived, all but my dish of vegetable fried rice. The children ate well and enjoyed it, and Mirei astounded us by using adult-sized chopsticks incredibly well! Unfortunately, I didn't have my camera with me, but she looked an old hand at it!

My dish showed up just as the smoking patrons were getting to me, so Mirei and I headed off in the dark for home. It was a lovely night, and we looked at the stars and for the moon. Bryn's beer was 500 yen ($5.00) tonight because it was a full moon, but we didn't get to see it.

I had Mirei all set for bed when the others arrived home with the take-out containers, and then I tucked Riku into bed. I wasn't too long out of bed myself, but did take a few minutes to eat a half cup of my dinner, and then a half hour to do a little Ancestry family tree work, answering an inquiry from someone on Doug's mom's maternal line. That was enough to relax me and head off to bed.

10 April 2009

At Home

Ah, Saturday! Riku got to spend his early morning in his very favourite way, snuggled up reading beside me. He is such a reader and if we aren't reading together he is going through his books on his own. Mirei joined us in bed too, and we had a lovely slow beginning to our day. After breakfast, Riku and Akane headed out for an Chinese evaluation class for Riku. He spent one and a half school years, plus summer school last year, working hard on that language. He's good at it, has a good ear, and enjoyed learning it, so it would be a shame for him to lose it at this stage. It seems there may be one boy in his class, as they seem to be at the same level.

Meanwhile, Mirei was keeping busy around the house, using her Daddy as a table at one point, doing a puzzle, and we read some books together. She loves to "do my homework" too, and draw and colour so our time passed quickly. She wanted to do her "homework" after that, and printed the letters W, B, and R. I love the serious look on her face, with her tongue sticking out, ala Gram.

Yuu-kun was here to play with Riku at 2:40, and of course Mirei was happy to tag along after them, running to keep up and get Yuu-kun's attention.

28 April 2009

A Mid-Week Break for Riku, Space, Vincent and a Nest-Builder

This is the beginning of "Golden Week", a Japanese spring break, so Riku had today off from school. We were lying in bed looking through the new book on space that Akane had bought for him last night, when Mirei joined us. How I wish I had set up a tape recorder as I sat listening to these two cuddled together in my cozy bed. Here is a bit of the scenario and dialogue... "Mirei, do you want to learn about space?" "Yes Riku, I do." Riku closes the book, and says "Mirei, I want you to think about space for a minute. Close your eyes." and watching her, says again "Mirei, close your eyes." When she has done his bidding, he said "I want you to think about space for a minute. Imagine millions of stars." This went on for some time, with Riku opening the book, showing her a picture, telling her about it, and continuing on. He sounded so much like his daddy!

Gail called to say hello to Riku, as he was at school when she called and spoke to Mirei. However, Riku's hearing is not good, thanks to those stuffed up sinus passages so I ended up chatting with her instead after he gave up.

We all ate breakfast together today, a rare event, and it was just wonderful. Riku had drawn a picture with lots and lots of stars, and an astronomer way down below, looking upward. I told him it reminded me of a painting by a famous man called Vincent Van Gogh who also painted stars. Bryn found the painting on the Net' I was referring to and showed him some others as well, including his small but tidy room.

I ended up telling Riku a bit about his life... his medical problem with his ears, his supportive brother, how he loved to paint the stars and flowers. That led to the lovely song by Don McLean called "Vincent" but that I always call "Starry Starry Night".

After breakfast it was piano practice and spelling time for Riku, so Mirei and I went to my room with Akane's computer and spent all our time there looking at videos of her and the other children... at the beach with Amelia and Riku, Mirei with Riku when she was very young, not yet walking, her cousins in Toronto, birthday parties with Auntie Susan, Kevin, Mari, baby Finn, Brent. Riku joined us when he was finished, chuckling once more over the video that his cousin Bryn.

Pieter, the friendly neighbour we met two weeks ago, stopped by with four lovely big ripe tomatoes for us, and had some time to chat with Bryn too. He told Bryn about a shuttle to the airport which sounds a much cheaper, faster, and more comfortable trip than the trains/bus route they have been using. Riku asked Pieter where his girlfriend was, which made Pieter laugh; Kiku is his wife, and the children like her too.

After lunch, I took the children to the playground up the street. It was hot and the children explored a little trail at the far side, finding lots of flowers and bugs to check out. It was just the quiet time outdoors that both children love. There was time for a little Frisbee throwing although at one point, the Frisbee went into a gated pool area with a high fence around it. Riku, to my surprise, walked down to a dad tossing balls to his son, and asked him if he could get it for him! We all clapped after he made that climb on our behalf. Riku found some boys on that lower level with collectible cards, and sat down and joined them, again to my surprise. They were very generous about letting him handle them, they chatted with him too, and let Mirei look at the cards too when they went off to a shadier spot themselves.

We got home to find empty boxes at the foot of the stairs, and Akane and Bryn upstairs sorting through more. Bryn had set the children's colourful rug on the floor, and their twin beds up in their room, although until there are shades covering up their many windows, they likely won't be sleeping in there. It gets so light so early! But, that set Riku off sorting and rearranging all the toys/bins/and boxes of his belongings. He is a boy that takes after his Nana; he wants his things organized asap, and enjoys doing it. He's a nest-builder for certain! Mirei was happily sitting on her bed, making up a smaller bed for her babies, and tucking them in. She too is a nest builder too in her own female way.

And then followed... Riku's second piano practice (he missed last night), and I had some computer time while Mirei did her homework. naming her letters with her dad, and enjoyed some story time with him too.

Akane and Bryn walked out to find a desk, and the children had some playtime together with a big yellow plastic bus, a gift from Riku's third or fourth birthday. Akane made big bowls of noodles, broth and veggies for the children's dinner, they had a long shower together with their dad, and I got them ready for bed. Riku had been playing with his robot and hated to stop, but I promised him he could have it first thing in the morning. The last thing he said to me was "Are you sure you will remember?"

The desk was delivered tonight (!), right up to the bedroom/study (free delivery, as this was, or very cheap delivery service is very common here, but it's Sunday and the delivery was after 6 P.M., so I was very impressed!)

Bryn made Akane and me toasted tomato sandwiches for dinner and did up the dishes. What a treat! Akane fell asleep with Mirei at 7:45, Bryn was working in his new office upstairs, and I had lots of time to go through photos, answer emails, and blog! What a productive and fun day for all!

29 April 2009

A Dozen, a Baker's Dozen, and Mirei is Printing "Nana"!

This morning on the way to school I spotted a ladybug in the bushes again, and pointed it out to Riku. We didn't have much meandering time but in the short distance we still had to walk, Riku spotted twelve in total. I pointed out to him that made a dozen, a new--to-him term, and then told him about a baker's dozen when he found one final one. He liked the idea of a "free" extra. I told him of course about that extra cookie or donut at the bakers when I was growing up.

Mirei and I took another long walk today, this time with the goal of buying "sultana biscuits" as they were known when I was a little girl. Mom would almost always pick up a large package of them on Thursday night (our grocery night), and the entire family liked them except Don. He called them "crackers with bugs in them."! I spotted them on our last walk but hadn't taken money with me.

Today Mirei asked if she could take her babies for a walk too, so off we went with two babies in a tiny stroller. Mirei had a pack on her back, and a sweet funny pink purse Auntie Candy had sent along for her, so she looked quite the part of the overburdened mother!

We met Yuu-kun's grandmother (Baba) on our way there, and said "hello" in Japanese to lots of friendly people. Mirei has an opinion, and not favourable ones, about every person who doesn't reply to her! They are, in her humble opinion, one of the following... mean, not happy, not nice, and she always tells me she doesn't like those people. I have never understood how anyone can totally ignore a friendly child's greeting, but they're still out there.

Mirei and I were wandering around looking and ran into Akane! She had gone out earlier on her bike, and stopped in for a few items too. After saying goodbye, we continued with our shopping spree and lucked out. Mirei chose two containers of creme caramel, for her and Riku, we bought two packages of the cookies, much smaller than ours at home, and then lucked out when we spotted a small bag of half-sized marshmallows! I was going to bring some with me for our hot chocolate the next time I visited, so we were very excited to find these here.

I took a few photos in the store... of a single cob of corn, shrunk wrapped in plastic, and of some carrots, and noted the prices of some everyday items too. And then Mirei carefully took all our food and placed it on the check out counter; how disappointed we were to learn they don't take credit cards there and had to leave it all behind! The corner stores do, as did one of the larger grocery stores on Tuesday, but this particular one is cash only. I carried home the stroller and babies, as Mirei was tired of all the work by then.

We went out for lunch, had some quiet time, and when Akane and Bryn left at 1:15 to meet Riku at school and walk with him to his piano lesson, Mirei and I headed out to the local playground. For the first time ever, we were the only ones there for the entire time! We threw and rolled the ball we took along to each other, and Mirei sat on my lap in the shade for the longest time while we watched all the ants scurrying here and there right below our feet. At 2:45 we decided we could walk toward the river and see if we could meet up with Riku and his parents at the bridge. It was a long walk for this little girl and she yawned about four times, so we went right home after not spotting our crew. They were home already, having taken a different way home.

Riku had a nice long evening to himself tonight, which he thoroughly enjoyed. He read to me before his tub, early tubs for them both, and long ones too. Riku was the last out and he and I spent five minutes squirting water at each other from narrow nozzled plastic thingies, each time I opened the door to the tub area. My guess is we will be playing this game until I leave. We started it last night, and he's pretty sneaky, as he got out of the tub at one point, hiding behind the accordion door, so I was a very handy target!

Akane prepared a delicious dinner with spring cabbage lightly cooked, with tofu strips, a rice/barley mix which she noticed Riku has at school, lots of tiny cucumber slices, sushi for them (Riku got Bryn's one piece!), a big bowl of fresh tofu cooked in a tasty broth (which Mirei and I both particularly liked), and a small bowl of kabocha squash, cut in fine strips, with onion, and thinly sliced potato, all in a golden miso broth.

We were nice and early and ahead of schedule for our usual evening routine, so Riku was allowed to read all through dinner. He did a great job eating, but was totally concentrating on his book between bites. I know what a pleasure that is, to just read and relax while eating, and it's a rare treat for sure for Riku.

Mirei has been printing her name very nicely now for four days, and tonight I showed her how to print "NANA". She did the most amazing job with it, and then wrote it from memory, on the opposite side of the paper, but writing NAAN instead, but she remembered all the letters so we were impressed anyway. She had insisted on "doing my homework" while Riku did his spelling list with Bryn... twenty-five words with some new ones mixed in tonight, and a happy boy with his 25/25 score!

Riku was asleep tonight at 7:45, and I slept until 8:15!! Akane had fallen asleep with Mirei, so I got busy with emails and my blog, finishing off the night by going through a massive number of photos to get them down to just six per blog. Bryn has the program working to get photos again, and I am very excited to have it done! Still lots to catch up on with those pics, but slowly but surely they will get done. It's just so darned hard to choose those six though...

Mirei woke again tonight, crying. Still sick with a heavy cold, and tonight her Daddy is sick too with another sinus infection, it seems. Riku, happily, is improving!
