Murphys and Angels Camp

We set off at 9:30 this morning, heading almost due east, to Murphys, California, a small town of about 2,000 people. It was in its heyday in the late 1850s and was one of the famous gold mining towns. The population back then was about 3,000, and unlike most mining towns it remained a viable place to live and visit because of the mountain scenery and the nearby caverns.

The temperature was to be 89F today, and even though it was a hot day for sure, it didn't feel that hot to us. Doug and Tom found it hotter seated in the front seat, but Gail and I were perfectly comfortable. The first part of our trip was on the highway but the last half was on a smaller two lane road, and we enjoyed the country scenery all the way to Murphys.

It was time for lunch when we arrived, and we walked from one end to the other to check out our choices, settling on a small bakery/sandwich shop. The food was delicious; Doug and I had tuna on cornmeal bread, Gail had a veggie sandwich on cornmeal, and Tom had a turkey sandwich on focaccia. For a dollar extra it came with a small portion of tasty potato salad, two strawberries, and a few thick potato chips ... an overabundance of potatoes, for sure!

However, over the next two hours we walked it off. First stop was the local pokey, where Doug and Tom posed for us at the door. It really was the local spot for bad guys at one point, so was very interesting for sure. We took lots of photos of the lovely old stone and frame buildings, built in the 1860s onward. The local church was built the same year as the saloon, and Murphys Hotel had many interesting visitors in those days: Ulysses S. Grant, Mark Twain (aka Samuel Clemens), Bret Harte (an author), Thomas Lipton (of Lipton tea), J.P. Morgan, and John Jacob Astor of the Astor family. There was a plumbing store featuring antique parts for bathrooms, kitchens, and the home in general, and Doug found three old keys from Britain that he always keeps an eye out for. He was very pleased with his purchase.

Toronto had moose, painted up and placed around the city, Nanaimo had painted whales last year, and Murphys has frogs with gold pans (because of Mark Twain's famous story, "The Celebrated Jumping Frog of Calaveras County"). I couldn't resist taking a photo of one of them.

We stopped in at a candy store before leaving, and everyone bought a treat... Gail and I got a bag each of wonderfully tasty coconut brittle, Doug bought peanut brittle and caramel, and Tom bought chocolate with chili oil.

Angels Camp, less than half an hour away, was our next stop, another gold mining town that survived after the miners had left. Angels Camp is 50% larger than Murphys, so we found it odd that a huge percentage of its stores were closed at 4 o'clock on a Friday afternoon. Gail and I were more than ready for our tea, and we found the perfect spot, a large airy non-profit business, with lots of food and beverage choices. Happy Birthday to Me!

My first call of the day started off with a lovely phone call, complete with a rousing rendition of "Happy Birthday to You!" from Amelia, Finn, Susan, and Kevin. I had been lying in bed earlier thinking they would be the first to call, since Amelia is going to a summer camp this week and wouldn't be home until later on. So, it was with delight that I heard those voices first thing in the morning.

My second call of the day was from Judith Sarkany. It has been months since we connected and it took us over an hour and a half to even start to get caught up! By the time we said goodbye, it was time to eat, and get ready to head for the airport.

I wrote in my previous blog entry about Bryn and Riku's arrival and my day with Riku after that, ending with all the fun Riku had at the playground on 23rd Street in the Mission. Here is the rest of the story ...

Bryn and Doug picked us up at the playground about 5:15, and asked if we would like to have dinner at "Little Star" pizzeria, one of our favourite pizza spots, although we usually take the pizza home. This is always a very busy spot, but we lucked out and got a table right away. Riku spent the time drawing a very elaborate picture of a hammock with all the details of a lazy afternoon included... a bowl of fresh fruit, a purse, sunglasses, drinks, table, palm tree. Sweet! We all enjoyed every bite of our two different types of deep dish pizza, and then headed for home.

Bryn had a lovely bouquet of flowers for me, and there was a delicious spice/carrot cake from "Just Desserts" on our traditional crystal platter, used since the very first year of our marriage. Riku was thrilled to bits with the celebration, and grinned his way through the candle blowing and the unwrapping of the gifts.

Doug gave me a gift that made Riku happy too, three different kinds of hot chocolate mix, including one with marshmallows. I was delighted with the nice assortment of my semi-annual collection of books, tea from Nova Scotia, as well as a soft scarf in the blue Nova Scotia plaid. I also got a new and quite adorable toothbrush holder for travelling; it has a little face on it, and sticks to the mirror so the toothbrush can dry out, and yet is protected too.

Bryn made a fresh pot of tea, we all enjoyed the cake, and then it was tuck-n time for Riku, sound asleep at 9 P.M., later than I had hoped this first night, but asleep in minutes, thank goodness. This jet lag business can be tough!

I had missed three birthday phone calls while I was out, but all three left recordings, which is always fun. I taped them and will get to hear them many times again this way! All were singing messages too, from Kelly, Gregg, & Caillie, Gail & Tom, and Candy, Tara, Rick perhaps but included Kaitlin & Lauren too, so I wasn't sure. I did talk with Sheldon and Donna in the afternoon, and Marie called as well.

There were also lots of birthday wishes on Facebook, many email messages and cards, and a lovely group of cards on the mantel as well. My favourites are always the homemade cards, and this year there was one from Amelia, a sweet bunnies drawing, signed by her family, of course, and the card that Riku made this morning.

I always think about the call my mom made to me each year on the 29th. Depending on when she called, she would fill me in on what was going on so many years ago at that time ... for example, "Thirty three years ago, I was just an hour away from finding out that your brothers had a little sister." And she always ended the story with my birth "at eleven A.M.,on a beautiful Sunday morning when the church bells were ringing", and the nurses put me into a pale green nightie as they thought it suited my red hair. Touching memories from all those yesterdays... and today.

It was a lovely birthday!


29 July 2009

A New Playground and a Lemon Too!

Riku and I made up a list of things he wanted to do on this trip, something Bryn, Caillie, and I always did, and then Myk' and Bryant too. On Riku's list is to visit old and new playgrounds and today he got to cross off two of the as-yet-unknown playgrounds we plan to visit. This was a brand new one for us; a large area between our local Safeway and Akane's old art studio. One of the neighbourhood groups petitioned to have the badly neglected and very old playground replaced, and they managed to get a wonderful one built. Doug had a luncheon engagement not far away, so he dropped us off and we spent an hour and a half there.

I had bought a set of badminton rackets and after Riku discovered them, he was very keen to try them out. So we took those along, and this turned out to be a perfect place to practice. As well as a wonderfully set up playground with areas for both young and older children, there is a large open green space not far from a fenced-off soccer field. We had this whole spot to ourselves and it was a good thing, as those birdies went flying here and there and everywhere. Riku was able to hit them both behind and in front of him, while I tended to whack them far off to one side. We sure had fun, though!

We did go off so Riku could explore the play area too. They have a great set of balancing platters set up, so Riku tried those out, and watched as some older children crawled over and through linked loops as well. He agreed with me that Mirei will love the train on her next visit; it's a good sized one, and the bell rings too.

As soon as Riku had cooled down he wanted to get back to the badminton play, which is where Doug found us when he returned to pick us up.

We spent the rest of the day at home, with Riku working in the garden looking for bugs again, and watering the plants. Later on we snuggled on the sofa and read one of our very few books in the house suitable for Riku. I sent almost all of his books to Japan with him, not expecting him back so soon, so a visit to the library is planned.

Bryn and Riku went out for a walk as I was preparing dinner, but it was a very short one! They discovered a ripe Meyer lemon on the tree across the street, came back for a footstool, and went back to pick it. We shall make a lemon pudding cake with it sometime this week. Riku was just as pleased as punch, as Brynnie was when he picked lemons from the same tree on all of his spring visits with us.

Bryn went off for dinner at Susan's and Kevin's and to visit the children there, and Doug was at his T'ai Chi class, so Riku and I had a quiet dinner and evening together. He had a long relaxing tub, we read a story snuggled up in his bed, and he was sound asleep at 8 P.M.



30 July 2009

Riku's Long Visit with Kai

Riku was anxious to have a playdate with Kai today, a classmate from his Japanese nursery school program. I called while Riku was having breakfast and Kai's dad said it worked out fine for them, as Kai starts in at a summer camp program next week. I am so pleased I called, as this might be the one last week day these two have together.

Riku and I walked over to Kai's house at 10:30 (and how sweet it was to see them walking hand in hand for part of the trip home) and the two boys were together until we met up with Kai's dad and little sister at 3:30 at yet another playground.

What fun they had in between those hours; they got along so very well, happily going from one project to another here at the house. They started off by building a playground and log cabin with the log set, and Riku called on Doug at one point to help out with the roof. They spent a long long time in the garden, mostly digging in the dirt trying to find bugs. How I wish I could just rent lots of interesting bugs for the next month, as the occasional spider and a few pill bugs aren't very exciting. They certainly persevered though.

They had a good lunch together, sitting chatting at the table over their grilled cheese sandwiches, as requested, and a plate with cherries, carrot sticks, and a mandarin orange each. Later they snacked on cheese, cookies, and nuts, and lots of apple juice and water after their time in the garden.

We walked over to what Riku calls "Kai's playground", as it is just a block away from Kai's house. The playground on 23rd is "Riku's playground", and the one at the top of the hill here, which we haven't yet been to on this visit, is known as "Nana's playground". So far, the one we went to yesterday is being referred to as "the new playground", although that might change as we discover new ones.

The boys had a great time together while we waited for Kai's dad to arrive. They each had a Frisbee with them, and as it was quite windy they were running after them following each and every throw ... from the tops of the slides, across the grassy area, and onto the play area when there weren't too many children around.

Riku was delighted to see Kei's adorable one-and-a-half-year-old sister, Evi. She reminds me a lot of Mirei, as she is determined and fearless and independent. Riku followed her everywhere, pushed her on the swing, made sure she was safe on the round-about, took her hand when she let him, and gave her a hug and kiss goodbye when we left them at 3:45 P.M.

Bryn went off to a movie this afternoon, and he and Riku left for a visit and dinner at Susan's and Kevin's, joining another friend of Amelia's, as well as a friend of Susan's, her two children, and a friend of the friend too. It was a busy fun night for them all; they arrived home at 8:30.

Doug and I had watched a movie so that we can order "Spiderman" for Riku from Netflix. He is quite keen to watch it, so we had to send one off for this to happen. It forced me to just put my feet up and relax and not sit at the computer trying to catch up, a hopeless task at this stage.

Riku wanted dessert when he got home, and by the time he had that, and talked about his visit, and asked about plans for tomorrow, and the usual bedtime routine, it was 9:30 when I closed his door. He was asleep again tonight in about three minutes! I think his dad was, too, as he had been working in the bedroom, and I didn't hear a peep out of him. I turned off his light and shut his door at 11 o'clock when I headed up to bed myself.
