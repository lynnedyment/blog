Akane, Bryn, and Mirei Arrive Home

Riku was a very excited boy this morning. The first words out of his mouth were "Nana, I love you. Today Mommy, and Daddy, and Mirei are coming home!" We read four short Shel Silverstein poems and then he was up and ready to draw a picture at the table. I put his fruit plate in front of him, and went up to shower, and Gail sat with him while he ate his breakfast and I made his lunch.

It's the first of December, and I gave Riku his Advent calendar. He was very happy to see a small piece of chocolate behind the first door, shaped like a Christmas tree. We walked up to school at the usual time, and took a few minutes to realize that today is the day the children gather in the schoolyard for Monday morning assembly. I waved goodbye after walking him to his room, and came home to have an egg and sourdough toast breakfast with Gail and Tom, get the laundry going, Riku's bed stripped, etc. etc.

Gail and I also played a few games of morning cards, something we have not done since she arrived and then Gail vacuumed this entire floor thoroughly, making it look so much better! There are bags in the hallway downstairs waiting to be distributed to various places; how I love seeing things moving out of this house.

Glenn called on Skype as well, and sounds just awful, as he has a terrible cough and most likely has picked up a bug that is going 'round the Falls.

The doorbell rang about noon hour, and there was Bryn with a little girl, peering in my front window. I got a huge hug and big smiles from her, and then she was dashing up the stairs to see Auntie Gail and Uncle Tom. Grandpa had picked her up at the airport and got his big hugs there. Mirei immediately got busy with the little kitchen, making us "tea" and "breakfast", and then settled in at the table to play with her pretend birthday cake, serving us large slices while we all sang the birthday song a half dozen times. She ate a nice big real lunch and then she too took much delight in finding a chocolate behind the door in her Advent calendar.

Gail, Mirei, and I walked up to the school to meet Riku, and what big smiles and big hugs she got from her brother. His little face just lit up when he saw her and he held hands with her all the way home, telling her about the movie he saw, and pointing out BM on the sidewalk, and chatting up a storm, stopping now and then to cup her face in his hands, or just look at her. It was just adorable.

Back here the two of them cuddled up in the bedding on the floor of the small bedroom, looked through books together, smiled and read to each other, played out in the garden, and went upstairs with some of the stuffed animals. They both had snacks, which included a tiny chocolate bar for Riku from his Hallowe'en stash. As soon as he finished it though, he started grabbing at his throat and making loud clearing sounds, asking me for something to eat because his throat was bothering him. Now we really do think he has an allergy to peanuts as that was listed as an ingredient on the wrapper. I instantly gave him an antihistamine, and let Bryn know what had happened. We will get him to a skin specialist asap to see what is going on, as he has had quite a fine rash on his legs and torso over the past few days, so there may be other food allergies as well. I'm sure he will be getting tested soon, so we know what to he needs to avoid.

We all walked to Hampshire Street at 4:30, although the tablet by that time had made Riku dozy and a little edgy, so he wasn't thrilled about walking home, something he never objects to. However, Doug and Tom were at the Hiller Aeronautical Museum with the car, so we had no choice. We met Akane almost at the corner of Potrero Avenue, and all walked to the house together. She got a big hug too, and later I spoke to him on the phone and found out he had a very successful piano lesson, and was very happy with his new toy, a wheel that turns into a robot!

Tom and Doug were home shortly after we walked in the door, so we had a quick dinner and then dashed off to Costco. Back at home Gail and I had tea, a few games of cards, and then Bryn walked in with a visit and some gifts for us... tea and food goodies for Gail and me, chocolates and biscuits for Doug and some canned beer for Tom. We heard a little bit about their trip including the story of Mirei dashing out to play at recess with the children at what will be her new French immersion daycare in Kyoto.

We had planned to get some extra work done in the house, but both Gail and I were too tired to do much. I started collecting up all the chickens and knickknacks from the living room and the front bedroom, in anticipation of Bryn and family moving in, and Christmas decorating, coming soon! We had a last game of cards and went off to bed.

Such a Productive Day Around 1361 Rhode Island Street!

Well, days without children are much quieter days than is ideal, but the plus side is that with my sister here, we accomplished so much, and had fun doing so. Being raised in a big family, where everyone worked together, or at least were all working at the same time on household chores, spoiled me forever for having to do boring household chores on my own. I know what needs to be done, I make lists, I go to bed knowing what I am going to do first, and then each night wonder how the day passed without a thing on that list being done!

With Gail here to help and spur me on, we managed together to do a thorough cleaning and clearing out of the downstairs bathroom. The walls got washed, the medicine cabinet emptied for Akane's and Bryn's toiletries, and the knickknacks put away, making room for Christmas decorations. I packed up a big box of stuff collected last night from the shelves in the dining room, and perhaps by the time I get them back up in the spring, after Bryn and family have moved to Japan, and after Kelly and Gregg's wedding, I may be less enamoured of all these mementos and be able to part with some of them.

We took all the birds, wooden pieces, shells, rocks, etc. off the kitchen bookshelves, and pulled all the books to the very front edge of the shelves, leaving room for no dust collecting! The white board above the children's desk in the kitchen is now white, and no longer covered with the alphabet and the days of the week. The two doors off the kitchen are ready for Christmas-related pictures, replacing the paintings done over the past few months by the children.

Another "big" project, which isn't nearly as big with two hard-working women dealing with it, was a complete clean-out of the laundry room, aka the children's overflow toy area. I went through a big basket of toys and found plenty to give away to the Prenatal Homeless Shelter. Some items were listed on Freecycle as well. Doug and Tom took three bags from the previous days clean-out to Goodwill, and I have another stack to take out when we are out in that area next. Yahoo!

I'm working on the closets in the two downstairs bedrooms too, and managed to clear some of my clothes out of one, and find room for them upstairs where they really belong anyway. Now if I could just convince Doug to clear out some suits and shirts, we would be in great shape!

Susan stopped by to return fondue pots, and went away with a nice big bag of shoes for Finn, outgrown by Riku, but still in fine shape. And since Susan loves overalls on Finn, she took some of those too, leaving plenty of other outfits for one of my doula moms with three boys. It will be such fun to see Finn in these clothes of Riku's; I have such a hard time parting with clothes I can remember the children wearing.

At 2:30, Gail and I took a break and walked to the school to pick Riku up. Today was a gorgeous day, sunny and warm, and we didn't really need the jackets we wore at all. How I love holding hands with that little boy, as he skips along and fills me in on his day. He and Gail held hands as he walked along a very narrow ledge near the hospital, so he could be "Spiderman" and "walk" on the bit of wall around the poles. It was very cute to see!

We went to the garden area of the hospital while Riku had his snack, and we chatted about school; he found some ants to feed too and gave them a bit of bread. I had taken his Advent calendar with me, and he was very pleased to have that tiny bit of chocolate hidden behind door number 2. He had a quick runaround on the big sculpture before he came running for the last few minutes of the walk around the corner to home.

Mirei had gone to her Japanese nursery school program today, and I was surprised to hear that Akane got home without her falling asleep on the bus. She was sound asleep, though, when we walked in at 3:40 P.M. Jet-lagged girl; she had been awake for two hours in the night, from 1-3, and so I wasn't surprised to learn that she had finally just given in. She looked so long and lean sleeping on the big bed; I hope the waking was easy for her, and her night time tuck-in easier still.

Riku took a few minutes to show me his new toy from Japan; he turned a wheel into a robot in just minutes and it really is as cool as he told me it was on the phone last night.

I called Uncle Frank tonight, as it was Auntie Rita's birthday today, and I knew he would be having a very difficult day. He had indeed, and has had a few tough days before this too. He was alone for Thanksgiving dinner, and added to the not too long ago fifth "anniversary" of Auntie Rita's passing, it all adds up to a most distressing season for Uncle Frank. We chatted for a long time though, and shared some laughs, catching up on both sides of the continent. I am so glad I called, and wish during tough times like this for him that I lived closer.

We waited to have dinner with Doug, after his T'ai Chi lesson, and he really enjoyed the "hamburger" stew Gail and I made, as did Tom and the cooks too. Lots of onion, carrots, celery, peas, and corn in gravy, served over mashed potatoes. Yum! It reminds Gail and me of Mom and home, as it was a favourite dish of ours way back then too. Tom and I finished off a bottle of wine with a generous glass each, and after a little more kitchen work, we all went upstairs to watch the first of four videos of Gail's and Tom's six week cruise. Their ship was just beautiful, much more elegant than I imagined a cruise ship to be! It was such fun watching as this was a professionally made video which they bought, and we saw them three times during the hour plus movie!

Gail and I had one last game of cards, off to bed she went at 10:15, and now it's my turn, an hour later.

Vacaville, an Outlet Kind of Day!

Gail and Tom found some of the items they were after, and a few fun gifts for the children too, which they will give them before they leave. I wanted to get something done in the house, as there is so much to do before Akane, Bryn, and the children move in next week some time, so I took a big stack of my unread books off a two drawer stand in one of the spare bedrooms, giving them so flat space in the room, and emptied the drawers for them. Gail and I took some time to stop and read about a dozen or more very old postcards I bought at an antique shop last year in Niagara Falls, all written in the early 1900s! One day I hope to have them all in albums, in the appropriate years.

Gail and I had decided earlier not to eat dinner, then changed our minds and decided we would share a burrito. While standing in line to order, we also thought a shared quesadilla sounded like a nice appetizer to eat in the car. Yum!

Once at home, we sat to eat, had a pot of tea, and of course Gail and I played our usual card game, before starting back in at work. Gail and I plan to go downtown tomorrow. Tom and Doug are off on an adventure that doesn't interest us, so we're going to wander around the city, and have lunch at my very favourite restaurant, the "Zuni Cafe". There may not be time for any extras at the end of a day out. It was a big job but finally, by the end of the night, all was tidy and all went to various places... upstairs, in drawers, and out for Goodwill.

How wonderful to have help doing this! Gail has made it possible for me to be so much further ahead in not only Christmas preparations, but in preparing to have Bryn and his family move in with us for two plus months. I hate to think of her leaving in just three days, but I am grateful to have had her with me for two full weeks!

It was certainly a very long busy day for our houseguests, as they stepped off the plane at 11 a.m., after an eleven hour flight, and with a seventeen hour time difference.

It is tempting to go to bed myself although it's early for me, and I have emails to write, and photos to annotate, and things to do around here before turning in... and I do have the house to myself which is rare these days. Now there are ten of us in the house, so the next while will be very busy indeed.

28 December 2008

Don't Stop the Fun!

This has been the busiest day! Akane's sister and brother-in-law, Masu, were planning to spend the next two nights in a hotel downtown, and Riku was mighty upset when he learned about this. He was so disappointed and wanted to know, from me, why they couldn't just stay here, so he could see them and play with them. Of course I explained that they probably just wanted some privacy and some time downtown without taking buses every day, but it's all beyond his understanding.

I told Akane how upset he was, and she suggested they spend some time with the children this morning before leaving. They did this, but was my house bedlam! Riku was hot and sweating with the exertion of chasing Aya-chan around and around the house, and with Mirei right in there trying to get involved too, and the squeals from all three of them!... man, oh man!

They did have a little quiet time at the table, as both Ayako and Masu have little hand held gadgets one can play games on, and lots of other stuff too. There is even a little special "pen" to use with them, and that held their attention until the chasing started. Masu kept sneaking away into the bedroom to sleep some more, but that didn't last long! No doubt that is part of the reason they are heading downtown for two days. These two are not used to having their sleep broken so early or so often.

Riku had his piano lesson today at noon, so that meant they had to slow down a bit. All that energy could have lit up the town for a day! Akake made a big lunch for them of steamed broccoli, rice, and egg, and after they left for downtown. Not much later Akane left to join them, and I took the children off to a playground almost two hours. I so much more enjoy seeing all that steam let off outside!

I got to talk to a lovely grandmother from Brooklyn, who was at the playground with her grown daughter and son, and a five and three quarters year old grandson, happily playing with the visiting uncle. Riku got to join in their ball throwing fun, and after some chatting, we found out that both our grandchildren attend the Mandarin Chinese Immersion program at Starr King School... her little guy, Cosmo, is in the kindergarten class. They played so well together, tossing a ball back and forth to each other from various high points on the slides and the play structure.

Mirei and Riku had some time together too though; they sat in the sand and dug, and Riku pushed Mirei on the swing, something they both enjoyed so much. It's obvious that Riku likes being a big brother and it's very sweet to see. Mirei would tell him when to push her higher and when to slow down and he listened to her.

Mirei watched in admiration as Riku drew his name in Chinese on the sidewalk with chalk. I'm sure she will be trying to print her name very soon too!

It was a gorgeous day and the children shed their jackets. Nice to be wearing just shirts in the sunshine at the end of the year, and getting outside was so good for all of us. It's what we all needed after such a busy time lately.

I popped both children in the tub when we got home, made a big pot of pasta for dinner, and they were both sound asleep at 7:15 tonight. They so needed an early night, as while we have company they are all sleeping in one room .. in twin beds pushed together. The minute Mirei or Riku stirs in the morning, sometimes at 5:30(!) that is it for both of them.

Riku will have two good nights sleep in his regular small dark room, before his aunt and uncle are back. The children are so enjoying having company in the house and just don't want the fun to stop!

I did connect with a fellow doula today about a pregnant mom due soon, wanting to have a doula with her for her birth. I hope it's in the cards for me to be available for her, but we shall see.

Bed earlier than usual tonight, just in case the children do wake up early again.


29 December 2008

"Tale of Despereaux", Playground and Trail Time, Tub and Noodles!

I had told Riku last night that he could come upstairs quietly when he woke, and waken me, as we haven't had morning time together for a few days... or at least not snuggle time. I had just opened my eyes at 7:15 and in he walked, all smiles and ready to get in and put his cold feet on my warm legs! After they were warmed up, I came downstairs to get a fruit bowl ready for him and showered after putting Caillou on the TV. When I came out Mirei was sitting there with him too, still in her pink sleepers, with pink cheeks and a happy smile. Lucky me!

It had just finished... "just at the right time!" said Riku, and down we all came for breakfast. Akane and I sat with them while they ate, and of course there was drawing time for Riku, and Play-Doh time for Mirei too. We were going off to the movies at 11 A.M. so Riku got his piano lesson over with first, which works much better for him than at the end of a busy day. He wasn't happy today about getting at it, but Akane says he does so well and always ends up enjoying it.

I packed up snacks for the movie and at 11:15, Doug, Riku, Mirei and I were sitting in the theatre in Daly City, nibbling on popcorn (not Riku who ate grapes and gingerbread instead), We all thoroughly enjoyed the movie, with lots of laughs from Riku, and Mirei whispering quietly when she had a question. This was her first movie experience and she was just great; I think about 15% of the time she was very interested in looking at the people beside and behind us, and staring around the theatre, especially at the projector room. Mirei ate one kernel of popcorn at a time, every so daintily, and then had gingerbread cookies for "dessert". They were both given free t-shirts with a picture of the darling big eared mouse on the front too; I think they will fit when they are in their teens!

I thought some running around time after all that sitting was a good idea, and so I had Doug drop us off at a local Potrero Hill playground. He ran an errand and joined us perhaps an hour later, leaving us lots of time for tag (whenever I was close to catching Riku, he found a spot quickly and claimed it as "base!"). All three of us were running, taking turns being it, and having lots of fun. Riku pushed Mirei on the swing, he played on a hopscotch, and we stopped for a picnic snack in a sunny spot for oranges, grapes, and some water. When Doug joined us, we went for a little walk along a dirt trail, and how I loved seeing those two little ones holding hands as they walked along, or following along behind Grandpa carrying their sticks.

Surprisingly to me, once back home, instead of instantly wanting to have hot chocolate... it was cool today!, both children headed to the back yard. Mirei came in asking for the "poppers" that bang, and that kept them busy for a bit, distracting Riku from sweeping the yard, which needed the attention!

A half hour later they came in for hot chocolate with special vanilla marshmallows, and gingerbread cookies, followed by a tub coloured pink today, with lots of play-acting going on between these two. For the entire time, Mirei was the "mommy" and Riku the "baby", and he went along with it all. I could hear him "crying" and her consoling him, and when I walked in I found Mirei with a face cloth on his head and washing his face with another! It was hilarious to listen to, and to photograph! They had such fun. One of the things I think is funny and endearing is that whenever one of them gets water in their eyes, they ask for a "safe cloth". Riku apparently misheard "facecloth" many years ago, and Mirei has picked up on it too. I have told them both it is called a facecloth, but I must say for their use and "safecloth" is more apt a description! Following that, Riku happily sat and did two and a half pages of homework, which he whizzed through while Mirei sat beside him drawing. She is now making easily recognizable As, Ms, and Ns, and proudly announces that she has done her homework.

Bryn heard her saying she was doing a project two days ago, except she was saying "projec'", so he carefully repeated the word to her adding a very distinct "t" sound to the end of it. So for the moment "project" has become "projec...T"! It's pretty cute to hear this coming out of this just turned three year old!

Another recent addition to Mirei's vocabulary comes from the Dyment side of the family for sure... "actually". It turns out some of us say it a lot, and Mirei is now using it frequently. I will ask her if she would like juice or milk, she tells me her choice, and then instantly says "Actually, I will have... instead." I remember so well the first time I heard Caillie say this word and can repeat it to this day. She was caught by a five or six year old putting pink icing on her hands, and was told it was for the cookies. Caillie, at about three and five months said "Actually Emma, this is nail polish." and I just laughed so hard and will never forget it. I even have photos of the event. So, it's such fun to have Mirei saying this word so early too.

Akane came home from the apartment and made big bowls of udon noodles with veggies for their dinner, and they both ate it all, much to my surprise! It always looks like such a big portion for these children, but they love it. However, Mirei tonight decided she didn't like cabbage after all, but had no problem at all with the big mushrooms in her bowl.

Tuck in time was early again tonight, with both children asleep by 7:45. I worked on the Christmas Day album and can't wait to get caught up with the other two on the agenda!


30 December 2008

BADM, Ocean Beach, and New Year's Resolutions!

I've been pondering over New Year's resolutions throughout the day, and there is already quite a list of them. I spoke with Uncle Frank tonight, just past midnight, and he said he hoped he was right at the top of the list and at the bottom too! Of course he is in there for sure; I will drop him at least a line or two every day, as I know how much it means to him... and to me too as he knows he is on my mind.

Another resolution is to keep my blogs much shorter and more succinct; I will try to have a favourite line, or story, or perhaps a memory in each one, as well as the gist of the day's happenings, so here goes for today, just as a trial shot, to see if I can do it.

Bay Area Discovery Museum from 11:30-2 P.M., closing time today. On the way there, Riku came up with a new game after we had played "I Spy" for a while. He had a whole list of questions that all of us got to answer, including Mirei... "what is your favourite colour?", followed bye food, dessert, animal, bird, places to visit, and when he asked us who his favourite person was, he wanted to answer first and touched my heart when he said "Nana". Doug made him laugh when he said he had stolen his favourite person! This passed the time so quickly on the drive over the Golden Gate, and it was sweet to listen to Mirei's careful answers too.

Doug and I had such fun with the children at the museum. Doug took Riku off on his own while Mirei and I went into the toddler room; Riku outgrew it a few months ago (he's too tall now), so instead he spent a long time in the indoor fishing boat which he loves. We joined up in time for our outdoor picnic; it was cool sitting in the shade, but much warmer after running around! I sipped a big cup of tea today while Doug did the chasing in and out of the vine maze. Fun for all!

Since it was early, we stopped in at Ocean Beach for an hour. It was a little foggy, but perfect for walking and running, which we did today. These two children love the beach as much as I do, and I am going to see they get as many days there as we can fit into the next two months.

We stopped at Rainbow Groceries for eggs, and the children and I sat in the car while Doug went into the very busy store. I took a fun video of Riku entertaining Mirei.. hilarious!

Home for hot chocolate, with those delicious marshmallows again, some cinnamon toast and pears, and of course the tub, coloured blue today, and with lots of laughs and pretending going on again tonight.

Akane had been to the apartment for most of the afternoon, and we sat down together and fed them dinner... pasta and lots of veggies. They were almost finished when Ayako and Masu walked in, and after finishing up their meals, they were off to play and have fun with them. Riku and Ayako played a board game, with Mirei joining in, and doing very well at finding the right clues!

Riku wanted Ayako to floss his teeth tonight (usually I do it, and so gave her a one tooth demonstration!) and then he sat on his bed with his aunt and uncle and read two stories, with the door shut for complete privacy. He will miss them when they go, as will Mirei.

Riku had a little trouble falling into a deep sleep tonight, and just when I thought he had, he rolled over, whispered "Nana?", and when I answered asked me "Is the brain a muscle?" One wonders what else these little ones think about!

Bryn, Akane, Ayako, and Masu left about 8:30 for dinner out followed by some evening New Year's celebrations in the city, with Mari as well. I was still in with Riku when they left, but was out his door soon after.

Mirei woke up at 10, calling and crying for her mom, and it took me about ten minutes of more to settle her down.

Kelly called, I called Bryn and Caillie at their Dad's, and then called Uncle Frank to wish him a Happy New Year's too!
