Protection Island and Dinghy Dock

Tom and Doug went off at 10:30 this morning for the opening of the new Nanaimo skating rink, leaving Gail and I to our usual devices: cards, breakfast (egg, delicious toast, and tea), followed by cards, and then some time on the computer on our family research. It's hard to pull ourselves away once we get started but today we were off for a walk around Protection Island. We parked the car downtown and were at the dock in lots of time for the ten-minute ferry trip at 1:10.

We ambled along the pathway, admiring the homes and fenced-in gardens, and Gail pointed out three huge heron nests to me, which they had seen on their last trip here a couple of weeks ago. One was sitting on another tree branch, so that was a treat; we took a few photos and meandered back for lunch at the Dingy Dock, a restaurant right on the wharf that we always enjoy. I guess we were all hungry, and waiting an hour for our lunch was not as much fun as it could have been if we weren't eating so late... 2:30 seating and no food for another hour. However, the view was lovely, and we survived, and Gail and I thoroughly enjoyed the free tea we got served after the waitress said she would have the ferry do a trip on the half hour so we didn't need to dash right out after eating.

Home, computer for Gail and me... Doug and Tom out on the deck reading, as usual, and a break for dinner of homemade lasagna, a big plate of raw veggies, and fruit for dessert. Back to the computer, filling in a few more spots on Gail's Ancestry.com site.

Bed after a couple of card games... reading... night night.
