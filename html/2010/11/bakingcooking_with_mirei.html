Cooking with Mirei

Mirei and I have always enjoyed spending time in the kitchen together, so it is lovely to be back with her sharing these moments. Last Saturday, our first weekend day together, Mirei chose to have scrambled eggs, even though she prefers them fried, just so she can cook them herself. Frying eggs is mostly standing back and checking to see when to take the lid off. Not fun.

She hasn't forgotten how to crack and mix an egg, so after carefully pouring the mixture into the frying pan, Mirei stood carefully pushing the egg out from around the edges of the pan to the middle with a spatula, slowly and carefully. They were perfect, and she ate them all up, steaming hot in a bowl, which is how she prefers them served. The toast and "bacon" go on the side. Yesterday was another scrambled egg day, as we only have time for this on the weekends.

During the week I had made French toast, poached eggs, porridge with dried mango and sunflower seeds, and a fried egg, just for variety, saving the scrambled for Mirei to cook up herself.

Today we made pancakes together. When I told Riku he could mix them next Sunday, he responded that he would rather draw than cook! That will make his sister very happy.

Before we start cooking, each and every time, Mirei washes her hands, puts on her apron by herself, and clambers up on the chair at the counter, all set to measure and level and pour and beat. I took photos. :)

She joined Riku at the table for some drawing time while I cooked up the pancakes, serving them up with warm maple syrup, brought from home, and some sausages that Akane cooked up as a side dish.

We do love our weekend mornings!
