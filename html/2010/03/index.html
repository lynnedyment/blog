Monday ... Some Familiar Faces

Riku's internal clock goes off about 6:30 most mornings; he's always the first one awake, and the first thing he does is turn on the switch in the living room for the heat. Fifteen or twenty minutes later, when Bryn comes down the living/dining room and kitchen have started to warm up. Riku now sits and watches part of a movie on his mom's computer until Bryn has his fruit bowl ready. Nice little pattern for him.

Walking Riku to school has always been a favourite part of my day, whether it was in San Francisco, or here in Kyoto. I was hoping when we left the house today that we would see some of the people we used to meet on this walk, and we lucked out immediately. The very friendly woman, who lives just around the first corner on the way to school, used to be out sweeping her little courtyard each morning, but today she was up on a tiny, second level balcony hanging her laundry. She must have recognized our voices, as she stepped up to the edge of the balcony to wish us a good morning. I was delighted to see her again.

Riku told me that he now says hello to two other people on his walk, and insisted we walk a different way than we used to so he could see them. Right on cue, another friendly woman, wearing one of those old-fashioned "prairie bonnets", stepped out from her house to say good morning as well. Riku told her I was his daddy's mother, and we bowed and smiled to each other. Apparently her husband is often with her, so Riku was a bit disappointed that I wouldn't meet him today too. Lots of days left for this though...

We held hands all the way to school, as we always did, and said good morning to the principal, another person I was looking forward to seeing again. I used to walk Riku into the school building itself, but today as we rounded the corner to go up a short ramp to the schoolground, Riku told me I could leave him there, and I learned later this is where Bryn leaves him now. Riku took a quick look around before he kissed me goodbye, something he wasn't shy about last spring.

On the way back to the house I passed a mom and her daughter who live just a block from here; we passed each other each school day when I was here in the spring and she greeted me with a very big smile and cheery "good morning" too. It's lovely being back!

Akane's etching press and a few other items that have been stored at a studio since last year were being delivered this morning, and Akane and Bryn were busy with that for a few hours. The press is extremely heavy and had to go up a flight of stairs; I sat answering emails while all of that was going on as it sounded dangerous and tricky, and I didn't want to witness any of it. It was cold with the door open and I was chilled to the bone. I can't seem to get warm here and hope that there is an early spring!

Akane and Bryn went out to run some errands in the early afternoon, so I had some time to write emails and do a little ancestry work. The time flew by though and I went off to meet Riku at 3:30 in a light drizzle. I took his brolly with me, and was greeted with his very happy smile when we met up before he reached the bridge. Back at home, I made him up one of the snacks I used to make for him in SF... an egg and "bacon" sandwich on toast and a cup of hot chocolate, with apples on the side.

Riku had a little homework to do and did it in a jiffy... writing down what time it was for each of the dozen pictures of clocks featured, and then we went upstairs while he did some piano practicing. I can't help him with it, but he's very happy to sit going through his books and playing the pieces he especially enjoys and some that he thinks I will especially like. I did some taping again, as I want to be able to see these once I am back at home. It's obvious that Riku so enjoys playing.

Mirei came home with Bryn and Akane just after 4:30 and spent the next half hour spinning and dancing and cavorting about with just her sleeveless undershirt and her long socks! I was cold just looking at her, but her skin was as warm as could be. I took some video and pics of her, and that big happy smile of hers. What a delight this little girl is.

At one point, Akane showed her a cream puff she had bought for her for dessert, and Mirei immediately offered to share it with me. Bryn said he wanted half too, but Mirei pointed out to him that there wouldn't be enough, just for her and Nana. But, there was also a delicious pastry stick on hand too, and she offered to break her half in half so that Bryn could have a treat too. She's quick with her answers and Bryn and I often share a look at something she has come up with so fast and with such clear reasoning.

She and I put the last of the stickers in the "Angelina, Ballerina" book that we started on Saturday, and then Mirei read to me from "Ten Apples up on Top" once more. We read two more pages, with new words for her, and will practice again tomorrow.

Bryn took Riku off on the bike for his 5 P.M. karate lesson, and then picked him up again at 6:30. I had promised Riku I would wait and have dinner with him; Akane and Mirei eat earlier so that Mirei can get her tub time in and in bed at her usual time. It was the first time I saw Riku in his karate uniform and wearing his orange belt; I must say he looked very handsome. We sat at the table eating our late dinner, and looking at a book while we did so, not something that happens when we all eat together.

Riku was anxious to read as much of "Captain Underpants" as he could, and Mirei decided she wanted her mom to tuck her in tonight. Riku was very happy about that and at 8 P.M. we were under the covers and read two chapters of his book.

That was it for me for the night too, much to my surprise! I dozed off, happily as warm as could be.

1 March 2010

Feeling Like a New Woman ... and Mirei Loves Me!

I awoke at 4 A.M. today, too warm, as I fell asleep wearing my clothes ... three layers on top, my slacks, socks, and Bryn's scarf. This was one of the very few times since I was a teenager that I have gone to sleep without doing my evening routine of washing my face, and flossing and brushing my teeth. Ugh! However, ignoring that at this point, I stripped off all but one layer, and went back to sleep until Riku woke at 6:20.

After my shower I felt, and looked, so much better; all those hours of sleep, ten of them, had put some colour in my cheeks and I wasn't cold still. Yahoo! Four days of that jet lag was enough, and the secret was finally getting more than four hours of sleep in a row.

Riku and I talked on the way to school about how many people we greet regularly on the walk to school. There is the our gal around the corner, and she was out again today, and we passed a friendly stranger whom we thought we might see again. Riku was disappointed that neither "his" man and woman were outside today, but we both agreed it would be fun if we could add some new people to our list.

Something triggered our memories about the song "Going on a Bear Hunt", which Kelly sang to Bryn and Caillie many years ago, and which I have sung to many children since. We made up some of our own scenarios ... a big snowstorm, a high wind, and sang all the way across the bridge and along the fields, now starting to be cleaned up and ready for planting.

Akane and Bryn had barium swallow tests today, part of a city-run program, so I had the house to myself all morning and for part of the afternoon. I made the most of it by finishing up my blog entries, answering a large number of emails, writing to Uncle Frank, and reading while eating my breakfast and lunch. I did up the dishes, got closer to finishing my book, and before I knew it Akane and Bryn were home.

Riku got out of school early today, at 1:45 instead of 2:40, so I set off to meet him, expecting to see him close to the bridge. No Riku in sight, so I walked on, walking almost all the way around the school to the courtyard, where I spotted him on his own, with his head down, totally immersed in his own thoughts. I decided to just continue walking on past him, as he hadn't spotted me approaching him; I was two steps past when it hit him that it was me. How he laughed, and ended up retelling the story to me three more times.

As I was approaching the school, I had spotted three young girls and a boy all leaning over one of the shallow irrigation ditches, checking out something they had spotted. I thought it would make a great photo but they sensed that I was there, and all turned at the same time leaving me with a different photo than I intended.

Just a block from home, Riku and I were walking by a cabbage patch. Riku was so pleased as he had told me yesterday about discovering "some perfect cabbages, with no holes in them, and I wanted to show them to you when I saw them." I already had my camera in hand to take a shot of them. Riku knows me very well!

The rest of the afternoon and evening sped by in a flutter of activities. Riku did his piano practice with Akane, then had his snack and a bit of time to himself before his second Chinese tutor — Ma Lau-Shi (teacher Ma) — arrived at 3:30 for a two-hour session. I sat on the sofa, working on the computer and reading while the two interacted in Chinese. It surprised me to see that this fellow too has Riku sitting on his lap at the table while they did paper work ( writing and drawing), but it seems something that comes naturally to these particular fellows anyway. It's sweet to see. Riku had a few five-minute breaks and then they went back to the table; this teacher spends less time playing and chatting and more time actually teaching, using paper and pencil, and also watching Riku do some of his homework.

Mirei got home in time from her visit to the doctor (she has a cold and a cough, as of last Saturday) to spend a few minutes watching Riku play a game on Bryn's IPhone while watching with Ma Lau-Shi. She was happy and sunny, as usual. I asked her what the doctor said and her answer was "Nothing". She followed that by saying "to me. Just to Mommy and I don't know what he said."

I made dinner for the children tonight, since Akane was going to be home late with Mirei. I kept it simple and used one of the two packages of "mac and cheese" I brought with me from home. The children like it ... it's a whole-wheat version of a Kraft dinner with organic shells and white cheddar, and not something they have very often. I cooked up kabocha squash with sweet carrots, mashed them together with a little butter, and steamed broccoli. Both children enjoyed it, and I had the veggies with a bowl of brown rice Akane had cooked up for me this morning, along with some slivered ginger-tofu I had brought with me as well.

At dinner Mirei did a very interesting thing, giving all of us titles, our relationships to one another and designating us as female or male. Here is a summary of it all; it was quite astonishing. "There are five of us. Two are boys and three are girls. One is a mommy, one is a grandma, one is a brother, and one is a daddy. One is me. I am a sister. One is a Goda. Also Riku and Mirei are Goda but also Dyments. Daddy is a Dyment. Nana is a Dyment."

Mirei keeps herself busy, so often with little tiny notebooks, and pencils, again reminding me so much of Caillie. Today she wrote "I love you Nana" on one side of the paper, in pink ink, and asked me how to spell "from". A minute later she handed me the paper and on the back she had written "from Mirei" with kisses and hugs. It's a keeper, this wee bit of paper.

I bathed both the children while Akane made a potato cabbage dish for hers and Bryn's dinner, Riku had some music practice to do before bed, as well as reading a chapter in his English reading book, an interesting story about baboons that I had given him for Valentine's Day. There are lots of complicated words and I am amazed at how well Riku sounds them out.

We were busy right until the last minute before bed. Akane took Mirei upstairs, I snuggled in with Riku finishing off the "Captain Underpants" book which had him laughing out loud over dinner. He wants the whole series now, all ten of them still to go, I believe. They will be fun for him to have in SF this summer.

I didn't fall asleep tonight and wasn't even tempted to, but did have some time on Akane's computer, as she fell asleep with Mirei. I finished off my excellent book and went off to bed with "Travels With Charley", a John Steinbeck book I haven't read. I only brought three books with me, and am thankful that Bryn has some English books here I can read to fill in the gaps.

Bed at 10:30 tonight.

Slow-Catching-Up Kind of Day ... Lovely!

Gorgeous sunny spring-like day today!

For the first time ever, Riku and I did not say "good morning" to one person on our walk to school. Such a disappointment after our resolve yesterday to try to find more "regulars".

Riku made an interesting observation this morning on our walk: he spotted a bright red car parked on the side of the road, and stopped to admire it, saying that "I never see red cars in Japan! Mostly they are white, or black, or grey." Sure enough, we looked around at three different parking areas and every single car was one of those three colours, except one beige one. A few blocks later we saw a blue car, and on my way home I saw a metallic blue, and a green car, but for the most part Riku was right. I'm sure this is not true in other areas of Japan, so perhaps this particular part of Kyoto is just a little more conservative.

Today we talked about kissing goodbye, and Riku suggested we run a little bit so we could do so as soon as we turned the corner heading up the ramp to the playground. It worked. This new routine makes me smile at how much has changed in the last year. Thank goodness holding hands is not considered embarrassing yet!

Mirei was cold when she got home from school, which is most unusual for her, and made us laugh by pulling Riku's one thick coat over her body ... she had stripped off her school uniform and was down to socks and underwear, and then pulled the sleeves of his other thick jacket up over the legs and feet.

I walked to the school, meeting Riku as he came off the bridge. He enjoyed his snack of cheese and "bacon" grilled on a piece of toast, as well as a fruit plate. This boy will always choose fruit over a regular dessert. He did his little bit of homework, still working on clocks and time, packed up the books he needed in his backpack for tomorrow, and settled in to watch a movie, a treat he was looking forward to on this day with no extra lessons. Bryn set aside his computer and sat on the floor with Riku watching the sci-fi movie, with lots of discussions as to what was happening.

Riku read me a chapter from his book, and for the first time read it by whole phrases, not just one word after another. He was very pleased with himself and how that sounded; I think it makes it easier for him to know what the story is too.

Akane made up a curry dish for dinner, and left for her class just before 6. While the children finished their dinners, Bryn played some music for them, starting with Abba, so that I could see both of them throw their arms out and sing "Knowing me, knowing you! ..." Hilarious. They both have lovely voices and enjoy singing so much.

Mirei decided she wanted to sleep with me tonight, and so Bryn tucked her into my bed at 7:30 and ended up staying there until I went to bed just after 11 o'clock. I tucked Riku in upstairs, promising to stay with him until he was asleep which took about seven minutes! I was downstairs at 7:50, and had the evening on my own.

p.s. I took a photo of the lovely silhouette of leaves on the window screen in my room.


3 March 2010

A Walk Along the River and a Bike Ride Too!

The sun was shining beautifully this morning as Riku and I walked to school. The air was warm and, after saying goodbye to Riku, it seemed the perfect day to go for a walk along the river.

It's a lovely pathway, which goes on for miles; there are no bikes allowed, and there weren't many people out walking this early. I thoroughly enjoyed the quiet hour, and the long slow walk, not feeling the need to rush home and get warm! I stopped to take photos of the water, duck families, and a few of the egrets I spotted nesting or fishing in the low grassy areas just below me.

Akane took the train to Osaka this morning, leaving as soon as she dropped Mirei off at school. Lovely day for this trip.

About 12:30, Bryn suggested we got for lunch at a cafe up the river in the opposite direction I had taken this morning. Since it would have taken quite a long time to walk there, we set off on the bikes, me for the first time in perhaps eighteen years or so. I walked with the bike on the most narrow areas, not quite confident of my ability to not knock into someone walking or riding coming along in the opposite direction. I so enjoyed riding when the road was wide and clear!

Lunch was delicious and plentiful, a larger portion than is generally served in Japan. The dish Bryn had recommended I try is called a "Loco Moco", a dish of a fried egg, atop a hamburger patty of some kind (mine was vegetarian), and that atop a mound of rice. It is surrounded by a gravy, and on the side was mashed kobocha squash, one of my favourite vegetables here, with two crisply cooked very tasty green beans stuck in the top of the squash. Since it was more than I could eat, I had to leave some of it behind. No "doggie bags" in Japan (although Akane told me later that some places allow you to bring your own containers and take it home with you). I was sorry I wasn't able to save it for dinner that evening.

Two extra bits... this cafe had one of the Japanese toilets I like so much; the seat was warm, and there were all the fun buttons to push that I remember from Sakai.

I noticed baskets sitting beside the tables in the cafe, and it was a very clever idea... to be used as a spot for purses, bags, etc. instead of having to put them on the floor. I did take a photo of this, but didn't include it in this entry.

Riku usually has his piano lesson right after school, but today it started an hour later. So, I got in my second walk to school today, and a little free time with Riku before he and Bryn went off on the bike.

Friday night is a night to relax here; no school the next morning, no rush to get homework done, etc. so the children watched a favourite tv show, and we all settled in for the weekend.

Big day for Riku tomorrow afternoon... his piano recital! We're all very excited about it, but Riku seems to be taking it all in stride.

5 March 2010

Riku's Big Day: His First Piano Recital

Today is the day we have all been looking forward to for some time, to see Riku on stage playing the pieces he has been practicing and enjoying playing for some time.

He and Akane set off at 10:30 for a noon rehearsal, while Bryn, Mirei, and I spent a quiet morning at home. It was raining and we had to take two buses, but she's such a little trouper, easy to travel with and as patient as could be.

When we arrived, not long before the 2 P.M. performance, Riku looked very handsome in his dark slacks and vest, with a white shirt and black tie. Very debonair! He seemed very relaxed about everything, and I don't think it occurred to him to be nervous about this public performance at all. He had a little bit of time to play with his two cousins, who arrived about the same time we did, and then we took our seats and he went off backstage. Akane's parents and sister arrived just in time, so there was a nice family group there for Riku's special day.

The stage was beautifully set with a grand Steinway piano and two big gorgeous displays of flowers. Riku was perhaps the third or fourth child to play; he came out to the front of the stage, bowed without looking out at the crowd, took his seat and played beautifully. He didn't do the usual relaxed swaying he shows at home when playing, but he was certainly relaxed and not at all self conscious about being centre stage. As he bowed at the end of his performance, Haruka, his five-year-old cousin, and Mirei ran up with bouquets to give to him. It was all very sweet.

Riku was one of two boys in the concert, and the other was a teenager. We sat and watched the other pianists, and were all very impressed with the calibre of the talent. It was just sheer pleasure to sit and listen to them and I was especially pleased to hear the teacher play. She's lovely, and I am sure that she is one of the reasons that Riku is doing so well, and enjoying his lessons as he does. She is nurturing and so very encouraging, and also allows her students to choose pieces they would like to play or play them in more than one style.

Riku was on stage for one more piece with all of the younger players, singing "Do Re Me" accompanied by bell ringing, which was lots of fun to see and watch. Following that was a group photo of the students and there were lots of cameras were flashing from all sides.

Before leaving for home, the four children got to spend some time together in the lobby of the hall, until it was time for goodbyes, and we all went our separate ways. We five popped in a cab, as both children were tired, and we were ready to get the evening routine started with them.

After dinner and baths, Akane and Bryn went off with Ayako, to join the Godas for dinner downtown at their hotel, and I tucked the children into bed.

Riku played on the computer for the twenty minutes that it took me to read "Small Pig" to Mirei — she was pointing to all the words she recognized, and learned a couple of new ones too — and leave her sleeping.

After his story, Riku too fell asleep quickly. He went to bed knowing we were all very proud of him. Bryn especially had told him so many times today how proud he was. Riku had enjoyed his day too. Can't wait to see what next year brings ... perhaps Mirei will be on stage too!

I stayed up until Akane and Bryn arrived home at midnight, after a very rare night out for them, perhaps the first since I was here last May.

Lots of "Hellos"!

... there were lots of big puddles on the way to school this morning. Riku should have worn boots, as his shoes got wet, despite him being careful about where to step ... so I got to explain what a "soaker" was.

... as the week went by, more and more children are greeting me in English or expecting me to greet them first. One sweet little girl this afternoon stood right in front of me to say "Hello", and answered "I am fine, thank you" to my question. Then I pointed to the sky and said "It is raining today." to which she replied "Yes, it is raining today." Each word is pronounced very carefully so sounds a bit stilted, which often makes me wish I could spend an hour or two a week with some of the children who are most enthusiastic about speaking English.

Han spent quite a long time telling me how special he feels our family is; how much he enjoys being with such affectionate children, how kind Bryn and Akane have been to him, and how much he enjoyed getting to know me as part of that family group. I was very moved by what he had to say, and do hope I see him again.

The children and I read some of their books together, with Riku joining us for Mirei's "Fancy Nancy", and before we knew it we were into our usual evening/dinner/bath/routine. It all flew by too quickly but the children were very excited that the Easter bunny would be visiting overnight.

Actually, at some point Riku asked me quietly how the Easter bunny got all those candies to the children, and before I could answer he suggested that perhaps the Easter bunny was just made up. He put it in such a way that I had to answer him and so told him that it sure was fun believing that a giant bunny could do such a wonderful thing. So, he knows, and I hope this doesn't mean that he will not be believing in Santa this year. It changes things for them, I know, as I remember that all too clearly myself and with Kelly and Bryn.

Bryn and Akane are continuing to clear out drawers and cupboards, so I had some time on Bryn's computer looking at photos, until it was time to get busy wrapping up all the jelly beans, and the tiny chocolate eggs, and the gifts from the Easter bunny. Bryn and I spent quite a long time counting and putting pink and blue bows on the squares of plastic wrap, just as Kelly and I did not that many years ago for Bryn and Caillie ... lovely memories. I hid them about the living room / dining room while Akane and Bryn finished up their sorting job and off we went to bed. I'm sure the children will be awake extra early tomorrow!


27 March 2010

Easter Morning... a Week Early

Mirei was awake especially early today, and Riku was ready to join her in the big Easter egg hunt the minute she came down the stairs and into our room. It was just 6:30, and I was very surprised to find they had already entered the living room and had found a couple of eggs before their parents were downstairs. So, I hid them, Akane and Bryn came down just a few minutes later, and the children were off, dashing from spot to spot. It was lots of fun to see... they obviously remembered all the good spots, so cushions were lifted, curtains pulled aside, every drawer in the room opened, and they were up on tip-toe and down on the floor peeking everywhere.

Mirei spotted a bag with blue ribbon, and knew that was Riku's. The idea was that they would find them all, and then share them at the end. However, this particular bag had a candy in it that for some reason appealed to her, so she had a chat with Riku convincing him that she would give him a really good one of hers in exchange. Riku agreed, mission accomplished, and back they went to work until every packet, plus the one big gift, was discovered.

They sat tidying up their found treasures, comparing them, and were given permission to eat one packet each. Chocolate was saved for after lunch.

As the children were inspecting the booty, Bryn was busy making his homemade hashbrowns, Akane scrambled the eggs, bread was sliced and toasted, and a fresh pot of tea and juice was ready for us to enjoy. The hash browns were a hit with all of us; Bryn felt they could have been slightly less well cooked last night, but I thought they were perfect and did they ever hit the spot. Yum!

I have written up today in three segments so on to the next... the temple market.

   

28 March 2010

A Last Visit to the Market at the Local Temple

As soon as we had finished up breakfast, and everyone had done their "morning routine" — dress, teeth, hair brush, and face wash — Akane, the children, and I were on our way to a local temple for the Sunday market. The weather was lovely, and we enjoyed our walk/run/stroll along the river, over the bridge, and through the big gates. It was bustling, and Riku headed right over to the stand where he bought the homemade apple "jam" the last time he was here with his mom and dad. I bought him a jar for a special treat, and we wandered around a bit, but before long the children were ready for some quiet time along the stream.

Riku was anxious to play in the woods, so after I found some little gifts for Amelia and Finn, and bought three more of the lovely handmade postcards I had bought from the same friendly gal the last time, the children and I went off to find a quieter spot. We passed two young girls dressed so beautifully in traditional kimonos, with their hair perfectly done up. They were being photographed as they walked along looking at the various booths.

We also saw a group of men in bright yellow robes carrying a large coffin-like box through the crowds, and were delighted later on as they passed us by, without the box, and insisted that the children join in on the photo that I asked if I could take of them.

How Riku and Mirei enjoyed that quiet time in the woody area, just off the beaten track. We were pretty much on our own and the children got busy working together to build a "castle" out of rocks and twigs. Riku was the builder and Mirei the worker, as she went happily wandering about, finding, or trying to find, exactly the sized piece of wood or stone that Riku requested of her.

Akane stopped by with some snacks for them, and went off on her own to wander a bit more. I took some footage of them running and racing together too, on a long pathway.

Riku was very sweet with his sister, deliberately letting her beat him, and telling her she was a "gold medal winner" when she came puffing up to the finish line. I was so proud of him.

At last it was time to go home, and I promised Riku that we would come back within the next day or two so he could continue on with his castle.

But before leaving, we spotted a wedding party, something I believe I have seen every time I have visited this particular shrine, and I stopped to take some photos. The bride and groom, in a group photo with family, along with the bride's mother, gave us all a big smile as we stood admiring them.

We walked along the river again on the way home, but weren't rushing at all ... stopping at a teeter-totter, where Riku pushed Mirei up and down — it was more like a bench seat — until we felt rain drops. Akane put Mirei in the stroller and dashed ahead of us to try to get some clothes off the line at home, while Riku and I walked along, not minding at all that we were getting a bit wet.

We were rewarded with the sun coming out in just a few minutes, and with a big hello to the principal of Riku's school, who was walking along the footpath under the bridge we were crossing when I spotted him. Riku was delighted, and we had a chat about how good it was that his principal was out walking, getting some exercise, and not sitting at home watching TV.

And now on to the third part of our day: colouring Easter eggs!

28 March 2010

Sunday Afternoon in Kyoto

Home for lunch and then it was time for a fun and favoured once-a-year activity ... colouring eggs. I had taken a kit along with me; they had just started putting out the Easter goodies two days before I left for Japan, so I lucked out. The children spread paper on the table, and we got right to work, colouring ten Easter eggs. Riku and Mirei delighted in the colours and took their time dipping and re-dipping; they all turned out beautifully!

Mirei got a new hair-do today; she had her hair pulled back on the sides, and looks adorable. She spent a lot of time playing with a doll house that Akane brought down from her bedroom, and what a time she had with the tiny furniture; Akane joined her for a while too.

Later on, Mirei decided I too needed a new look and stood behind me on the sofa pushing and combing my hair this way and that. I listened to Riku practicing his piano above us, realizing that I have just two more days with the children before winging my way back to San Francisco. I hope the next few months fly by as quickly as this one did!
