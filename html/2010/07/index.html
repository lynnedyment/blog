Morning Playground Outing, and a Dinner Date for Mirei and Bryn

Mirei slept until 8:15 this morning and walked into the kitchen on her own, wishing Doug and me both a "Good morning!" in a big cheerful voice. What a way to start the day!

She is doing such fun pictures right now, using bright primary colours, drawing lots of fun figures, and imaginative flowers. However, she also wants to cut them up into smaller parts now, so I have to dash and get the camera so the whole image is preserved first!

Once Mirei's breakfast was over, she disappeared into the living room to keep Bryn company while he was working. This gave me a chance to clean up the kitchen and sneak in to take a photo of them busy together.

Mirei rode her bike around the garden in the morning and afternoon today, keeping herself amused with a big long stick while riding. I think she was trying to find bugs, and when Riku gets here she will be happy to have him put them in the little bug carrier she had with her on her travels.

It was another gorgeous sunny day, a perfect day to visit the playground up on Potrero Hill. On a hot day this is a great place to be, as it is often windy and cool when the fog is hovering. Today the weather had drawn lots of families there, and Mirei had no trouble at all finding two different playmates for the two and a half hours we spent there.

Home for lunch, a walk to the mailbox with Grandpa again, did her spelling with Bryn ... ten words a day I believe, and read two extra pages in "Are You My Mother?" to me. How well she reads and she hasn't balked at doing it either, that or the spelling.

We had talked about making a cherry pie yesterday and Mirei reminded me about this today. During the day, Mirei often brought up all the women we met yesterday. "Grace had pink lipstick and Victoria had red lipstick but Angie ... hmmm, I don't think Angie had lipstick on, or maybe Ellen too." Mirei thought we could visit them again tomorrow, but we will have to hold off until at least next week.

At 4:45 Bryn and Mirei left for dinner with the Doering/Ellis crew; I had made up two loaves of semolina bread this afternoon to rise, and sent one of the baked loaves over for them. I even got a half batch of cheese bread baked while Mirei was chatting on her "cell phone" while tending to her baby.

After they left I got busy and finally got the second bed made up in the front bedroom, put all of Mirei's clothes in their proper places, put away several books that Mirei had looked at this morning, and wrote lots of emails. There was some toes-up time too, with a cup of tea and the morning paper before Bryn dropped Mirei off at home for us at 8:40. Bryn and Susan were off to a concert tonight.

Mirei had a great time with them all, and was yawning away during her second Caillou story. She was fast asleep in less than ten minutes, and I left her room at 9:05 P.M.

Little Moments

Mirei likes to decide how they will be cooked. "What is scrambled? Oh yes, when the yellow and white are mixed together." or "Fried? What is fried? Oh yes, a dippy egg." Today for the first time she slid her own egg into the frying pan, and checked twice, lifting the lid carefully, to see if it was "just right!". The egg was served today, along with toast and "bacon" as her brunchy meal before going on to a...

Picnic! Yes, Mirei went to a second picnic in a row today, once more with her dad, and joining up with Auntie Susan, Kevin, Amelia, and Finn and other friends of theirs. Mirei was all primed to go, and once we had the right top on (we decided she had better wear a sleeveless top) kept calling to Bryn "Let's go Daddy! I'm all ready!" There will be many many more picnics this summer for certain.

Drawing: Red letter day today! Mirei drew lots of flowers, and a house beside some giant flowers, but tonight she decided to draw a man and a woman getting married (we had watched a Japanese movie called "The Cat Returns" which had a subplot of a wedding) and she drew bodies on them both, rather than stick figures for the trunk, arms, and legs. She seems to have bypassed the nipple, belly button phase though as her people are always dressed.

Spelling: Sometime after I left Japan in March, Bryn started "spelling homework" with Mirei. It's lots of fun listening to her rhyme them off, sometimes using a singsong voice, sometimes being perfectly silly and deliberately misspelling them just to hear Bryn protest, I'm sure, but always getting them right. I think she's ready to move on.

Little bits... tonight I videotaped Mirei and Doug using a fake language, something I started with Riku when he was Mirei's age. It is such fun to watch and hear her doing this, as she can keep it up for the longest time, sounding perfectly serious and involved with the pretend dialogue with lots of animation involved too! She and I walked several blocks home from the playground the day she arrived chatting away in ourspeak!

Happy 4th! Fireworks and noisemakers are going off as I type this!

We stopped in at the library and Mirei had a little time pulling books out to look at them herself. "Fish is Fish" is home with us, as well as the picture book of "Baby Beluga" which we have been singing. Another song she has learned is "You Are My Sunshine" and she has the first verse down pat. Bryn came in while she was finishing up her dinner, which was lovely as I thought he might get home after she was asleep, which was at 7:30 tonight.

"Lucky's" it was, a one-man-run diner with Lucky taking the orders, cooking up the food, cutting the pancakes into small bites for the children(!), serving it, urging the children to finish what was on their plates, passing the leftovers over to Grandpa and Dad, cleaning the counters, and taking the cash too. We were impressed for sure, and the food was typical diner breakfast food, but at a much lower price than usual. The children sat drawing at the counter while waiting for their breakfast.

On the way to the zoo, we sang all the songs on our tape, always singing "Baby Beluga" with gusto, and with everyone joining it too. The children love us to sing together, and we do quite well with most of the other songs now, as we have heard them so often.

This zoo was wonderful, my first trip there ever. We tried to go once when Kelly and Bryn were youngsters, but it just poured the entire time we spent in southern L.A. that year ... and it was the year the song "It Never Rains in Southern California" by Albert Hammond, was popular too. I remember oh-so-well singing it all the way down and back in the car.

The children both enjoy carrying a map and plotting out their moves, so Riku suggested we go to the reptile house first. He was very taken with the massive boa constrictor, and weeks later Mirei still remembered, and demonstrated, how they give you such big hugs that they crush you.

We didn't see half the zoo, but we did take our time and enjoy all that we did see. Doug was thrilled to see a huge tank with a massive hippopotamus, up close, very close! It's the best view he has ever had of these massive creatures and the children crowded in to see him move around too. They were quite taken with the large sculpture of a hippo too and waited for their chance to climb on it, and play for as long as we were willing to sit there.

I have a fondness for elephants, and toward the end of our time there, we came to the huge area devoted to twelve elephants, both Asian and African, and all just beautiful. We saw giraffes and condors, and massive tortoises too. It's a grand zoo and I would jump at the chance to go again.

The weather was changing from hot to cool all throughout the day, but that was preferable to the searing heat we might have had. I think we lucked out with the weather on this trip, as only Disneyland was a little hotter than we would have preferred.

Before leaving about 5 P.M. the children got to choose a small toy from the gift shop and they both chose lizards. These proved to be very entertaining for part of the trip home, as Mirei babied hers, when it wasn't battling with Riku's. However, once again, the children proved to be such troopers at sitting in the car for eight plus hours. We stopped once for Mirei to go to the bathroom and twice for gas.

We ate dinner in the car again, to get us home faster, and we had snacks along with us too: shortbread cookies, a bit of fruit, and nibblies ... cheese sticks, a few crackers, and lots of cold water as well.

Mirei entertained us by singing "Up the tall white candlestick went little Mousie Brown", with such flair and emphasis on "But Mother was in town!" that she made us laugh out loud more than once. Riku encouraged us in telling bits of ghost stories, one of his favourite ways to pass the time, although Mirei's bits tended to be very long and detailed and he had to cut her short sometimes.

They had had a busy few days, and not as much sleep as usual, so I fully expected them both to be asleep very soon after starting out. Riku gave in first, about 8 o'clock but Mirei held out until after 9, chattering away to herself and her reptile babies or to us. Finally that little head dropped and she was out like a light, all the way to San Francisco, finally landing in at home at 1 A.M.

I didn't sleep at all, much to my surprise, and Bryn carried the children upstairs and tucked them right into bed together. The children slept right through their move from the car to the bed except for just a few minutes during the transfer. I got a peek of them snuggled up beside each other before I headed up.

I stayed up to wind down a bit, checking my emails and responding to a couple of them. Bed at 2 A.M. for me with plans for a much earlier to bedtime tonight!

Home Again and Franklin Square

23 July 2010

Pets, and a Playground!

Bryn and Riku were up and ready for their two-hour drive to Sacramento about 7:30 this morning. Bryn planned to stop at McDonald's for a rare breakfast as they drove; they will be meeting up with Susan, Kevin, Amelia, and Finn for a long day at the fair. I packed up enough fruit and veggies in a cooler to last Riku for two days, along with a few snacks too for the car trip and in the hotel tonight. Surprisingly, Mirei was fine with letting them go off without her, but I had promised we would have fun here today.

And we did.We had a splendid day, started off by a long long "let's cuddle!" suggestion by Miss Mirei. Unlike Kelly's Bryn, Caillie, and Riku, all of whom always wanted to be read a big stack of books in the morning in bed, Mirei prefers to talk ... about what we are going to do today, what we might do tomorrow, and going over some of the things that happened yesterday. I get lots of hugs around the neck, lots of attention if I need some ... she is very good at finding the pressure points in my face when my sinuses are bad, or rubbing any sore spot she thinks I might have. I get an extra blanket and an animal too, and then we get to "talk" animal talk together, as she always gathers one in for herself too.

After lunch she and I headed out on the bus to a playground at the top of Noe Valley. I hadn't been there for years, but I spotted a pet store on the opposite corner as we got off the bus, so we went in to check it out. There was a big friendly dog to get past first; Mirei loves the idea of dogs, and is fine petting their back ends, but she is very nervous about their teeth and tongue being too close to her. Once we were at the back of the store, she was just thrilled with all the birds in large cages: lovebirds, parrots, finches, and others. We spent at least a half hour while she checked them all out, and then checked them out again. She now does a great imitation of a parrot moving up and down!

The playground at the corner of 24th and Diamond has been completely redone since I was last there with Bryn and Caillie ... many years ago! They have the soft cushiony padding down, a large curving sandbox, and a great little climbing set, more for the younger children, rather than Riku's age group. There are also some climb-on toys and a see-saw, so enough to keep Mirei happy for a good two hours, especially with all the darling young babies and children around! We met ten-month-old Noah and his dad, Andy, then a little girl named Nova, and after that a darling little boy called Cooper, who it turned out is a neighbour of Noah's! All the parents were totally smitten with Mirei and her motherly ways with their little ones. Mirei is sweet and affectionate with them, and they smile and smile at her ... as she does them. Mirei insists she is not going to have a baby "because it hurts too much", but I hope she ends up with at least four of them! What a great mom she is going to be!

Mirei asked for a "treat" before leaving Noe Valley, so I bought her a little tub of Haagen-Dazs vanilla ice cream which she ate on the bus on the way home. There was a friendly woman standing behind us on the crowded bus, and she engaged Mirei in quite a conversation, talking about how pretty Mirei was, and about her own little granddaughter too. Mirei is quite happy to chat with strangers, although can be shy in some situations.

When we were on the bus on the way to the playground, I thought I had left my camera at home, but the more I thought about it the more I convinced myself that I had put it in my purse. But as the time went on, I somehow believed that it had fallen out when the snack pack I was carrying fell on the floor of the bus. I was so upset to not only lose the camera, but also to miss out on all the great shots of Mirei with the little ones today. Sure enough, when I got home it was not where I thought I might have left it, and I phoned the bus office to report it missing.

We read a nice stack of books tonight at the table while Mirei was eating her dinner. It was a nice treat as we don't often do this, and certainly never this many.

As I was reading to Mirei in bed, Doug opened the bedroom door, much to my surprise (it's not something he would normally do), to let me know that he had found my camera ... in my purse! It had just worked down into the corner of my almost empty bag and I just hadn't felt around for it long enough. Whew! Doug had been thinking about how hard it would be for the camera to fall out if it, as the way the purse is designed would make that difficult for sure.

First I felt relief, of course, but I was also sad that I had missed all the afternoon shots out with Mirei. But, what would I have done if I had lost it??? Lucky me!

Pancakes, Puzzles, and Painting

It's Sunday, so we decided to make Grandpa pancakes. Mirei helped with each stage of the mixing up as she always does if there is anything to be cooked! I took lots of photos of her doing each step, and she reminded me to "take a picture, Nana!" if I had overlooked a photo op'! The pancakes were delicious: Mirei was delighted with the heart-shaped one I made for her, and served Doug the homemade applesauce he was putting on his first couple today.

After her "morning routine" Mirei went upstairs and brought down several of the Ravensburger puzzles that all of the grandchildren have done over the years. She, like the others, is very good at them, and often finds a particular piece we are looking for before me. We worked one of the three together but it was more fun to watch her face as she scouted out the right piece for the right spot.

Mirei realized she had painted only one picture since she came to San Francisco, so out came the paints. She painted a glorious happy pair of flowers and was especially pleased when she found out that I had a little bit of white paint and she could indeed have pink! She quite enjoyed mixing up her own shade of her favourite colour for the rest of that painting, and the next one of a tall house too.

We had talked about making cookies as a "surprise for Daddy", so we got right to that after the painting was done. I took a kazillion photos of Mirei baking with me again; she loved using the mixer, turning it on and off between adding the dry ingredients, as well as pushing the batter down away from the sides of our very full bowl. I didn't have the right candy bar for these wonderful cookies of Judi's, so I cut up some Turtles and Mirei was very happy scooping them up and adding them to the batter. She could hardly wait to lick the batters!

The cookies were made of oatmeal, chocolate chips, Turtles, and dried cherries; some oatmeal and bits and pieces of the chopped Turtles had fallen to the floor during the measuring, so Mirei got busy with the broom and dustpan and did a great job sweeping it all up! What a girl she is; she even said "You shouldn't have to do everything, Nana, so I will help you." ... and she really did. She even volunteered to wash the paint holders; of course she loved holding the sprayer and did a great job managing that too, and I was only too happy to dry them, as she suggested.

We had expected Bryn and Riku home earlier in the afternoon and thought we might have an outing to a playground with him. However, by the time we had tidied up they still weren't home, so Mirei suggested a trike ride on the sidewalk. Mirei hasn't had a lot of practice at steering at all, and she went all the way up two long blocks figuring that out very well, well enough that she could steer into and out of a small space at the town house complex to "scare" me.

We were on our way up from the opposite side of the sidewalk when Bryn and Riku pulled into the garage. I'm not sure if Mirei was happier to see her Dad, or he her! Lots of big hugs and many kisses, while Riku told me about some of his adventures in Sacramento. I heard more over dinner time with the children; Riku loved being in the pool with his dad, Amelia, and Finn, he loved the high flying swings (Bryn sent photos to us), and Mirei got to tell Riku about the birds and the new playground too. Bryn said next summer Mirei would go with them too. I'm thinking that a really inexpensive summer holiday treat would be three days in a hotel with a pool, or even four days! Just think of the money we would save on gas, and we could just hang out all day long in the pool.

Errands, "Our Playground", and a New Book Too!

We had tentative plans to go to a movie today, either with Susan and the children or on our own, but the morning was a busy one with both children involved with either drawing or reading. It seemed we had plenty to do to keep busy, plus Riku had homework as well. He's quite keen to get some done every day, and said he was worried about not finishing it. So he did two full pages today of math and Japanese writing, and filled in some titles of books for his reading list. Mirei read to me from her current book, but today we started in the middle and went forward from there. By the time she gets through most of it, she is not so keen to keep at it, so this seemed like a good plan.

Mirei drew me two pictures today and now signs them to me as well, each and every time without my asking. She was cross with me at one point, though, and went to an older picture she had drawn for me that was hanging on the fridge, crossed off "Nana" and printed in "Mommy". Later she apologized to me and said she would never do that again.

After a big lunch of mac' and cheese with carrots, spinach, and their one piece of candy from Riku's trip yesterday, the three of us headed off to run some errands, followed by playground time.

They are both always happy to go to Rainbow with me, to sample the cheese, and to help pick out the carrots. It was a quick trip for more strawberries and yogurt, and then we set off for Costco for a few items there. Mirei had memorized our list three days ago and she had it down pat: oranges, dates for date squares, and skin cream "for Daddy". They like the samples there too, and had yogurt, and some chicken and fish too.

I was willing to go to a new playground Bryn went to with Mirei two weeks ago, but Riku insisted he was okay going to this closer one. It worked out beautifully, as it gave us more time there, and a nice grassy space where Riku and I could use the badminton set. Mirei started off playing with him, but soon found some children her own age, and spent the two-plus hours we were there mostly with them. I was surprised to see her with a lime paleta (Mexican Popsicle) at one point; one of the girls' dads had bought them all one, and Mirei was delighted. She shared the end of it with Riku too.

Riku had an orange for dessert and Mirei chose to have "a bowl of custard with just one strawberry on top." The Maira Kalman Exhibit at the Jewish Contemporary Museum

After the sweet photo of the children sleeping last night, I couldn't resist getting one of them this morning, being anything but sleepy! They had lots of fun rolling around on the bed for the longest time, taking animals and babies in with them, and finally emerging for some quiet drawing at the table while they had their fruit plates.

Riku decided after breakfast he had better get some of his homework done, so Mirei continued taking care of the babies and animals.

Today Riku surprised me by making the bed himself. It was quite a challenge after all the tossing and turning of the duvet but he worked away at it and did a very good job. With Mirei now sweeping the kitchen floor quite regularly, and both of them folding their PJs in the mornings, I told them I would give them a quarter every Sunday if they kept it up. I know, it's child labour for certain, but they are very excited about their new earning power!

I had intended to go to the Jewish Contemporary Museum since it opened on Mission Street a couple of years ago, perhaps, but had never made it there. Susan told me the other day that there was an exhibit there that she planned to take Amelia to, and suggested that we come along.

We had time at home for Riku to work on his homework, and Mirei read to me as well. Nice to have that out of the way so early in the day, and both children were happy doing it.

I hadn't heard of the very well known and respected illustrator, writer, and collector of whimsy, but Susan thought that there was a good chance I would recognize some of her work. I did, all from The New Yorker covers I have seen here at the house, but it turned out that I had also read one of her children's books to Riku, about a dog who eats everything from A to Z.

Susan treated me to the exhibit and the children were admitted free, and given very interesting small plastic boxes with a handle, containing clues to a hunt for particular objects, a notebook for observations, and some fun objects to think about. Riku and Amelia particularly enjoyed the search for a key, a shoe, and a rubber band, and all of them enjoyed working with the small felt boards, making various faces with them, and drawing them in their notebooks before changing them once again. I loved felt boards when I was a child, and must get some in for the children. They're light and easy to carry too, so would be a great addition to the drawing pads and pencils in restaurants and the car.

I enjoyed this well enough that I would love to go back and see more of it at my own pace and interest, as I too love to collect whimsy, if on a much lesser scale! However, my old wooden thread spools, tiny packets of my grandmother's sewing needles, hooks and eyes, ancient baking utensils, tiny figures, and household objects, plus many other items I own, would have fit in perfectly there.

We were all hungry when we finished up, so we decided on lunch at Mel's. All the children had small milkshakes — strawberry for Amelia, chocolate for Mirei, and vanilla for Riku — along with their grilled cheese sandwiches. Susan snapped a photo of them drinking their shakes, which I will include here. One day the children will love having a peek at these, as will we.

Today the children had been invited over for a playdate with a friend of Amelia's. Riku and Ivan are a year apart in age, but Ivan has similar interests to Riku — art, reading, and Ultraman — so Bryn really wanted them to get together. Anabel and Mirei are just a few days apart in age, I believe, so they are likely to get along as well. We had some quiet time at home before driving to their home in the Mission District at 3:30. I came home to get a few things caught up, mostly blog entries and photos, but also to prepare dinner.

Doug picked them up at 5:15 and they all drove to the train station to meet Bryn coming in on a 5:40 train from the valley. Bryn was delighted to see the children at the station platform, although Mirei found the train very loud, but she got to tell him about her time with Anabel, and Riku about visiting with Ivan. They all enjoyed it very much, which was lovely, as they haven't had a lot of playdates this summer.

We had dinner as soon as everyone was home, followed by our usual evening routine. The children were tired and fell asleep easily, and I got a few more days worth of photos caught up after that.
