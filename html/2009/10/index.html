Bryn and Mirei Arrive!

Bryn's and Mirei's plane touched down a half hour early this morning, and we were there to see those smiling faces as they walked through the gate. Mirei had her hair cut last month, and the shorter more structured cut is adorable on her. Bryn looked fit and well, and had a great flight with Mirei. He said not only did she sleep for four hours, not something he was sure would happen, but she napped in the limo on the way to the airport, and was a delightful traveling companion: happy, entertaining, and quiet. I'm sure their seat companions nearby appreciated that too!

Mirei walked in the house and within minutes was back doing just what she always did ... serving us "breakfast" from the little kitchen area, tending to her two dolls in the cradle and stroller, deciding to draw me a picture, followed by asking to paint, then use the Play-Doh, and finally ending this morning's activities by going off to the living room to read on the sofa. Whew!

Bryn left with Doug after noon to pick up his rental car, and was heading straight off after that to Murphys, a very small town in central California, to meet up with groom-to-be Brent and others for the pre-wedding guy party. Mirei gave him a big hug when he left, and gave her Grandpa a big hug too.

We puttered about, doing this and that downstairs. I was busy with laundry, too, and she was happy to just poke about rediscovering old toys. The bag of various necklaces came out and Mirei was busy stringing those around her neck, but decided not to use any of the necklaces with shells on them. Interesting. They would have been Riku's first choice!

She had Doug busy catching and tossing a ball to her at one point, and then she joined me upstairs while I was having a quiet cup of tea. We decided to just drop the ball from various heights into each other's hands. I got lots of snuggles in there too! She pulled Bryn's old play farm up on the bed with us and she chatted away about animals, and Old MacDonald, singing the song too, of course.

The afternoon passed quickly, Mirei ate a hearty late lunch, and then we walked over on this bright sunny warm day to the playground on Potrero Avenue, singing nursery rhymes as we went.

We passed a funky car on the way, and Mirei posed very prettily beside it after noticing I was just going to take a shot of the car!

Once at the playground, she climbed, went down the slides, climbed up on the big structure there about halfway only today, and used all the other playground equipment too. We walked home slowly, with Mirei stopping to poke a small stick into whatever holes in the sidewalk and walls that she could find. About a half block from the house, she declared she was tired, but didn't ask to be carried. She must have been soooo tired after such a long, long day.

I popped her into a warm tub with bubbles and two "babies" to wash and shampoo, and made her up a big dinner of eggs, "bacon", half a bagel, corn, and edamame. I was pouring her some milk and realized she hadn't said a word; I just assumed she was busy eating, but instead found her slowly drifting sideways, as she was off to sleep! I wanted her to eat more than she had, but it was no use at all. She managed a couple more bites, once I put her on my lap, but then she snuggled into my shoulder, I carried her off to bed, and shut the door at 7:45. I do hope she sleeps through the night and wakens happily adjusted to the time change, but we shall see.

Such a sweet loving girl is Mirei! We all got lots of hugs and kisses throughout the day, and as always she had us laughing at her antics. How she loves to make people laugh!

I'm a bit jet lagged myself, mostly because I didn't sleep long enough the last few nights before leaving Ontario, so it's going to be an early night for me too.

Jet Leg and the Bay Area Discovery Museum

Much to my surprise, Mirei slept in until 9:45 this morning, a fifteen hour jet lag recovery! She awoke at 1A.M. and I joined her in bed, but she slept right through, long after I had left her room. I even managed to get a photo of her and she didn't budge!

It was a perfect San Francisco day; the sun was shining, the weather was warm, no wind, and the sky a beautiful blue, with no clouds. So, after quite a leisurely two hours at home, Mirei and I headed off for her first day's adventure, to the Bay Area Discovery Museum. Half an hour later, with heavier than usual traffic, Mirei was sound asleep! I let her sleep for a while in the car at the museum, but she finally roused with the comings and goings of the other cars.

We had lunch immediately, our usual picnic fare of a grilled cheese sandwich, and filled out with goodies I brought from home. I was surprised that Mirei chose to go to the indoor activity area first, rather than playing outside, but it was quiet and relaxing there today, with fewer people around than usual, so she quite enjoyed people watching at the clay table.

Not much later the craft room opened, and Mirei happily joined in, making a puppet face with a paper plate. It's interesting to see what a people watcher Mirei is! In both places, and perhaps moreso today because she was tired, she often just sits and observes others around her. She loved gluing pink "hair" onto her plate, and added eyebrows and lashes to the face too.

Her next stop was the toddler area. My guess is that she is going to be too tall for this spot next year. I hope not, as she still loves to meander from room to room there, jumping, lying, and relaxing on the water lily ponds, buzzing about the small climbing platform next door, and moving fast between the two. She made me laugh out loud when she spread out her legs over three areas of the place where she can make the frogs' bubbles visible by stepping on them. Too bad she didn't have a middle leg for this, as she managed to really stretch out those legs!

We spent a lot of time there, until I reminded her that the fish stream was outside, and that used up another half hour of our time. Before we left that area, she stopped to use the little chalkboard, and wrote her name, Nana, Riku, and Mommy all by herself. A fellow who works there, and had recognized her from previous visits (even asking where her big brother was!), was amazed. She likes to print too, as Riku does it and she wants to be just like him. All her favourite things were fitted in, ending with climbing on the boat, going through the giant log, climbing up the log steps all by herself, and ringing the bell several times at the top of the hill.

We left just as the museum was closing, at 4 P.M., so there was no time for the rolling pennies table today, but I promised her we would be there earlier next time. She managed to stay awake on the way home, and we stopped at Rainbow for a few groceries.

Mirei was practicing catching a big ball today with both Doug and me, and we did more practicing this evening. She's improving steadily and is very proud of herself! Later she told me she liked my garden and went outside on her own, riding on the trike for a bit, and taking her babies out for a stroller ride.

We had dinner before the tub tonight; her baby got another hair wash, as did Mirei, and the bubbles were out too during tub time. It was a nice relaxing end to our day, and she was tucked in at 7:40 tonight.

At 10 o'clock Mirei woke up. Even though she is lying quietly, with me beside her for over an hour, it seems that sleep is eluding her, so I am off to join her now, at 11:30. That wee cat nap must have given her some extra mileage!

Saturday ... Felt Like a Sunday!

Mirei slept until 10:33 this morning! Even though she was awake for perhaps an hour and a half after 10 last night, she still slept twelve and a half hours. She has done so well with this adjustment to West Coast time, and stumbled into the kitchen squinting and smiling. After a cuddle and some hugs on my lap, she went right to her little kitchen to get me something to "eat", just as though that was her duty. I prepared eggs, toast, and "bacon" for us while I was served fruit and juice. She obviously knows me well. We had a very lazy morning, and Mirei watched "the green Caillou" while I showered just before noon. I must say I love mornings we don't have to dash off.

We didn't have much in the way of plans for today at all. It's the weekend and often the places we would like to go to are very busy. We did have casual plans with Susan to possibly meet up at Ocean Beach this afternoon, but set off after lunch to a Vegetarian Food fair near Mirei's Japanese nursery school. As we drove her by the school, we noticed the door was open, so stopped to see if Mirei's teacher was there. She was, and greeted Mirei with a big smile. Mirei was quite shy with her, but did want to go inside the door. The teacher and her husband were decorating for Hallowe'en, and so Mirei saw a little bit of that. We made arrangements for Mirei to go to school on Fridays, from 9–1, and then she will go on to play with Jonah, a little boy from her class, after that. Right now Mirei is saying that she doesn't want to go to that school, or to go to Jonah's house, but we shall see how she is feeling closer to the day that will happen.

The Vegetarian Fair was interesting, and Mirei was quite happy trying out various samples of yogurt, vegan cookies, tofu, veggie "chips", juices, and coconut ice cream. She also picked up some stickers and chose a poster of healthy food choices, done in a cartoon style, that is now hanging in her room. There was a friendly gal who invited Mirei over to colour, so she did that, and chatted away with her, telling her her name, and her brother's name, and their ages.

It was quite windy and she had Doug carrying her to and from the car for part of both trips. I had my Sudoku book in the car with me, and while I was sitting in the back seat with her, she practiced her numbers, telling me each one correctly. I hadn't realized she knew her numbers so it was fun being her "teacher".

We drove to Ocean Beach for the sand castle contest, but it was 4 P.M., the tide was coming in, and there weren't too many people on that blowy beach. I even sat in the car while Doug and Mirei got out to see if we could spend a little time there, but it was just too cold and windy.

On the way home, we stopped at the playground at the top of Noe Valley, and spent forty-five minutes there. I was cold, but Mirei was quite happy going down the slides, having Doug push her on the swings, and watching all the birthday children gathered there.

Mirei got right into the tub when we got home, I made pasta for dinner, and then we practiced catching the ball ... she's getting better and better, and is now tossing it into the air and catching it regularly, and reading on the sofa. She had gone in the living room before me, while I cleaned up the dinner dishes, and I found her sitting quietly in the corner chair waiting patiently for me.

It's such a pleasure to share our days with this little girl. There wasn't an unhappy moment in her day from morning until night and those smiles just keep on coming. She's such a good little companion. Mirei has her Grandpa and me grinning away, and it's obvious she enjoys entertaining us; I just can't imagine her not here with us.

Sound asleep at 7:35 tonight, snuggled up against me, with her nose touching mine! Such a pleasure from that first sleepy smile to that last snuggle.

Tilden Park Day

Mirei slept until just after 8 o'clock today, giving her a solid twelve and a half hours of sleep. I think the jet lag is over and she has settled into a regular sleep now. As soon as she saw Doug eating his breakfast this morning, she decided she wanted to eat exactly what he was eating, so it was a container of yogurt, a cinnamon raisin English muffin, and juice for her, or at least for the first course of her morning meal.

She watched "a different Caillou, the blue one" today while I showered. Later, while she was making lots of party dishes for me in the kitchen, I told her Daddy was coming back today, and her reply was "Today? Tuesday?!"; I learned later from Bryn that Tuesday is her day of choice when she feels driven to name a day.

I am hearing lots of "Oh my goodness" and "Oh my gosh!" from this little girl lately, as well as many other expressions that sound like me.

It has been a long time since we had Mirei at the little steam train ride in Tilden Park in Berkeley, so I packed up a picnic lunch and off we went. First stop was the "Redwood Valley Railway Company" and we lucked out in that the smaller, and free train was running when we got there. We had to wait a little bit for the steam to build up, and it was chilly standing there, but once on the train we were in the sunshine and it was much more pleasant.

We were able to get on the larger train immediately and Mirei pointed out every small building along the route. There was a group with five children sitting behind us, and as always Mirei spent a lot of time observing them, able to tell me later how many were boys and how many were girls. She doesn't miss very much, this girl!

It was time for lunch and so we drove close to the small farm in the park, enjoying our lunch sitting on a bench in the sunshine. Mirei was anxious to finish up and play in the playground, so after half of hers was finished I let her run off and come back for bites. There is a series of small climbing rocks here, and Mirei climbed them all. She did a great job except that she insisted on climbing down the last foot or so standing looking out!

We played ball for the longest time, using a small pink ball someone had left behind. We bounced it, rolled out, and tossed it over the course of the hour we spent there, and had lots of fun. Then it was time to stroll off to the little farm, a much larger area than Doug remembered it being from many years ago. Mirei saw cows up very close, goats, sheep, turkeys, both wild and domestic, ducks, chickens, rabbits, and rams, and managed to find a few pieces of lettuce and celery to feed to two cows.

On the way home, Mirei asked me to point out the numbers in the Sudoku book I had in the car, so with the point of my pencil, I was the "teacher" and was surprised that she recognized the numbers to nine. She hadn't known them when she went to Japan in March, and so it was the first time I realized that she had learned them.

We sang lots of songs on this trip and Mirei has started doing what Brynnie did when he was a little guy ... making up the words for new songs. I recorded her doing this yesterday, and wish I had gotten my camera out today to do more of it. Once home we continued singing, and just before Bryn came home, about 7:15, we had been singing about the ducks not coming back. She will have this down pat within the week I am sure.

Tub time, of course, and then dinner, since she had snacked on the way home with her leftover lunch, and then Bern, Bryn's friend, came over to say hello to her just before her bedtime. Mirei was thrilled to bits to have her here with her, and they played with all the "food" and dishes in the little kitchen, making more party food as she had this morning. Guess we have a party girl on our hands!

I took Mirei off to bed, just as Bryn and Bern were leaving for a concert. It was 8:15 when she fell asleep; it took her a little longer to settle down after her excitement of having both her Daddy and Bern here. She told Bryn she missed him so much, and she was so glad he was home. He looked mighty pleased himself, after having had a great weekend wedding celebration with Brent, Mikela, and all their guests.

We're Going to the Zoo!

I woke up at 6:30 this morning, and even though I was very quiet, Mirei woke up at the same time and decided she had to go to the bathroom too. I was sure that was it, that she was awake for the day, but I was amazed that just minutes after snuggling back into the duvet, Mirei was breathing quietly and slept for another hour. I dozed on and off, and was watching her sleep when those eyes opened up. She always looks over and smiles, as though questioning whether it's okay to be awake, and today when I answered her "good morning!", she said "That was fast!", meaning, I assume, the night. Perhaps she thought it only lasted that one final hour!

Mirei had suggested yesterday that we go to the zoo, but I thought Doug had something going on today that required him having the car. I was wrong, and so the zoo was on after all, much to Mirei's delight. I decided we would spend the morning at home, and head out after lunch, avoiding the need to carry our lunches around the vast zoo grounds.

I had some cards and letters to write, so after Mirei had time with her "babies", and I cleaned up the kitchen, I joined Mirei while she watched "Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs". In between watching the bits she had questions about, I got quite a bit accomplished. This worked well and it gave me a chance to put my feet up too, before the busy afternoon got started.

Mirei asked if she could ride her trike this morning, and so Doug took her out, up and down the block again, although Doug said today she was more interested in a more exciting ride, perhaps standing on the seat while he pushed her! Guess she feels she has that pedaling down pat.

We got to the zoo at 1 P.M. and left just after 4. We covered a lot of territory during that time too, and still didn't see the bears, all the Australian animals, or the tigers, so we will go another day. We did see lots, though ... the male lion wandering about, the baby gorilla climbing on a biggish rock, while his mom looked on ... and then disappeared on him, causing him to slide down off the rock head first, scrambling after her.

The giraffes were wonderful to see, as always; Mirei pointed out the zebra, and now knows that male peacocks are the ones with the pretty feathers. Today Mirei was determined to climb on every animal sculpture she came across, and I think I got photos of every one of them!

We went to the farm area, and Mirei got up the courage to feed the rambunctious goats, with some help and security management from Grandpa. She did a great job, though, and for the last two-thirds of the feeding she was keeping her hand nice and steady and not minding their being a little aggressive. Mirei enjoyed watching the tiny chicks, not long ago hatched out, and felt the woolly sheep too.

I think her favourite part of the zoo of all today, though, was watching a tiny alligator, who was sitting on a turtle, and then watched as the zoo keeper picked him up so that the turtles could be fed. She spent a lot of time moving about and peering into the water to see them, and watched an owl for a long time too. We should probably take her once a week while she's here just so she really gets to know her animals. Today she showed so much interest in so many of them.

We all spent some time watching the gophers today; Mirei grinned away as she got to pretend she was one of them. And she seemed to enjoy watching the meercats too, just like her Grandpa, who finds them fascinating.

A special big treat for her today was candy floss, the first time she has asked for it. Before buying it, though, we went into the café so she could eat a yogurt for her mid-afternoon snack, visited the farm area, and then she had the floss. Interestingly, even though she really liked it, it wasn't too long before she handed me back her last piece and said we should save some for another day. It will be interesting to see if she asks for it tomorrow. Riku likes it too, but ate even less of it than Mirei did, before saying he didn't want any more of it.

On the final leg of our day at the zoo, Mirei decided to ham it up. She pulled her arms out of her shirt, and walked the rest of the way out of the area with her shirt up, patting her belly, posing with silly faces, and walking backwards. It was just hilarious and she attracted lots of attention, which just egged her on. What a girl! I think she needs a t-shirt that says this!

I could see that Mirei was close to drifting off in the car on the way there, so we opened our windows up a bit, and did a little game of naming letters and numbers. This kept her busy and happy, and she was active and busy all the time we were at the zoo. On the way home she went back to the paper and pencil drawing and naming letters, and she wasn't even close to drifting off, much to my surprise.

Bryn bought a new skateboard today, and Mirei was so very interested in it. I don't know if this bodes well for the future, what with her desire to stand on her trike at this stage! Bryn put her on it in the kitchen and she grinned away as he scooted her up and down the kitchen area. I got a video of it too, which made her laugh.

Mirei has been playing with Play-Doh a lot during her meals; it's a treat for her to just sit and roll it, press out shapes, and blow out little circles using a hollow tube. It's one of her favourite pastimes and there is lots of it here for her.

Quick tub time after dinner tonight, and then I read her the first of the seven stories from "The Everyday Story Book" that I won when I was in grade one. All of the grandchildren know these stories, and of course their parents too, so I'm glad I remembered to read Mirei "Sally's Lost Shoe" tonight. She enjoyed it very much and we shall continue with the rest of the stories this coming week.

It was a grand day, and the weather was perfect for it as well. Our busy girl was asleep at 7:35 tonight, and I got lots of emails cleared up.

Mirei Bakes Date Squares

Mirei and I baked date squares, using my Mom's recipe from many, many years ago. I used to bake these at home when I was a teenager, and of course continued to bake them after I was married and had my own children. It is one of the first recipes hand-written in my over-forty-year-old collection of recipes. They are a family favourite, and Mirei decided she likes them too. Here is the original recipe:

1 1/2 cups of flour
1/2 teaspoon of baking soda
3/4 cup of melted butter
2 cups of rolled oats (I use the finer, rather than the coarse flakes)
3/4 cup of brown sugar (I use just a scant half now)
1 pound of pitted dates (I remember going to the store for "dates without pits") as a child and being worried that I could only find "pitted dates", which I thought meant that they had pits!)
1 cup water (I use perhaps a cup and a half)

Sift flour and baking soda together and add rolled oats, the sugar, and melted butter. Mix thoroughly. Set aside.

To make filling:
Cook together the pound of dates with water added to them. Cook on a low heat, stirring occasionally, until the dates soften and break down to a soft mass. Cool.

Grease either an 8×8" pan or—as we prefer them to be thinner and with more of them!—a 9×13" pan. Divide the rolled oats mixture in half and spread one half on the bottom of the pan. Press down with hands or the back of a wet fork.

Spread cooled date mixture over the surface, filling in the corners, and then press down with a fork to even it out. Spread remaining oats on the top, smooth it out, and then press with the back of a wet fork.

Bake at 350ºF just until golden brown, perhaps for a half hour. Do not allow to overcook. Cool before cutting into squares. Enjoy!

(If using a glass pan, lower the heat by 25ºF degrees; my Mom told me this and sure enough, later on, in Home Ec' class, I learned that this was good advice.)

For a different square, especially at Christmas, replace the dates with mincemeat and follow the same recipe.

Enjoy!

During the last fifteen minutes I made up a snack pack for Mirei mostly, with an egg and "bacon" omelet, grapes and orange sections, a few treats, and tucked in a new book for her too, "The Saggy Baggy Elephant". She's a good little traveller and I know it will be a good trip for them both.

Bryn suggested we drop them off at the airport but I wanted to stay with Mirei through the checking-in procedure, and I was glad we did. It all took a while and she and I were able to have some extra time together. We all waved many many times while they went through to the security line, and then one last wave before they disappeared on us.

The house was very very quiet and I immediately started pulling sheets off the beds, and keeping busy. There is so much to do before I leave for Japan in just eleven days, and I sat down and added to the long list of things to do before I am on that plane for eleven hours. The rest of the day flew by for me, but I took time to sit and go through some magazines; they have been stacking up faster than I could read them, and Doug and I watched one of our rented movies that have also been collecting. My mind kept going to that plane, and as the hours went by I wondered what Mirei was doing to keep busy, whether she and her dad were sleeping, and how excited they would be to get home again.



29 October 2009

Not the Day We Expected to Have!

I checked my email as soon as I came downstairs today and was happy to have one in from Bryn, saying that Mirei got a ten out of ten as a traveling companion, and that they were at the shuttle stop, waiting for their trip home. Later I heard that Mirei slept only one hour of the eleven hour flight, but was a happy and engaged girl all her waking hours. She didn't even sleep on the shuttle on the way home, which really surprised me!

That was good news to start my day as the rest of it was one of those days that makes you realize that you can make plans and then sit back and watch as they all go awry. I had a few clothing items to buy for birthday/Christmas gifts, and wanted to get them bought immediately so that they could be mailed before leaving for Japan. Doug and I had decided to go to the Vacaville outlet mall this morning, have lunch, spend a few hours there and head home mid-afternoon. The trip took longer than usual, as the Bay bridge has been closed down for repairs since Tuesday, so we took the longer "scenic" route, adding perhaps an extra 3/4 hour to the usual fifty minute trip!

Fifteen minutes from Vacaville, Doug felt another of his "spells" coming on, so we pulled over and I drove the last bit to the mall. We decided we would have lunch right away, giving Doug a little time for this to settle down. He was fine for about half an hour, and then he felt another coming on ... but this time it did not leave. In minutes he was very very pale, felt nauseous, and the room was spinning. He didn't eat another bite, didn't want to leave right away, so I got some tea and we sat for perhaps a half hour. We talked about whether it made sense to try to get to a hospital in Vacaville, not sure if there even was one, but decided it made more sense to get back to the SFGH just a few blocks from home, and one of the best trauma hospitals in the States. It's a teaching hospital and we knew there would be good people there to check him out. Two staff members in the restaurant stood on each side of him and guided him to the car.

Doug closed his eyes all the way home, as opening them made him feel woozy, but he was able to tell me just where we were on that trip ... coming to the end of the small main road we took going home, getting on to the 101, on and off the bridge and the exits and on-ramps. It was a little game that kept him occupied and awake.

We drove right to the ER, and staff came out with a wheelchair to get him inside. When I came back after parking the car, he was undressed, on a gurney, and had already started answering questions about why he was there. So much happened after that ... Doug had an EKG, several visits from different doctors, most of them neurologists, had a CT scan done, and when they discovered that there was a self-healed formerly torn carotid artery, they decided to check that out with an MRI. They ruled out a stroke at the back of the head with that test, and were deciding on whether they should go in and redo that carotid artery.

This all took many many hours, as the ER was filled with people lined up in the hallways, where Doug was as well, but his situation was very controlled and not quite so outwardly dramatic as the drunks, the men who had been beaten up or roughed up or had done the roughing up, as well as the woman who signed herself in after trying to commit suicide, and then spent an hour trying to convince every staff member who walked by to give her clothes back to her. They took them after she dressed herself three times, exposing most of her body to whomever happened to be there while she was doing this.

In the middle of the night, things had quieted down enough that we were given a private room. Doug did quite a lot of dozing in between all the medical visitors, but I found it hard to sleep on the hard chair, with no place for my feet, and all the lights blazing.

Doug continued to feel nauseous and light-headed whenever he was asked to stand or move around at all, so the puzzle was yet to be solved as we approached Hallowe'en.

30 October 2009

Hallowe'en Was a Non-Event For Us This Year

I must be honest and admit that the hours from midnight last night until Doug was released from the hospital about 12:30 P.M. ... 21 hours after he went in, are mostly just a blur. Lots happened, and there were certainly interesting situations going on all around us too, but mostly we just waited while various doctors came and went. Doug's last nurse was a sweet Irish gal and she, along with all the other staff members, was just wonderful. One wonders how they can maintain their cool under such dire circumstances, but except for a little eye-rolling and exchanged glances, they do incredibly well with it all.

I checked to see if the car needed to be moved before 9 A.M. and then went home to wash, brush my teeth, and send out an email to everyone letting them know what was going on here. I also called Susan to let her know that unfortunately we would not be out trick-or-treating with them, as was the plan. I even had our costumes out ready to be ironed, our usual clown outfit for Doug and my witch costume. It's the first year we have missed being out with the children since they were old enough to go, so it was a letdown for sure.

At the end of his time there, Doug was finally assured that they were sure there was nothing to keep him in the hospital. They definitely ruled out stroke and heart-related problems, but want him to see his family doctor next week asap, for a baseline visit, and then a follow up CTA scan in two months on the self-healed carotid artery.

It was a lovely day when the nurse wheeled Doug out to the car. Doug was able to get up all the stairs at the house as there were handholds in all the spots he needed them. It was good to be home, and take a hot shower, but we were both tired out from not sleeping last night. So we had lunch, then I read and fell asleep, went upstairs to watch a taped show and fell asleep again. Doug just pretty well stayed on the sofa for most of the day and ... slept!

He was feeling very disoriented and wasn't able to read, or spend more than a few minutes on the computer. We tried watching a movie, and even had popcorn to eat, but Doug was seeing double so that didn't work either.

We hadn't bought candy to give out as we thought we would be wandering around Belvedere Street, so we turned out all the lights. The door bell didn't ring once and there didn't seem to be any children out on our street at all. No doubt more and more children are going to either private parties, or choosing streets where they don't have to climb 38 steps up to the doorbells!

At 10 o'clock we went up to bed, hoping we would manage to get a few solid hours sleep in a row, despite our too-many naps today!

p.s. The next day, Susan sent a photo of them all dressed up in their wonderful costumes!
