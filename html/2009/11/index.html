Dizzy Doug and Chats

Doug and I both slept surprisingly long and well, despite both of us dozing off several times yesterday for long periods of time. Whew! I slept in until 8:45 body time but, as we moved the clocks backward last night, it was 7:45 on the clock.

Doug is feeling much the same as he has been for two days, and after lots of reading on the Web today, it sounds as though this could go on for some time, from several days to several months, and even up to several years in severe cases. So, fingers crossed here for a speedy recovery!

Doug is unable to read easily, but spent a little more time on the computer today than yesterday, so I was lucky enough to get a few photos done for my blog entries, as well as family tree photos too.

I continue to add jobs to my to-do list as I think of them, but managed to cross a few off today too. I am very excited about the upcoming trip to Japan, now eight days away, but am also prepared to cancel it if Doug is not over his imbalance. He is not able to bend over comfortably, certainly could not get out to drive, or maneuver much around on his own, so we shall play this out day by day and see what happens. Doug did lots of reading on the various things it could be, and even found a forty-five minute "exercise" that might get rid of any tiny crystal build-up that might be the culprit too.

I spoke with Susan, Amelia, and Finn today. They all had a good time last night, and Amelia told me she had collected a half basket of candy in her Hallowe'en pumpkin. Finn was still feeling sick and even though he claimed he wanted to talk in actual fact he just was not up to it. Poor wee guy. I may see him in the morning for a few hours, depending on how he is feeling then.

Bryn and I had a chat on IM and it was good to catch up a bit. Riku's piano teacher told Akane that she envies Riku his lovely singing voice. It was good to hear that, as Riku loves to sing, and there are lots of opportunities to do so at school.

Here is another bit of conversation between us.. said Bryn: "He was complaining about the piano stuff in his class ... "Too easy!" He says that he wants a challenge, like "this" (pointing to his music sheets)". I know how he loves to play the piano, although isn't always keen to practice, so it was good to hear that he feels this way.

Bryn went for his second run this morning at 5:30 along the river and said he enjoyed it very much, except for the running. It was a humid clear day there today and warmed up nicely during the day.

Riku wanted to talk to his Grandpa this morning, before going off to school, so they arranged a Skype chat just after Riku finished up his hot cereal. We both had a nice chat with him.Doug had the headphones on, but I put my ear next to Doug's so I could hear Riku, and see him too. (I'm like Mirei and can't resist seeing and talking to him too!) How I miss seeing that little face! I am still getting used to him having his top two adult teeth in. How that changed him.

He had some questions for Doug about his hospital stays and they talked about needles and what else they did to him. He laughed when Doug told him about having to have bits of his "fur" shaved from his chest before they did one of the tests, an EKG.

Riku told me that he liked the Hallowe'en book I sent along with Bryn, and that he was going to Universal Studios tomorrow for the big Hallowe'en event there. He will be one of the UltraMan characters and Mirei a female cartoon figure I have forgotten the name of ... "Peach" or something.

Riku had asked me if I were coming to Japan soon. When I told him perhaps not because I couldn't leave Grandpa alone, he said "Maybe you should get a Grandpa-sitter."! How we laughed!

I am hoping to be there for his birthday on November the 13th, and Bryn called out that they would just have Riku turn seven when I did get there finally. That made Riku laugh. He wisely suggested that Doug use a cane, so Doug went off and got one he owns, and it does make walking easier for him. What a good suggestion!

Mirei was anxious to chat with us too, but we promised we could talk to her after Riku left for school. Later on in the day, we had that chat, and she was her usual bubbly self. They had all been to see a skin specialist, and Mirei told me she now had cream for behind her knee, for her bumps.

I took a snapshot of her too (blurry, but still so good to have), and we said goodbye, but she told me first that she missed me. I miss her too, very much, and do hope I see them both soon.

One of the things I crossed off my list today was the twenty Christmas cards I promised myself to write. They're done!

A Morning Visit with Busy Finn

Susan likes to get her groceries on Monday mornings, and I like to have Finn here with me on those Mondays that it works out for both of us. Last week he went to the same nursery school/preschool group that Amelia went to, so we missed out on that day, but made up for it another day with Mirei and some time at the BADM together as well.

Finn was a little disappointed to hear from his mom that Mirei wouldn't be here to play with, but finally made the decision to come and play on his own. He was all smiles when I opened the door, and he came in and got busy! He wanted to go upstairs first, so up we went, where he played with the race car set, the plastic blocks, the abacus, the little rocking horse decoration, and a stuffed dino toy. He moved so fast through all of these that it was almost impossible to get shots of him! We did sit on the bed and watch a Caillou video together while I fed him some oatmeal. He ate more than I thought he would as he had croup last week and is still suffering from a bad cold. Poor wee guy. He is really stuffed up!

Finn always loves to spend time in the garden, so off we went. He had brought his own little two-wheeler car which he scooted around on, pushing with his feet, but he quickly moved on to one of the plastic cars, swept and gathered up pine needles with me, rang the wind chimes with his broom, and used the toy rake to work in the garden beds themselves.

We were outside for close to an hour, and then it was time for more indoor toys. I'm not sure if I can remember all that he did over the next hour but I will try ... big truck, big basket, Tinker Toys, two different keyboards, snacks, toy kitchen time, small basket, stroller, push toy, and finally some time sitting at the table with Play-Doh. Finn likes most to poke sticks into the dough, and at one point he was very happy when I told him he made a tree! I managed to get that photo, as he was quite pleased with it.

Just fifteen minutes before Susan arrived Finn started to yawn and rub his eyes. He was ready for his nap for certain, and did very well despite being so sick. Susan came in bearing the most beautiful bouquet of flowers, a big lemon tart (one of my favourite desserts, but then Susan knows that), and the groceries she had kindly picked up for us at Trader Joe's.

Susan emailed later to tell me that Finn was asleep within minutes of leaving here, and slept until it was time to pick Amelia up from school at 2:40.

Doug is still feeling disoriented and slightly nauseous, but spent quite a bit of time on the computer doing some research into inner ear problems. It does seem that this is a matter of waiting to get over it, not an easy thing for anyone to do.

I did phone and managed to get him an appointment with his "primary care physician", someone he hasn't seen since we lost our health insurance in 2001. The good news is that that medical group accepts Medicare, and we are very happy that we don't have to go searching for new doctors.

Bryn had gone to the doctor today thinking he might have the dreaded H1N1 virus that is making the rounds, but luckily did not have the high fever associated with it. Phew! Kelly is still suffering with a very bad cough and not feeling any better after three weeks of it. She has missed more school than she ever dreamed she would, and misses her kindergartners.

Riku and Mirei had been looking forward to going to Universal Studios, dressed up in the Hallowe'en costumes Akane had ordered for them, but Bryn wasn't up to going to Osaka with them, so now they have some play costumes and they will find some other special event to attend at some point.

Bryn went down and set up Akane's computer with Skype so I could have a little chat with the children. I got to speak to both of them and Mirei gave Riku some time to chat on his own. They were busy watching something on TV so we didn't talk long, but long enough to make me happy. I'm now having doubts as to whether or not I will be able to go to Japan on Monday or even the Monday after that. I was so hoping to be there for Riku's birthday, but things happen and change the best laid plans, and this is one of those times. We shall continue to play this out day by day.

Ear/Nose/Throat Doc', and Skype Chats

Doug had an appointment with the specialist this morning at 10 A.M. We were hoping perhaps to confirm that Doug did indeed have an inner ear problem, but perhaps with an idea of which of the three types it might be. The visit lasted almost an hour and a half, and Doug was thoroughly examined; not just his ears but with a tube up behind his sinuses, and with a series of tests to check out hearing, etc. At the end of the appointment, Doug was told that perhaps it was not an inner ear problem but neurological after all. The next step is a visit to a clinic where they will submit Doug to a series of tests that have his eye movements being measured while they spin him and shake him 'round a bit. Despite a waiting list, Doug has been lucky enough to get an appointment in just three days in San Raphael, for which he is very grateful because the waiting is not something he wants more of.

My friend Judith called this morning before we left, and since it has been months since we have been in touch, we decided to finish up later on. Doug and I had a few errands to run after the appointment too. He can get around with his cane, as long as we are not moving too quickly and I'm sure being out is a good change for him too.

That was it ... laundry, lots of paper sorting, time with my feet up going through several more magazines, but not much emailing, as I spent a lot of time on family tree in between jobs, trying to clear away some of that paper work! All in all, a productive day, although I realized as soon as it got dark, that I hadn't been out for my walk! It just disappeared in a flurry of chats today! I will make up for it tomorrow for certain.

I snapped a few photos of Riku and Mirei and of her with her dad, but they shall have to wait to get posted until Doug gets home.

22 November 2009

Happy (American) Thanksgiving ... and Doug Arrives Home!

I woke at 5 this morning and had trouble nodding off again, so turned the light on and read for an hour, putting my very exciting book, "Searching for Nouf", down just one chapter before the end! However, I knew I needed the extra sleep and was very happy to awaken at 7:45!

I hurried downstairs to turn the heat on, and shower, as I thought Amelia might be coming over for a visit with me this morning. Finn will be busy with his Spanish sitter as is usual on a Thursday, and I knew Kevin and Susan would be busy making their big Thanksgiving dinner. However, no call came and I spent lots of time with my siblings exchanging funny childhood memories via email.

I wrapped some gifts this morning and got one lot ready for mailing tomorrow, so now my counter looks that much tidier. I answered emails, one to a dear friend, Carol, whose mother passed away yesterday; her mom had a serious stroke two days ago but this was still unexpected so soon.

I picked Doug up at the airport at 3 P.M. and we went to Safeway for some produce before heading home. I had decided this morning that we would have a Thanksgiving dinner tonight.I had a delicious "Celebration Roast" in the freezer, a vegetarian entree we both had tried previously and liked a lot, but I needed bread for stuffing, and a turnip, for our feast.

Back at home, I got busy making Mom's stuffing, chatting with Doug, and catching up on all our news. This was always a special day for Uncle Frank, who is an American, so I called him and was happy to hear that By' and Neal were both in visiting with him. They were ready to leave, though, so we hung up for their goodbyes, and then Uncle Frank called me back. Glenn called in the two or three minutes between our calls, so it was good to hear that lots of people were thinking about him today.

While I peeled, chopped, sauteed, grated, and stirred, Uncle Frank and I chatted about all sorts of things ... poetry, his table-mates at the retirement home, photos of the children I had sent on, the video of Riku skateboarding, his physical therapist and fitness routine — he's so happy with them both — and relatives in England. We were on the phone for well over an hour and when we said goodbye dinner was ready to go on the table!

Doug was delighted, as he hadn't expected a special dinner at all. This is not a typical dinner for us at all, and Doug always comments on these "real" dinners as he says ... meaning the kind we grew up with ... an entree (the sage flavoured "roast"), at least extra two veggies (Mom's traditional carrots and turnips mashed together with butter), plus mashed potatoes (I now use a combination of sweet and white), cranberry sauce, and gravy.

I realized once we got home that I had forgotten to buy the Brussel sprouts, so may pick those up tomorrow and serve it when we have hot "turkey" sandwiches for tomorrow's dinner. It was very very tasty!

Bryn sent me a voice recording tonight from his IPhone, another lovely surprise after receiving his video of Riku last night. He had just taken Riku to his piano lesson on his bike; they were a bit rushed, it is a longish way from their home, some of it uphill, and it was gorgeous with a hot sun shining (65ºF). Bryn was hot and tired when they arrived and so decided to stay outside while Riku had his lesson. He tested his newly discovered recording abilities and taped a chatty little message to me; I could hear birds singing in the background! Not only did I hear Bryn's voice very clearly, but I get to keep the recording, as I do the video of Riku, and play them whenever I like. Can't wait to get more of these and with Riku and Mirei's voices too! Not too much later a lovely photo of Riku came in, surrounded by beautiful trees in autumn colours, taken on their way home for the lesson.

Doug processed some blog photos for me from the last few days, but was feeling quite disoriented and his vision was not right. He did tell me on the way home today that he was not noticing any changes at all, despite time and the exercises he has been doing, so is even more discouraged right now. I hope tomorrow is better for him.

(I just looked at the photo of my dinner, and yup, there is a place there for those missing Brussels sprouts! I shall have to take another shot of dinner tomorrow night!)

I'm off to finish that last chapter, and then I get to choose a new book!

26 November 2009

Small Blessings

This did not start out to be my day. I woke at 4 A.M. and could not get back to sleep, no matter which way I turned or how many relaxation exercises I tried. I didn't want to disturb Doug by turning on even my tiny flashlight as he hadn't been feeling well at bedtime last night.

I gave up at 5:30, and came downstairs with two bed buddies in hand, intending to crawl into bed downstairs in the guest room with newly warmed "buddies". While I waited for them to heat, I checked my email and decided to write to a friend I hadn't written to for far too long. It always takes me a while when responding to an older email as there is lots of catching up to do.

At 6:30, after answering two more, I had almost convinced myself to just stay up. Common sense prevailed though, as I know that with just four hours sleep I would not get much accomplished anyway. So, I tucked in, read for perhaps five minutes and woke at 8:15. Yahoo!

For various reasons it was an emotional day for me. I talked to two of my sisters, took a very very long hot shower, and really pampered myself with lots of lotion and a lovely lavender rub on the back of my neck and earlobes and felt much much better.

I am having worrisome ... not pain, exactly, but a stretching feel on the left side of my abdomen, which is just huge right now and is just not flat lately, even in the mornings before I have had any wheat. If I stretch or pull to the side I can feel something is not quite right, and yet I cannot feel a lump or anything like the hernia I have thought it might be. So, that is niggling in the back of my mind as I am constantly aware of it. Until I can get a colonoscopy done I won't be feeling good about any of this.

I thought we might head out to do some shopping today, but instead was happy to just stay put. We did go out though to buy those Brussels sprouts we didn't have with our dinner last night, and I mailed the second part of UF's birthday gift to him, as well as a package for the children in Japan.

We came back to find two photos from Bryn waiting; they were all at a clinic to get the Swine flu shot for the children and the regular flu shot for the adults. The photos were of Riku and Mirei in a play area, and the second showed the well built house they had made together.

I was quite sure that Riku would be okay getting his shot, and he was, and I was equally sure that Mirei would not be, and she wasn't. I was very happy to be able to talk to her about it all via Skype soon after we got home. Mirei wanted to show me where she had her two needles... they must have had both shots... so she told me to "just wait right there!" and went off to pull off her first layer. I had seen part of her pretty undershirt and she was very happy to show it to me in full. Lovely. When I asked her if she cried, she said she screamed "but I was only fooling around." Right. We chatted a little bit and Riku popped his head around her shoulder to say hello too. We had to hang up too soon, as it was time for their lunch and Riku's tutor was there, but my heart lifted after that call; I got some sweet pics of them too, as well as some funny tongue shots from the two of them! Blessings for sure...

They had hoped to get one of the only shipment of Christmas trees today, sold at IKEA, but they had sold out already when Akane called. Now they have to look at what other options they have but I'm sure they will come up with something. Wouldn't it be nice if IKEA realized that they could sell lots more and bring in more? They were only charging $20 per tree, although they were not sure what size they were.

Doug and I had delicious leftovers tonight, but with the perfectly cooked Brussels sprouts we missed out on last night. Still to come... potato cakes with an egg for a late breakfast tomorrow, and hot turkey sandwiches for another day.

Doug and I watched a taped "Gray's Anatomy" episode later on with a big plate of fresh fruit for dessert... half of the wonderful Honey Crisp apples we just recently discovered, a Mandarin orange each and lots of crisp crunchy red grapes.

I've been working on emails and photos since then, and talked to Sheldon later in the evening as well, a nice surprise call from him.

No "now" photo from Susan's iPhone in two days. I wrote to her tonight telling her I missed them, and I had a newsy email back from her; busy two days for her, of course, with lots of plans, but we made tentative arrangements for the children to come over, perhaps on Sunday.

I've been listening to Susan Boyle's just-released DVD this evening and have so enjoyed it; "Wild Horses" is so beautifully sung.

Just before bedtime Bryn sent me a sweet photo of Mirei taking his drink "order"; she told him she didn't have any beer, but had "tea with apple juice'. A little later I received a photo of a "real princess"... a quick change of lifestyle for our used-to-be-waitress!

Bedtime. I won't be reading long tonight.

28 November 2009

Dickens Christmas Faire

Susan kindly gave us two free tickets to the annual "Dickens Christmas Faire", held in the Cow Palace in San Francisco. This was a wonderful start to the Christmas season.

Doug and I have been twice before several years ago, and took Riku with us both times. The first year there was a wonderful display of wooden parts for castle building and Riku had a great time. He also chose a long glass beaded ornament to hang on the Christmas tree. The second time we went he quite enjoyed listening to the rousing music and watching the dancers on stage. They had a wonderful Victorian Santa there too, who was of course a huge hit, especially since he was a really gentle, soft-spoken Santa.

This huge venue is done up as Victorian streets, complete with shops, cafes where you can buy scones and tea, restaurants for fish and chips, and pubs and bars for cider and beer, etc. The very best part of all are the 3000 actors (though not all there at the same time) walking around in period clothing, keeping in character with their various British accents, from the highbrow to the scruffiest accent imaginable. If you are walking by and hear a couple talking they are doing so in their accents, discussing matters of the "day" way back when. We stopped to watch a foursome of young people playing a game; it was a delight to listen to them talk in their chosen dialects, and as we approached one of the gals called out to us "e's cheatin' Ma'm, 'e is!" and the other chorused in to second it. It's just the most fun!

Because we were on our own, we sat and watched a two-hour range of entertainment at "Big Sal's" saloon. There was singing, dancing, and bawdy plays, and we got to join in and sing along too at times. The entire cast was obviously having a grand ol' time, as were we. We were there for over three hours; there wasn't much of a crowd when we arrived later in the day on the first weekend, and so the actors far outnumbered us this time.

Later that evening Bryn sent a lovely video of the children tossing rocks into a stream at the back of a café they had been to. It was Sunday afternoon for them, and such a treat for me to see them and hear their voices at the end of my day.
