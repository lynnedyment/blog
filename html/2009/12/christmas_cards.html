Christmas Cards

Today Doug and I finished up the last of our Christmas cards. For all but two of our forty-four years together, we have sat down together to write and address our cards. Doug mentioned tonight that we may well be in the last generation that has this ritual, as so many people now either don't bother with cards at all, or send E-cards out instead. I certainly will miss the cards spread out along the mantle when no one bothers any more.

So, continuing on with our tradition, today we put on the Christmas music in the background, spread out all the cards we have, and name by name go down the list, choose the appropriate card, I write the card, and Doug writes the address. When it comes to a friend who is more Doug's friend that mine, then he will often write the card, and do the envelope, while I write longer notes in a few of the others.

I had done some cards on my own when Doug was in B.C. as I didn't want to be rushing through them once he got home. He also was having trouble with close work when he left, so it made sense for me to go through the list and take care of any requiring an annual update, generally to those in the family who are without computers.

It took us only an hour and a half to finish up, and we mailed off the first lot for overseas and to Vancouver Island. Mid-week we will send off the Canadian cards and then the U.S. shortly after that.

6 December 2009
