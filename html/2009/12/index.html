A Visit with Susan and Finn,

I had offered to have Finn this morning and was expecting to hear from Susan earlier, but Finn had decided he wanted to stay home and watch a rented movie instead. Susan was sure that wouldn't last long, but I didn't hear anything more.

And then, about noon, the door bell rang and Finn had his nose pushed against the glass, calling out "Hi Nana!" He and Susan had gone out, stopped to pick up some yummy food for us to eat together. There was a vegetarian croque monsieur (with mushrooms, sun-dried tomatoes, and a really earthy cheese), a high and light veggie quiche, a quiche Lorraine for Susan and Finn, and a lemon tart for dessert. Yum!

Finn, however, could not be talked into having a bite of any of the quiche, or the pistachios Susan opened for him (usually a sure thing), or the grapes I offered that he rarely refuses. He had his mind set on using the hose, but it was cold outside, Finn was coughing, and Susan hadn't planned to stay long as Finn needed his nap. He was furious with her for not giving in, and finally after much patience on Susan's part, it was obvious that he really just needed to sleep.

They went off, with Finn waving goodbye from the back seat of the car. Less than a half hour later Susan sent me a sweet photo of sleeping Finn. It's an interesting photo of him, as I can so clearly see what he might look like as a young teenager in his face.

About an hour later Doug left to go to a double feature movie. My five or so hours alone swept by too quickly, but I enjoyed the time and the evening with Doug too. We didn't watch a DVD tonight as Doug was movied out, but I got lots of computer related work done while I had Doug here with me.

Farmer's Market, a Letter & Drawing, and a Movie

We stopped in for burritos at "Lucinda's", a spot that both Doug and Bryn are very fond of. Doug and I went back to the nearby mall at Corte Madera, and strolled along having samples of all the wonderful produce currently in season. We bought all of the above, as well as some of the freshest Medjol dates we have ever had, grown in the desert in California. Oh, and we had a spoonful of an incredibly good creme brulée. The fellow, who owns his own company, works out of San Francisco. We tried the marshmallow version and it was so good, we just had to buy one. How we will share this fairly remains to be seen.

He wrote a letter telling me how much he was enjoying his skateboard, and sent along a wonderful drawing of us having drinks together ... tea for me, and a cold drink for Riku, with a lovely big house nearby with wings, perhaps, a water fountain in the garden and lots of beautiful red and yellow flowers nearby. I would not want to lose any of them, although the paper was a little too wide for our scanner so the ones along the bottom edge got cut off. :(

Riku wrote the address himself and the very long return address too, except for the last line. And of course he decorated the back of the envelope so very nicely for me too.

There were lots of emails in from Tessa and Caillie today; these two girls are keeping me busy but I'm happy to hear that they are also in touch with each other too.

Doug had won two free tickets to the latest and as yet-unreleased Robert DeNiro movie for tonight, but since it is first come-first seated, we had to leave the house at 5:30. It felt rushed to me, as we hadn't been home that long and I start to wind down about that time anyway. However, we found a good parking spot, I sat on the floor of the lobby to read for an hour, and we both thoroughly enjoyed the movie, called "Everyone is Fine". It wasn't an upbeat movie, as I had expected it to be, but the acting was wonderful, and it was a very touching film.

Home to emails from both Kelly and Gregg, wanting to know whether they could go ahead with the new furnace. Gregg leaves tomorrow for twelve days in Fiji, on a business trip, and Kelly will have to deal with being there for the installation on her own.

Cold cold night; even Doug was happy with the bed buddies warming the bed up so quickly!

5 December 2009

Christmas Cards

Today Doug and I finished up the last of our Christmas cards. For all but two of our forty-four years together, we have sat down together to write and address our cards. Doug mentioned tonight that we may well be in the last generation that has this ritual, as so many people now either don't bother with cards at all, or send E-cards out instead. I certainly will miss the cards spread out along the mantle when no one bothers any more.

So, continuing on with our tradition, today we put on the Christmas music in the background, spread out all the cards we have, and name by name go down the list, choose the appropriate card, I write the card, and Doug writes the address. When it comes to a friend who is more Doug's friend that mine, then he will often write the card, and do the envelope, while I write longer notes in a few of the others.

I had done some cards on my own when Doug was in B.C. as I didn't want to be rushing through them once he got home. He also was having trouble with close work when he left, so it made sense for me to go through the list and take care of any requiring an annual update, generally to those in the family who are without computers.

It took us only an hour and a half to finish up, and we mailed off the first lot for overseas and to Vancouver Island. Mid-week we will send off the Canadian cards and then the U.S. shortly after that.

Again though, his therapist is very pleased with his progression, and commented today on how fast he moved along out of the office to practice walking as straight as he could in the long hallway outside. It Rained and It Poured... and Happy 90th Birthday to Uncle Frank!

Uncle Frank turned ninety today. It's been an interesting past two weeks with him, as several times during that period he had mentioned to me that his big day was coming, or that it was only so many days until the "Big 90!". For all the years that Uncle Frank has been in my life, and that has been since I was ten months old, he has never discussed his age with me, or with anyone else. One of his sons had no idea what year he was born, and daren't ask either. I did know that, but certainly never discussed it with him, as he felt his age was a private matter and no one else should feel they had the right to know. But, I think he is very proud of having reached this day, and why not? It's a day to celebrate for sure! He told me two days before his birthday that was was "proud of my first 90 years" and was looking forward to the next ones. His own Uncle Frank lived to be 101, and I'm betting my money on this one to do the same.

What a blustery day this turned out to be! The sky was heavy with black clouds for most of the day, and once in a while the sun shone through, all looked well, and then within fifteen minutes we couldn't see out the front window across the street. This went on several times over the course of the day and the evening too.

I typed away with Bryn for a while this morning, as he was at Mirei's French school helping to set up for her Christmas party there. He sent a short video of the children running around in the big auditorium beforehand, and we spotted Akane, her mom, and her sister too, who had arrived last night at 10 P.M. so they could attend the concert.

Bryn had surprised us last night with an email with "gift money" for us. He wanted to treat us to his favourite spot for "chips"... aka "French fries", before it closes for good on the 19th of this month. Doug suggested that we have lunch there, and then do a run to the post office, so off we went. I was driving and it was certainly raining hard, but only when we finally parked the car, a long block and a half away, did the skies just open up and pour down on us. There were massive puddles everywhere, we had to hop over wide stretches in the sidewalks where the water was inches deep, and by the time we reached the front door of "South" we were both soaked, despite our rain jackets!

And to add insult to injury, the restaurant is only open for dinner on the weekends! We stood dripping for a minute before heading off into the deluge again. My fabric purse was soaked all the way through, and both of us were drenched from the hems of our jackets down. Doug got a soaker, and I just felt wet and chilly.

We had to go to the bank, but I knew the underground parking area would be jam-packed, and of course it was ... worse than I have ever seen it before. Water was dripping down from the roof above, horns were honking as people vied for spots, and four of the usual spots were so deep in water that no one could park there. I lucked out and found a spot finally, and by the time we got up to ground level, there was the sun shining through again! Exciting times!

Bryn had sent enough money that we could also splurge on his favourite falafil (as the restaurant "Old Jerusalem" spells it), so off we went to do a pick-up. Doug wasn't sure about the eggplant that is part of their falafil sandwich, and I was surprised to see them served like a very long burrito ... a first for us, but we took them home, heated them up, and thoroughly enjoyed them ... sitting upstairs with the storm raging 'round us, watching as Billy Connolly toured around England and Wales, completely bypassing Liverpool! For heaven's sake! How could he? Again though, after seeing again how beautiful it all is, we are both just keen to get back to the British Isles and spend a long while there.

Thank you, Bryn! I'm looking forward to those chips at Monday lunch.

p.s. I did call Uncle Frank this evening but Glenn and Wendy were visiting with him, he had been out for lunch with his sons, his daughter-in-law and two of his three grandchildren, and also had a visit with Sheldon and Donna after dinner. More is coming up tomorrow for him. He sounded very pleased with his day!
