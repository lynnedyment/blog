Cabbages and Flower Wreaths, and Acquaintances Old and New

What a lovely day this was! The sun was shining, the air was warm, and there was a slight breeze. Riku and I left for school five minutes earlier than usual, at 8 A.M., and so I had time to stop to take a photo of the very friendly neighbour on the next block who never fails to say good morning to us, with a big smile, and a slight bow. And there are always an extra few words for Riku as well.

Coming home, I decided to take a new street, and passed by one of the many commercial gardens in this area. I saw a man cutting lovely big cabbages, and stopped to have a look at them, and at the quickly-filling pea pods too. We had exchanged greetings and I walked on, but he stopped me with a "one moment" in English. He proceeded to cut a lovely big cabbage for me! I was so very pleased and thanked him so much, and asked if I could take his photo. He was a little shy about it, but nodded yes. I do like to have these memories of the friendly people I meet!

Akane went to Osaka/Sakai on her own today, via bus, train, train, subway, and bus I believe. Mirei and I set off at 10:30 to walk to the grocery store for those groceries we tried to get yesterday, but she had spotted what we thought might be a playground on the way home from that trip. I am so glad she had mentioned it, as we stopped by there first and met a "Baba" and her three-year-old grandson there. He and Mirei played so well together, and the grandmother was fun too, playing "baseball" with them both, and then weaving a lovely head wreath of blossoms for Mirei. The children picked the flowers for them, and followed each other around the big field exploring. We hope to meet up again tomorrow.

And once again today, we passed another "regular" on our walks, an older woman who is blind in one eye. It's permanently closed, and as we stopped to chat with her a bit as she was resting against a wall, Mirei asked her lots of questions. but all in Japanese so I have no idea what they said to each other.

We continued on, bought our groceries, and a tired little girl after an hour and a half of physical activity unpacked the groceries from her tiny bag, looking every bit the little housewife.

Our afternoon was busy with various activities, and Mirei snacked, and snacked, and snacked. She must be going through a growth spurt, as the calories going into this little body are huge! We went out to see if we could see Riku coming, waited a few minutes, and were greeted with his big smile. They had a snack together, then played for a while, before Riku ran over to invite Yuu-kun to play for an hour.

I put together a very quick dinner for the children, from various bits and pieces in the fridge, and after their tub Riku especially ate so much and so well. The usual reading/spelling followed, and Riku drew a picture of this house for Skylar, his "special" friend at Starr King in San Francisco.

Akane arrived home in time to say goodnight to Riku, although Mirei was fast asleep by then! Her daddy tucked a very tired little girl in at 7:15.

That's it folks!

Riku did his spelling session with Bryn tonight, which beat last night's record of 25 words in less than four minutes! Mirei has been dressing up in her princess dresses a lot over the past couple of days, and this morning Riku decided to join her. It is the funniest thing, as he makes quite a cute girl, and it's just hilarious to see him wearing a skirt, or lovely dress, and tiara, plus whatever bracelets, rings, and necklaces Mirei decides to share with him. I missed the evening photo but Riku promised he would get dressed up again for me soon. Mirei liked her plastic dangling earrings — and some of those necklaces — so much that she decided to wear them in the tub tonight too.

Riku was asleep at 7:40, Mirei not too much later, and Akane and Bryn went out for a late dinner at a Korean barbecue place, followed by an hour in a very smoky karaoke bar, with really bad videos of the songs being sung.

Visitors for the Children, a Walk Along the River, and a Quiet Evening Home Alone

It's "Golden Week", a national holiday here so Riku has the next three days off school. It was such a treat to have both children join me in bed for cuddles this morning, three days in a row. Bryn told me not long after we finally emerged from my room, that Akane's mother and sister would be visiting today, arriving some time mid-morning.

While they got breakfast going for the children, I took a really long shower and then soaked in the tub for a while, just relaxing. It's the first time I have tubbed this trip, being a shower kind of gal, but it was nice to sink down in that big stainless steel tub.

Knowing the day was going to be busy, Riku got his reading and spelling out of the way early today, and Mirei and I passed some time in my room, looking at a funny video her cousin Bryn had made, and then of course we went on to watch lots of others of all the children after that.

Ayako and Mrs. Goda arrived about 10:15, about ten minutes after Riku's piano practice had started. There was lots of excitement, as their grandmother had arrived with lots of food treats, which both children wanted to sample right away, and some small gifts for them as well. I said hello and then left them to have some time together. Mirei was happy to have their full attention while Riku finished playing, and then he too was busy visiting, and eating.

I took the time to myself to read quietly in my room. Propped up with two pillows on my futon on the floor, windows open to a nice breeze, I was nice and cozy and enjoyed getting something done too. At 11:30, they all headed out for lunch and shopping, so at noon I made Bryn and me a hot lunch of pasta with fresh tomatoes, cheese, and some carrot sticks. Bryn went right back to work and I spent two plus hours on the computer emailing and blogging.

By then I was ready to stretch my legs, so left the house at 3:30, walked down to the river and along that lovely path for a long time. There were lots of people out, couples walking, children in the little play areas, joggers, elderly men and women well covered in hats and long sleeves, and cyclists. There were lots of benches scattered about, and I sat to read for a bit, although really spent more time watching all the action.

As I was sitting, I was attempting to open a new bottle of water I bought at the 7/11 on the way down. I have the worst times with those lids for some reason, and a man walking by happened to notice me struggling with it. He came over to offer help, which I gladly accepted, and then we "chatted" for about close to an hour or more.

This was such an interesting encounter, as of course I speak about five words of Japanese (and don't always get those words pronounced correctly!), but it was perhaps three more words than this fellow knew in English. Well, not quite but more of that later. Despite the lack of a common language, we did manage to communicate over the course of that time, that I was born in England, raised in Canada, flew to Osaka from San Francisco, and then took a train to Kyoto. And yes, I was on my own, visiting my son (not sure this was understood), and had two young grandchildren. He was born and raised here in Kyoto, doesn't have a passport, has never left Japan, and has four grandchildren.

I believe he was attempting to ask me out, as he asked if I liked sushi. Just try explaining to someone in another language that one doesn't eat meat (and yes, I know that not all sushi is meat or fish related, but I assumed in his case it was what he meant). And yes, embarrassingly, as Bryn later told me it was, I moo-ed and baa-ed and even tried to get some young teen students there to help us out. So, no word for "no meat" but I said "no" to "steak?" with sawing motion, "yes" to spaghetti, "yes" to wine, and another "yes" to ice cream. But it was obvious we had said all there was to be said, and so it was "sayonara" after that. It was a very pleasant encounter and I spoke with another set of three young students, all thirteen, and happy to practice their English with me.

I was back home before Akane, Ayako, and the children, as they had dropped Mrs. Goda off at the train station for her trip back to Osaka, and then fed the children dinner. They didn't arrive home until almost 7 o'clock so it was a short evening at home for them: baths, dessert, and bed late at 8:15. Both asleep very quickly after such a busy day out and about for all those hours. And they came home with a flat screen TV (for the tatami room when Mrs. Goda visits), and a bigger one for the living room on order, gifts from Mrs. Goda.

Once the children were asleep, Akane, Ayako, and Bryn went out for dinner. I had some computer time. a bonus catch-up day for me today, and they were back at 10. Akane and Ayako went off for a walk, and I headed off to bed at 10:30 tonight, tired after a long day, and ready for tomorrow!

Wednesday ... Felt Like Sunday, and Then Came the Big Hole!

It felt like Sunday today, because Riku just had five days off, and tomorrow he returns to school. He's had his Baba here for a day, and Auntie Ayako for two extra days, so the time no doubt went by quickly for him.

Today we had a very early start, with Riku awake at 5 A.M.! He agreed to try to get back to sleep, but a half hour later I suggested he get a book and read for a bit. He came back with paper and pencil and sat beside me drawing quietly. The book came next and he had his banana while he read. We got up at 6:30, and met Bryn in the kitchen; everyone else was still sleeping, including Mirei, who finally woke after 7, very unusual for her these days. Riku, in the meantime, had kept busy and very quiet doing some cutting and pasting to make his own face, which he changed into his dad with the addition of eyebrows!

After breakfast the children played happily together in their bedroom for the longest time. Riku was helping Mirei clear her bed of all the toys, games, stuffed animals, and collection of myriad bags, purses, hair accessories, and doo-dads! Her part of the room reminds me of Caillie's: every single object, no matter how tiny or what it's made of — plastic, paper, cellophane — is precious, and must not go in the garbage!

Riku did his piano while Mirei had some time alone with her aunt upstairs. Bryn is, of course, working, as it is Tuesday in North America.

Late in the morning, Ayako and Akane went out to run an errand and I suggested they have lunch out together, as Ayako is leaving this afternoon at 2. I made up some fried rice for lunch, and heated up the cabbage dish leftovers to go with it. Bryn joined us for lunch today, and I had a tiny bit of pasta, also a leftover.

The plan was for me to take the children to the playground for a long stretch for all of us, but both of them were reluctant to go out. They were playing dress-up in their room and Riku happily co-operated with Mirei, as she made suggestions and helped him with it all, and then paraded downstairs to show him off to me. It was so funny and such a great rainy day activity for them.

Mirei walked around for most of that time with her "packback" loaded onto her shoulders, and usually two extra bags as well in her hands, all set with extra scarves and accessories to add to Riku's outfit. What a girl she is! I wouldn't be at all surprised if one day this girl walks miles on treks without missing a beat, volunteering to carry everyone else's bags!

I again suggested that we all put on our raincoats, take our umbrellas and go to the playground, but had no takers. Eventually though they decided to put on their rain gear and go outside to look for pill bugs in the front garden area. About fifteen minutes later, they started arguing about some little tool that belonged to Mirei; Bryn asked me to have them both come in the house. We had been expecting Akane and Ayako home a half hour before, to say goodbye to the children, so it was time for a change anyway, and boy, did we get it!

Unbeknown to me, Riku had lifted a very heavy iron manhole cover off a sewage access hole, about four feet deep. (A few days later I asked him how he managed to move it, as it was really heavy. His answer ... he lifted it with some difficulty, but then kicked it off the rest of the way with his shoe.) Riku came into the house right away, but I went out for Mirei, and stepped right into that hole with my left foot and leg! Thank goodness it was me, and not Mirei or Riku, was all I could think later on!

What a shock this was, and what a very painful experience! It was all a blur, and I am not sure how I got out either, but was badly scraped along the top left side of my thigh, right down to my shin bone and ankle, something I didn't know until later. But, what really hurt was my back. I lay on my side moaning in agony; both children thought I was just pretending, and were laughing at my dramatic sounds! However, when I told them to tell their Dad it was an emergency, that changed fast; they both looked very worried. Riku went running. Mirei stayed beside me, patting me, and telling me it would be okay.

Lots happened: Bryn sent Riku to his room, where he kept calling down to me from his bedroom window, asking how I was, Mirei was so concerned and sweet, staying right beside me, while Bryn was asking if he could help me up, what he could do, etc. But there was nothing for a good fifteen minutes, as I just could not imagine trying to get up. Finally, he was able to get me into a chair which he brought outside, and things went a little blank and blurry. The cars and roads and trees were all silvery and disappearing, and I felt so dizzy, but that went away after perhaps ten minutes. Finally, Bryn supported me while I hobbled into the house, and sat on the sofa, still moaning and hating to do so in front of the children.

Riku was allowed downsatirs, and stood crying beside me. Poor guy! I asked if he would read me a story, hoping it would distract him and me, but he cried saying he couldn't and didn't want me to die. I felt awful for Riku; he has seen me hurt once before and that was his concern then too. I reassured him I would be fine in a few days, and Mirei asked why I kept saying "shoot". :) It wasn't what I wanted to say, believe me! Mirei got a wet tissue and wiped the blood off and cleaned a small cut along the base of my thumb; in Mirei's mind that was my biggest injury. Blood!

Akane and Ayako got home about 2:15, about a half hour after it happened, and Ayako suggested that I get to a hospital even if I felt that nothing was broken. By this time I was just feeling awful and thinking some pain medication would help, if nothing else! After Ayako made a few phone calls, to a few facilities I gather, off we went, arriving at the hospital at 4 P.M. Ayako left us to continue on home to Osaka; she had been so kind, and I appreciated her going out of her way to seek help for me.

I was hurting a lot; I couldn't sit, and I knew I wouldn't be able to stand for long, and the fifteen minutes to get into an examining room seemed forever. It is a big facility, with lots of chairs and sliding office doors where the patients are seen, and it was almost empty from what we could see. However, there may well have been a patient behind each door! It was all very quiet and still.

Akane was there as my translator, and explained what had gone on. The doctor asked a few more questions and then a nurse accompanied us to the X-ray department a few floors up. A Friendly Neighbour, More Friendly Faces, and a Lovely Walk to Riku's Piano Lesson

Oh, I was one sore woman today! Standing in one spot is what I find the hardest thing to do, so the little bit of it I did last night did me in. I felt fine while I was doing it, but getting up in the night was almost as bad as it was the second or third night. Even after my usual long hot shower, I just hurt everywhere. Arggh! I will be happy when I can just bounce out of bed again, and not have to rely on the children helping me put my socks on. It is sweet, though, that they are both happy to put on one sock each to help me out!

Riku and I left at 8 for school today, and once he was in his classroom, I spotted a young boy with a marvelous bright green bug on his hand. I admired it, went back in to get Riku, and soon we had a big group of children gathered 'round to see it as well. I let it crawl on me, as Riku was afraid it was poisonous. Someone at school has been telling him that most of the bugs they see are poisonous and he believes them! I am determined to prove them wrong.I think the children were very impressed with my bravery!

I have never spoken to him, as he is almost always crouching, but I did sneak a photo of him from the back one day. Today his neighbour, a man, was coming out of his door, but I was on the street, and moving fast enough that I didn't have to try to decide whether to speak to him or not. Last weekend I was out walking with the children, and a man passed by us, greeting the children by name! I had no idea who he was, and neither did the children, but later in the week, the same fellow passed by again, greeting me warmly. So, we walked slowly to school, and met Riku, along with lots of children who surrounded us on our way out of the grounds.

It is just a lovely walk to where Riku's piano teacher lives, in amongst the hills behind the school. The road is winding, and the views are just lovely all the way along up to the forested part of the area. When there is time, Akane and Riku usually stop to have his snack on the grounds of a small shrine, but today, since he is now in school longer, we had time for just a quick look, and a few photos too, of course. He snacked on apple slices on the way.

Bryn and I sat in the small room behind Riku and the teacher at the piano, and of course I took lots of video and a few photos during practice. A Drizzly, Grey Day, and My Last in Kyoto for Many Moons

Riku slept in until 6:45 A.M. today, waking when he heard Mirei running about upstairs. Mirei was very fond of the lantern that really lights up, and was delighted that my room was so dark with the metal shades still pulled across. Riku liked the (plastic) "Swiss Army" knife, and Mirei used it to "clean" my fingernails!

Mirei insisted on taking her umbrella, even though it was barely misting at that stage, but I was without a hood on my jacket and was very happy to carry it for her when the rain came down more seriously! Kyoto to San Francisco

I had a very restless last night, awake every hour or two, off and on throughout the entire night. Each time I felt as though three or four hours had passed, only to find a mere hour had. I had a lot on my mind ... leaving the children for so long, how the flight would go when I wouldn't be able to move much to get comfortable, how many pain tablets I had left, whether I should get up and take two right away, but most of it was upset, hating the thought of not seeing these children for six months. They will have changed so much by then, and I know how much I will be missing. I'm hoping I sleep on this flight, not something I usually do, but today it would be bliss.

Riku woke at 6:15, after hearing Mirei upstairs. He just bounces out of bed, but there is always a ten-minute coming 'round period too, when he is still slumbering, and wraps his arm around the back of my neck. Tonight he will be back upstairs, sleeping in his own bed, and being a big boy again.

I put the kettle on for Akane's and Bryn's tea, made the fruit plates for the children, one for myself today too, and showered, letting the hot water relax those tight muscles in my back. Then I sat with the children as they ate their breakfast; Mirei had two fried eggs this morning with her toast and ate every bite of it. Riku had his usual one, plus cereal with blueberries, and got to play with a funny little recording device. He and I both spoke sentences one at a time, and then he howled with laughter each time we heard them played back, as our voices were somewhat distorted and often interrupted the previous sentence.

Mirei helped me with my socks for the very last time today, even though I was attempting to put them on myself. She insisted, and told me that Grandpa could help me when I go home. "Soon you will be all better, Nana!" and yes, I shall. Can't wait!

It's a gorgeous day here today; I'm so glad I didn't leave yesterday when it was grey and drizzly all day long. Doug emailed to say there had been a hot spell, which has broken, and so it's going to be lovely on both ends of my (long) day.

I said goodbye to Mirei at the door, and made it quick, as she really has no idea how long it will be before we see each other again. I was feeling pretty teary, and I didn't want her to be sad. I got a nice kiss and a gentle hug earlier too, and a wave goodbye as Riku and I left for school at 8:05. Bryn will be walking Riku to school from now on; I'm going to miss our walks each day, as I did when he left San Francisco. It's always a lovely way to start off a weekday.

Riku spotted a long thin worm today as we walked along, and there was time for him to stop for a good look at it. It was moving fast, which really surprised Riku, and he asked me to tell his parents that he had seen his first live worm! Apparently he has seen only dead ones, which is hard to imagine, and even though I looked for it on the way home as promised, he/she had disappeared. We talked about buying a box of worms, maybe a hundred or more, and letting them loose in his back yard to make it healthier, and he thought it would be fun to dig them up too, and see how they were doing. We always check for the heron near the river, and we saw just his head and neck today; he was very still and must have been looking for breakfast.

And of course today I got to say "goodbye" to everyone, and not just "good morning". First was our friendly neighbour around the corner, then the principal who took my photo with Riku for me, a couple of the moms we always pass, and finally a group of boys who often greet us in the morning. I took two photos of them, so it will be fun to see all those familiar faces once I get home. Riku and I had a private kiss and hug before we got to his classroom, and then a long hug on his way back from getting a drink at the fountain down the hall. I had a hard time with that final goodbye and delayed it as long as possible. We waved to each other all the way down to the end of the corridor, before he went into this classroom.

I had a letter to mail to Skylar, Riku's "friend" from Starr King in San Francisco, so I walked the long way home leaving me to come up to the mailbox from the streets below us. It paid off, as I hoped it would, as I got to say goodbye to Mirei's "snowman" lady who was sitting outside her door in a chair, watching the gardening going on in the plot next to her home. That made my day, and I was so pleased to wave goodbye to her, and to take her photo too!

I put my bedding in the washer, got Bryn to hang up the two duvets we used, and then had breakfast and finished up my packing. My pick-up is at 12:50, for my 5:30 P.M. flight. I dashed off an email to my sisters, letting them know what time I would be in. Strange, as I leave at 5:30 tonight, Monday, and will be arriving in San Francisco at 11 A.M. today!

I had a big fruit plate for my breakfast with a few nuts; Akane came right back from dropping Mirei off at school and made me a wonderful packed lunch for my trip ... those yummy noodles with veggies and tofu that we had on Saturday, five inari (I like Akane's more than any I have tasted) and (I was delighted to find) a bag with lots of fresh carrot sticks too. It was all packed carefully with napkins and wooden chopsticks as well! Lucky me!

I had so much I wanted to take, and got right at it ... some photos of the upstairs, pics of the hole I had dropped into, a frozen juice slushie for each of the children, and a note to them each. I was just finishing off Riku's when the shuttle arrived, ten minutes early, but of course by the time the suitcases were packed, and I was in and settled, with the bill paid, it was exactly 12:50. Gail and Tom had thoughtfully dashed off some mega painkillers for me in the mail, and it turned out that after I had said goodbye to Akane and Bryn, and just pulled away from the house, Bryn checked the mail box and found they were there! They hadn't been fifteen minutes earlier, as I had checked. Too bad, as this might have made the trip a little more comfortable for me.

We drove into Kyoto to pick up an American couple from NYC, very friendly people, who had just finished up their three-month world tour in Kyoto. We chatted on the way to the airport, and it was lovely having company. We arrived at the airport at exactly 3 P.M., a half hour earlier than Akane had requested the porter, to help with my bags. However, the fellow at the curbside, along with my very nice driver, managed to get him there earlier for me. He was an enthusiastic, friendly young man, and took care of all of my bags, staying with me until they were loaded on the weigh scale. We waited less than five minutes to get to the check-in counter.

By this time I was ready for my first cup of tea of the day, so stopped at a Starbuck's cafe before going to my gate. Once I had that in hand, I realized I was hungry and so ate half of my noodle dish and an inari too, both so tasty! Then I was off to the gate area to read until my flight was called. The woman from the shuttle bus tracked me down to ask how I was doing, and, when it was time to board, an American fellow, who was passing by as I struggled to get out of my seat, offered to carry my bag on board for me.

I had changed my seat at the check-in counter, hoping that I might end up with two seats at the back of the plane to myself, and I lucked out! It was just perfect, as I could still sit by the window for the view and the extra leaning space, but not have to struggle past anyone when I wanted to get up to move a bit. I put my four hot packs on my back, and settled in for the long flight home.

It all went very well, much better than I hoped, as there was enough room that I could fidget at will. I watched a movie, did lots of reading, slept for perhaps an hour, enjoyed the rest of my meal from Akane, except for the inari, which I plan to enjoy here at home, and enjoyed the little meals that were part of the trip ... dinner, and a snack shortly before landing. I always get hungry on planes, so having the extra meal was a plus for me.

We landed a little bit early, and it was such a treat to walk out and find Doug waiting for me. It is good to be home, and I know I will be taking it much easier here, not having the temptation of walking with the children.

I had zero energy for the rest of the day, happy to lie about reading magazines, checking email once in a while, and napped about 1 P.M. for just an hour while lying on the sofa. It was wonderful to have Doug taking care of me, making me tea, and heating up some dinner for us. I was awake enough to watch a movie with him too, which ended at 11, and then turned in for the night, with two pillows tucked around me. Home.
