A Visit With Finn, Baker Beach, and "Sea Glass"

Riku and I had a long snuggle in bed this morning. I was awake and at the computer the day before when he woke at 8:30, and he told me that from now on, he wanted me to stay in bed in the morning so he could come up and snuggle with me. We giggle and he tells me his dreams, and asks about mine, and then he asks, "What's the plan for today?"

He had his fruit bowl in our bed, while I showered, and after the second part of his breakfast he went outside to crack open a geode I had bought for him in Nanaimo. It was hard! He used Doug's hammer and knocked away at it, until he finally put it on the bricks and whacked it really hard. Finally it broke into lots of pieces; I think he was disappointed not to have one large crystal emerge, but the bits were fun too, especially after he washed all the gritty stuff away and had a look at them all outside.

Amelia had a sleepover last night, and Kevin had a few errands to run, so I volunteered to have the children here this morning, while Susan and Kevin got ready for their week at a farm, starting tomorrow. However, Amelia and her guest were busy for the morning and early afternoon, so Kevin dropped Finn off to play with Riku. Riku was thrilled, as he had been asking if he could have them visit, and was quite happy that Finn made it over ... except when Finn broke his log building. However, he was quickly reassured that Grandpa and his dad could rebuild it all, exactly the same, and then he proceeded to show Finn how to build structures. Finn had a great time, and it was very cute to watch them.

They also went outside for a while, sweeping and digging, then upstairs where Riku got out the sit-on train for Finn, and puttered about downstairs too, pulling toys out of the big basket in the laundry room. They had lunch together, and then Finn followed Doug upstairs and spent a while there. When Doug came down, Finn followed him, and then asked Doug, bending over and asking very clearly in a hard-to-resist voice "Grandpa, do you want to go upstairs with me again"? How could he resist such a query? So up they went and Riku had a little time on his own for drawing.

We had made plans with Susan and Kevin and the children to meet up at Baker's Beach in the afternoon, and although it never did get as warm and sunny as I had hoped for, we packed up and left about two o'clock .

Amelia and Riku walked with Doug and me down to the far end of the beach, searching for "sea glass", Amelia's latest passion. You have to have sharp eyes to find the tiny tiny bits of coloured glass, with smooth edges caused by rolling and tumbling about in the sea. I have only ever seen quite large pieces, but even the biggest bits we found today were minuscule. That didn't curb the children's enthusiasm for looking, though, and soon Bryn and Kevin joined in the search too. There were a couple of orange pieces, a lot of green, a few white ones, and Amelia found the only blue one! She and Riku carefully tucked the treasures into the pockets of their kangaroo jackets, although at the end of the day Riku handed all his over to Amelia, but one. He told me she was collecting them, and he wasn't, but it will be fun to look for more of these at Ocean Beach one day.

Finn happily trundled about the beach, the two dads watched over the children and chatted, Doug spent a lot of time with the children when they were still in searching mode, and I sat and took photos once we gave up looking for more glass.

Susan and I had packed up some snacks, so we had cheese and crackers, cherries, Fig Newtons, and various flavours of potato chips. It all hit the spot, although Susan and I would have liked a big thermos of tea, which neither of us thought of beforehand.

We left close to 5 P.M., with Bryn accompanying Susan and family for dinner at a 60s Mexican-style restaurant. Riku was anxious to watch a movie he had borrowed from Amelia, and so after a nice deep warm tub, he was sitting up in bed set to eat his dinner there as Bryn had promised he could. Doug kept him company while I made dinner, and I sat with them while Riku ate. They stopped just a half hour short of the end of the movie, but Riku was fine with that. He knew ahead of time that it was going to be time for bed before it ended, and accepted it gracefully.

He was asleep at 8:30 tonight; I cleaned up the kitchen, wrote up my blog entry, answered a few emails, went through some photos for tomorrow, and went to bed at 11:30.

(And yes, I stayed in my bed this morning, reading for a while, so that I was there, nice and warm, when Riku came up for our snuggle.) Not hard to make this little boy happy!

A Card for Me, Swimming Lesson, and Some Basketball Time too!

Riku had his first of seven private swimming lessons scheduled for 11:30 this morning, so we spent a quiet morning at home. Riku asked me for one of the blank cards and envelopes I keep on hand, as he wanted to "write a note, not draw a picture" for me today. I read the paper at the table, promising not to peek while he did my card. It took him quite a long time and he didn't ask me how to spell any of the words at all. I didn't realize this until just now, many hours later! I think this was a first! On the front he put an American flag on one side, for me, and a Japanese flag on the other side, representing him. I did suggest that next time he could do a Canadian flag for me, but he said that was too hard! I think all the points on the maple leaf lead him to believe he won't do it properly. I'll work on that. The card was very touching; what loving thoughts go through this little boy's head. The drawing on the card shows our meeting at the airport, with the overhead monitor which let us know when they were coming out of customs and immigration, and with Bryn coming in behind him carrying the luggage.

At 11 o'clock we left the house. Riku was quite apprehensive about this class, and was very quiet on the way there. He had told me several times in the morning that he just didn't want to go to this lesson. Poor wee guy; it couldn't possibly be as bad as he thinks it is going to be.

He is just beginning to learn to swim at school in Japan, and he was stressed about that too. It just makes sense that with these seven private lessons he will gain some confidence, and if he's not swimming by the time he leaves, at least we hope he will be more confident in the water. Fingers crossed.

Of course the lesson went beautifully. I thought his teacher, one of three, was just the best fit for Riku's personality. She was very quiet and gentle with him, and it was obvious he trusted her and felt secure. Doug and I sat on a nearby bench at this small inside pool, and watched as he floated on his back, with his head supported, tried both water wings and fins, turned around with minimal help, and even slid off the edge of the pool, with Jennifer holding onto his hands. He told us he landed on her knee. I liked her so much that I requested her for him if she is available for any of his other classes. I hope this happens.

At the end of the class, the three children had a couple of minutes of free time, tossing a ball back and forth to one another. There was no other interaction at all during the class.

One of Riku's biggest fears is having his face underwater, but he has practiced this a bit in the tub each night here, and told me he did at home too. So, gradually, bit by bit, we hope this experience helps. His first sentence out of the water today was "That was fun!" He was pleased to know he was going to go again in three more days. Whew!

On the way home, Doug asked Riku if he liked doughnuts, and Riku answered that he "sort of did, if it was the kind with icing and sprinkles". So we stopped at Krispy Kreme and picked up a mini doughnut Riku chose for himself (with chocolate icing and sprinkles), two for Doug, and one for Bryn. I declined, as I would rather have chocolate.

I had told Riku the lesson would make him very hungry, and I guess he believed me as he ate an extra big lunch of mac' and cheese, as well as a generous plate of broccoli, and that was after the cinnamon toast he ate on the way home!

He was anxious to play with Kai again today, but he's at a summer camp program each afternoon until 3:45. However, I called his mom and we arranged to meet at the park at 4:15 for a short playtime. Riku and I had a leisurely stroll over there, stopping in to say hello to Lily first. She noticed Riku was taller, and thought he looked slimmer too. They were happy to see each other and shared a big hug.

Riku and I discovered the playground is closed for two days for cleaning and some reconstruction, so we waited for Kai at his house, and they both decided they wanted to come back here to play. They ran and laughed all the way home, hiding from me in the most obvious spots, and then just howling when I pretended to be scared when they jumped out at me. Once here, they shot some basketballs in the back garden, and had a snack. The hour and twenty minutes went by quickly, and Doug dropped Kai off at home at 5:30, on his way to his class.

I got Riku right into the tub, and he was singing and playing in there happily when Bryn came home. He filled daddy in on the swimming lesson, and ate another really good-sized meal tonight. I had a fresh fruit bowl sprinkled with crushed flax seed for my dinner, and Riku "snuck" a couple of strawberries during his meal. After dinner, he asked if he could have that for dessert. I don't think there are too many six year olds who would consider this a treat. Both Bryn and Doug would have turned it down, but I told Riku all of my sisters would like it too. He decided Sean would too, as we were talking today about how Sean is very healthy and takes good care of himself.

Riku had the donut first for dessert but decided after he ate it that he really would have liked maybe just half of it, and I have a feeling he is going to turn down the offer to stop the next time. He's just not a sweets eater ... ice cream isn't even a treat for him, and it wasn't for his cousin Bryn either. They may be the only two children I know who don't like ice cream!

Bryn was home for dinner and part of the evening with us, went off to visit Soo, and then spent some time with Sean and Bern after that.

Riku was asleep just after 8 P.M. tonight, and I am heading up now for bed at 11 o'clock, having accomplished absolutely nothing tonight, unless I count working three sudoku puzzles. Ah well, some days are just like that ... lazy lazy for some reason. Yawn!

An Unhurried Day and a Cook in the House

Today was a perfect day all 'round. The sun was shining and the day warm from the moment we woke up; Sutro Tower was clearly visible, which is always a good sign from Riku's point of view (it means it could be an Ocean Beach day), and we had nothing on our agenda but the Bay Area Discovery Museum in Marin County.

Riku had French toast for his "second" breakfast with Bryn, and drew a robot picture at the table before getting on with his day. I would like nothing more than a picture a day, but it doesn't always happen.

We didn't have to rush to get out of the house, and so he had time to look at some magazines while I packed up a few snacks. He also helped me make my bed this morning, smoothing out his side of it very carefully, and placing the pillows in exactly the same way I did on my side. Riku likes to be given little chores to do, and later today he helped out too.

Before we left, Riku asked for a bowl of pineapple and strawberries, with "sprinkles" on them, meaning the crushed flax seeds. He really really likes this, and told me he was going to ask for this at home in Japan too.

We spent four hours at the museum today, and it could not have been better. I didn't once have to suggest that we move on to something else, knowing we had lots of time to just meander. Of course he headed straight for the boat and found lots of other children interested in pretending they were sinking, or that they had spotted sharks and whales.

Riku wanted to eat our lunch outside at the picnic tables today. It's often too cool and/or windy out there, but the weather co-operated today. While I was waiting for our snacks and beverages, Riku had to wash his hands, and for the first time ever I let him go off to the restroom on his own. We had just been there, and he was fine with leaving me in line, returning shortly, and happy with his independence.

He ate a great lunch, and I bought him a bag of "Pirate Booty" as always for the ride home.

We meandered everywhere ... up to ring the bell, through the twig house maze, back to the boat, and today spent quite a bit of time in the craft area where he made a butterfly for the kitchen window. He window-painted too, as there were no other children there when we first went in. It is usually so crowded that he hadn't bothered with it the last couple of times we were there.

They had a wonderful exhibit in the big hall, of the lives of Chinese children, put together by the Boston Museum. There were hands-on exhibits, and buttons to push to listen in English, Chinese, Japanese, and Spanish. Riku chose the Chinese every time, and in the "language" area, repeated all the words as he heard them, except for one very long sentence. He gathered together pretend Chinese food for me in the kitchen area, and we saw several videos of the children introducing their parents, and watched them cooking & planting rice, and sitting down for a family meal. There was also a little Chinese theatre stage set up, and Riku donned a costume, wrapping it around himself so that he could appear as a scary figure alongside the two little girls all done up in the pretty costumes they had put on. This was so much fun and we spent a good deal of time in that area.

Once we had finished, Riku decided it was time to leave for home. Of course we stopped in at the gift shop, where he chose ten pieces of shiny "jewels" in a small velvet bag, Instead of watching the three pennies I gave him for the spinning circle spin down and into the base, he let them all go, one by one, caught them, and put those in the bag too! He sat happily in the back seat, eating his cheese popcorn, finishing off the grapes, and most of the cherries we had taken along for snacks.

We arrived home at 4:15 and I decided to make a cream soup with the asparagus Susan had left for us. As I snapped the first stalk, I thought Riku might enjoy helping, and I was so glad I asked. He was a big help to me, snapping all the stalks just right, then trimming off the ends for the garnish, and cutting the remaining stalks into one inch pieces. He was very careful with the knife and I left him to it, after watching him make the first few cuts. When that was all done he got up on a chair, stirring the veggies for ten minutes, and then carefully stirring again, once the hot milk was added. He's very patient when doing this step, much to my surprise.There was only a half recipe, but Bryn, Riku, and I all had a bowl, and Riku asked for more of the steamed tops to be added to his bowl. He ate every single bite, and told me after dinner that he wants to help me with dinner every night. I shall try to make sure I make dishes that give him something to do; the world can use more men who like to cook!

Riku had his tub, spent some time reading in the living room, and then asked for another bowl of fruit with flax seeds. We were in bed reading Shel Silverstein at 7:45. He had a busy active day and was asleep shortly after 8, after asking me what our plans were for tomorrow. I think if Sutro Tower is visible in the morning, we might just head out for an Ocean Beach day.

Bryn spent the evening at home with us, working, and heading off to bed early. I'm off too!

Oh, nice surprise in the fridge this morning, as last night Soo gave Bryn a very large portion of a tofu and fresh vegetable dish that I was always very fond of. She is coming over next week to make some special dishes for Bryn and Riku (likely a meatball clay pot dish), and some vegetarian potstickers for us too. Lucky us!

A Day in the Hood

Potrero Hill, that is! Riku and I had hoped for an Ocean Beach day today, especially after our gorgeous day yesterday, but it was not to be. The fog didn't lift until after 3 o'clock, and Sutro Tower was hidden in clouds just an hour and a half later. So, we stayed in our own neighbourhood, which worked out well, as we did some fun and varied things today.

I pulled out my old copy of Shel Silverstein's book called "Uncle Shelby's ABZ Book", a very quirky, silly, and often outrageous book. It is really an adult book, but has just enough silly bits that Riku really got a kick out of it. There are some pages that are just too old for him to understand the humour, and I passed by others that would have required far too much of an explanation for him right now. Some are just inappropriate for young children, but the ones he "got" really had him laughing out loud. Doug sat with him a little later on, talking about some of the pages he had already seen and heard, and I snapped a photo of the two of them.

While I did the ironing this morning, and got a couple of emails written, Doug and Riku went to the library in the Mission District to pick up a few books for Riku, eight in total. There is a book about baby spiders, Shel Silverstein's "The Missing Piece", "A World Full of Monsters" to read to me, a book called "Earthquake" by a Chinese author, another about the human body, with lots of illustrations, another to read to me, called "Sheep Blast Off!", "Baby Beluga" to read/sing to me, and the one we started reading/looking at/discussing tonight about volcanoes ... a nice varied lot of reading for him to choose from over the next week or so.

Riku is enjoying access to my computer and now is able to find his own way to his "favourites" site list (he was starting to do this before moving to Japan), and can easily pick and choose which one of the many choices he has open to him. Today he logged on to the Crayola site, and started filling in an online form! I gave him a pretend email address, after he asked me what his was, and he filled in our phone number, gave the name of a friend, and asked for our address.

He also said he was working on his blog, making Doug and I both smile, and tapped a few lines to his dad on IM too. He knows how to expand the screen now too, and finished up his computer time by making a painting using the Jackson Pollack site. Fun for him, and fun to watch him navigate his way through it all too.

After lunch, I dropped Doug and Riku off at the "playground at the top of the hill", while I went off to Rainbow for groceries on my own. Just over an hour later I joined them, and found Riku playing with Daisy, a little girl from the second grade one class at "Starr King", his former school. Doug said Riku was very happy to see her arrive, with her mom and siblings, and they played together until they left.It turns out there is a small reunion picnic on Saturday for old and new students in the Chinese program, so we may join in on that after Doug's class. Daisy's mom, Meg, sent me the information on this, and also said they would very much like to have Riku over to play one afternoon.

"Spiderman" arrived in the mail today as expected, from Netflix, and Riku was very keen to see it. So I made a hearty snack for him, and a hot cup of tea for me along with some guacamole and crackers for my lunch. We sat together and watched the first forty-five minutes of it; Riku has such a hearty laugh and obviously was enjoying it! He was so good about not complaining about having it interrupted so soon, but I had promised him we could cook again today, so he was looking forward to that too.

He sat and drew a picture of Spiderman while I got out all the ingredients for "Signal Hill Gingerbread Cake", an old favourite from years ago in Ontario. Riku was just so happy to be baking again, as over the years we have spent quite a bit of time in the kitchen baking together. He remembered just how to level off the flour and baking soda and spices with the flat of a knife, and how to crack an egg too. He did make funny references to how the batter looked, especially after he had added the molasses ... "vomit" was his chosen word, although "nasty" came up as well, when the egg was first added.

Soon the cake was in the oven, and Riku was back to his drawing until dinner was ready. Bryn came in much later from work tonight, after a couple of phone meetings, but was home in time to sit with Riku at the table, listen to his stories about his day, and talk about the photos about Pompeii at length.

Riku is a little worried about his swimming lesson tomorrow; even though he enjoyed it on Monday, he now is concerned that he might have to jump in the water. I think he finally accepted it will be fine. Bryn gave him a little talk about not worrying about things before they happen, as often it ends up not being anything to worry about at all. It's a hard lesson to learn for sure.

Riku told me this morning that he didn't wake up in the night at all, did waken when the big hand was at the 9 (quarter to seven), but did what I asked him to, and turned on the light to read. I heard a small noise just five minutes later, likely him getting a book out to read, as that was what he was doing when I joined him for our morning snuggle. He just lit up when I walked in the room; what a greeting!

Today, as we were at the front window waiting to wave goodbye to his dad, Riku wrapped his arm around my neck, put his face close to mine and said "I love your cheeks, Nana, 'cause they are soft, and I love your neck, and your shoulders, and your back, because they are soft and warm.

Riku was asleep just a minute or two after 8 P.M. again tonight.

Another Successful Swimming Lesson

We spent a quiet morning at home today, as Riku's swimming lesson was just after lunch, and I didn't want a tuckered-out boy going for his lesson. He started to worry about it mid-morning, with tummy aches, and complaints about not wanting to go. The morning was fun for him though, as we watched the second forty-five minute segment of "Spiderman" together, he spent some time on the Internet (having figured out how to log into the craft section of the Crayola site), and then "drew" using a new program that Doug installed for him. He now knows his way around his own "Riku's Favourites" on my desktop, so he can just go through all the various bits we have added to it over the past few years. He even dragged one into the "trash" following my instructions.

He helped Doug put away the large supplies of toilet paper and tissues this morning; he loves to help and be involved and I was happy to have him opening the big packages with scissors and then deliver it all to Doug in the storage room. After it was done, he decided it would be fun to cut the plastic covering into little bits, possibly hundreds of little bits. What a mess! I told him he hadn't made that big a mess since he was one year old, and pulled out all the plastic bags from my cupboard. He had a great ol' time, and had fun flinging it all up into the air when he had finished. Of course, then he got to pick it up and realized just how many pieces he had cut!

He drew a great picture of various monsters today, and one day I must put up an entry or two with his latest drawings. I notice he is not using colour as much as he used to, although sometimes he will go back and colour them once he has done the major drawing in black on white paper.

I made corn soup for lunch today, and Riku helped me break up the kernels to add to the pot. He was sorting through his books when I began the actual cooking, so he offered to help me another day. I got big compliments on the soup, though, as he told me it was really really good, and he ate every bite without any urging. He was happy to know that his dad liked the soup when he was a little boy too.

Once Riku is in the pool area, his fears seem to abate, or at least he has decided there is no point in talking about it! He watches the other swimmers carefully, and once his name is called he is up and on his way to the far end of the pool area. Taryn was Riku's swimming instructor today and again he lucked out, as both Doug and I felt she did a great job. I put in a request for her, and Jessica is also on his "preferred" list, as one particular teacher we saw there would just not have worked for him.

Riku seemed to enjoy it once more, and we were so happy to see him floating more comfortably, with his head being held, and also moving on his own for the first time that we could see. They tend to stay down at the shallow end of the pool, of course, and so it's hard to see exactly what is going on. However, this little boy came bursting out of the pool telling me "That was great!

The instructors make notes on their file sheet after the lesson, but there is no direct contact with them at all. However, I went to check on the name of the woman whom we don't want to teach him, and his own teacher told me that Riku had done really well today. How wonderful to hear that, as it meant he has come a long way from that one lesson before.

This morning he told me he wanted a Krispy Kreme doughnut after swimming today, after all, as he had decided on Monday that he didn't want any more. Then before we left he told us he didn't want one, but once we got on the road he changed his mind again. When he was getting changed after his lesson he said to me "I sure change my mind a lot. First I want a doughnut and then I don't. Then I do. Then I say I don't like swimming, and then I do, and then I don't, and now I do!" We had some errands to run, and the first stop was at Krispy Kreme for that doughnut with chocolate icing and coloured sprinkles on it. We stopped at the library for Doug, although Riku and I sat in the car chatting. I want to take Riku to one of our public swimming pools in the Mission and needed to pick up their schedule. The pool is in a large playground/soccer field complex, and Riku asked if we could stay there for a while. Doug had his book with him, and we spent an hour and a bit there, watching Riku go from one area to another.

It takes some time before he decides he can do something, and I can almost see him thinking about trying it, and then he does, carefully for certain, but going for it. His cousin Bryn was and may still be still the same way. Caillie would just go for it, as does Mirei, but not these boys. Interesting to see this process.

Quiet time at home for the rest of the evening. Riku went through the book of the body very very carefully, page by page, asking me to explain anything that wasn't obvious to him. We talked about autopsies, and how doctors hundreds of years ago didn't used to know for sure what was inside the body. We talked about X-rays, and bones, and skeletons, until it was tub time and then dinner. He ate a huge dinner tonight, brown rice mushroom risotto, with sides of veggies, and he chose grapes for dessert.

Riku had enjoyed it all so much last week, when we were there with Susan and the children, and we decided Caillie and Doug would like it too. I packed up a big picnic lunch and we were off. There really is so much to do and see there, and we all had a great time. Caillie and Riku did all the hands-on fun parts first, with the vortex and flying foam parts and more, and then strolled around the large area upstairs with so many interesting displays. Riku and Caillie lingered over these, calling to each other to come to see what they had found.

The animal section was next and Riku couldn't wait to show Doug and Caillie the huge yellow banana slug he discovered last week. We did manage to spot the coyote this time, although he was sleeping, much to Riku's disappointment. There was lots to see, though ... otters at feeding time, snakes, frogs, butterflies, birds, sleeping porcupines and badgers.

Right at noon, Riku announced he was hungry and wanted to eat right then! So we went back to the same picnic area as last time, mostly so that Riku and Caillie could climb the same tree he climbed last week. Everyone was hungry and we enjoyed our lunches first, and then Riku and Caillie did some climbing. There was still more to see inside the museum, though, so back we went, and Riku spotted the coyote, wide awake and standing up looking right out at us! We were all very excited, and managed to get some good photos of him.

We had heard there was a big new playground on the grounds, so we drove toward the entry gate, and discovered "Magic Kingdom", a lovely top-notch playground with a huge enclosed twisty slide, built into a massive play structure. There were also swings, and regular slides, and
"creatures" to climb on and in. It was too hot for Doug and me, but Caillie and Riku had a great time and Riku particularly did not want to leave.

There was a beach nearby and that appealed to me, so we walked toward the water, through a huge flock of Canada Geese (Riku now knows how big and green their BMs are!), but realized we couldn't get to the beach without walking too far in the heat. Riku begged for more time at the playground, Doug and I found a shady tree just outside the play area, and eventually it was time to leave for home, and a playdate with Ian for Riku.

Caillie finished her book yesterday, so she and Doug went to the library on 24th Street, coming home with two new books for her to read. I'm sure she won't get through both of them, but it doesn't hurt to have a spare. Later in the evening she read bits of one to Kelly, and it sounded like a perfect book for her.

Ian's parents picked Riku up at 4:20 and dropped him back home, after more playground time at a local spot. They had a great time together, and Bryn arrived home just in time to have a chat with Wendy, Ian's mom, before saying goodbye.

I popped Riku right into the tub, and finished off making a dinner I knew Riku and Caillie would enjoy ... fresh corn chowder, with two pieces of Riku's favourite fish fillets, and sides of veggies for them both. I also made a lemon pudding cake, an old favourite from when Kelly and Bryn were little. Caillie liked it very much, but Riku not so much. Instead he had a bowl of fresh pineapple, and we saved a dish of the pudding for Bryn, who liked it very much.

Riku was asleep at 7:45 tonight, leaving Caillie and me time for two games of cards, before she went off with her new book to read while I got in some time on the computer.

Hot hot night here! We have the fan going in our bedroom for the first time this month.



27 August 2009

Finally, a Gorgeous Ocean Beach Day, and a Reunion for Riku

Even though we have been to Linda Mar beach twice, and Riku and I had made an attempt for a picnic at Ocean Beach a couple of weeks ago, we just have not had that perfect day there we all love so much. After our very hot day yesterday, and night too(!) very unusual here, I had a feeling today was the day. And it was! Caillie and Riku watched a bit of Cinderella this morning, while eating part of their breakfast in front of the TV, and I packed up our lunches and snacks for our day.

Amelia was home from school, not feeling well, so we made arrangements to meet there. Traffic was slow, I had to stop for gas, there was construction after that to wind through, but finally there we were! It was just the perfect day, with lots of sun, cooler air at the water, and no wind, always the big hurdle with this beach. Caillie lay on the blanket on her own, while Susan, Finn, Riku, Amelia, and I wandered down the beach. Amelia had a nice big bucket full of shells already, and she helped Riku add to his bucket. Finn meandered along as well, enjoying himself, and we headed back to the central part of the beach after Susan stepped on a bee, and got badly stung.

We had our lunch, after which Caillie joined the children down at the water's edge. Susan had brought along a couple of water guns, so they were in use, although when they are filled from the edge of the water, they are also filled with sand. Caillie got really soaked, but was a very good sport about it, even if she was the favourite target. I took lots of photos, of course, as beach photos with the children are among my favourites.

Susan, Amelia, and Finn went back to the far end of the beach for a walk and exploration, giving Caillie and Riku some time alone in the water ... and for more photos of the two of them. We're down to our last day with Riku.

We got home just in time to have a quick drink, splash our faces, and go off to Starr King, (Riku's school here until this past March), to meet up with teachers and former classmates. Caillie stayed home on her own to shower off the salt water.

Doug dropped Riku and me off at the school, and he had such a lovely reunion. We first went to see his grade one teacher, Ms. Tong, and happily gave her as big a hug as he got from her, along with her lovely big smile.

Leaving her to getting her class ready for dismissal, we walked down the hall and spotted some of the children from his class last year, now in grade two. The teacher didn't know Riku, but had been told by the principal that we would be stopping in. His old classmates gathered 'round him, giving him hugs, holding his hands, and asking him lots of questions.

As we were walking down the opposite end of the hall, on our way outside, I noticed some other classmates, with a new teacher as well, and even though Riku was a little shy about going back to stand in the door of that room, he did, and got another chorus of "Hi Riku!". I am sorry I didn't get photos of these children too, but the teacher didn't seem quite so user-friendly.

Some parents of these former classmates spotted us as we walked downstairs to the courtyard, and stopped to say hello to Riku too, patting him on the head, and asking a few questions. Riku is quite shy about these situations until they are underway, and then he is just fine with it all and happy.

Outside we spotted Ms. To, his kindergarten teacher coming out with her class, and Riku went running up to her with no hesitation at all. She was obviously just as pleased to see him and enveloped him in a huge hug, and a big smile.

Once the children had been collected by their parents, Ms. Chang, his English teacher in kindergarten, came running up to embrace him in a, yes, big hug! At one point all three teachers were there, asking him lots of questions, patting him, hugging him again, and all of them told him how much they missed him, and how tall he was! It was adorable, and it was just great that he was here in the city just as school was back in session, to see these familiar faces.

Riku and I walked home down our familiar route. He stopped to give "his" little round tree a hug, and to comment that it needed some water. Then we walked down the 132 steps we had walked down so many times before, holding hands and counting as we we did so. It was so hot, and it seemed a perfect time for Caillie and him to finish watching their movie, with the fan blowing on them, while I got dinner ready.

About three weeks ago, Riku had asked if we could have "that dinner where you put the bread in the hot cheese on a stick" ... cheese fondue! I had no memory of Riku enjoying it so much; we always have it on Christmas Day, so he had tucked that away in his mind until now. So, we decided we would have it the last night of his stay here, with Caillie. Bryn was joining us as well, so it was perfect. Caillie said she wasn't crazy about it, but would try again (Kelly and Gregg now serve it on Christmas Eve or up in the Manitoulin sometime during their stay there). She liked it and had it for dinner, instead of something else, so I was very pleased about this. It really is a family traditional meal for us.

The evening was our usual one, with Riku tubbed first, then time with Caillie, on the computer tonight with the drawing program, and then two books before tuck-in at 8 P.M.. Doug and Bryn had gone off to a movie, Caillie and I had two games of cards with our tea, and then had some quiet time on our own for the last bit of the evening.

Sayonara to Bryn and Riku, and a Touristy Day for Caillie and Me

Riku called out during the night, and even though I was sure he would fall back to sleep on his own, I decided to have a few hours with him, and perhaps have him sleep in a little later this morning as well. It worked, as he awoke at 7:10, and was very happy to find me there, waiting to read to him. He ate his fruit bowl while we snuggled and read, for the last time in too long.

We had heard about a "science" experiment for children, using Mountain Dew soda, baking soda, and peroxide, and decided today was the day to try it out. It was supposed to turn a fluorescent green in the dark when mixed together, but to our disappointment, nothing at all happened. It turned out to be a hoax, as Doug learned, but the failed experiment made Riku decide to try some others. So, out came the vinegar, the baking soda, some cinnamon, a little oil, and a few other last minute ideas... green fluid made from mixing one of his marking pens in water, and a funnel. This kept Riku busy for the longest time, and it was lots of fun to watch him being so serious about it all.

Packing was well underway not long after Bryn woke, as I had most of Riku's belongings on the sofa, and some of Bryn's too. He got busy filling their two suitcases, and had most of it done when an old friend from Vancouver, Mark Henderson, stopped by with his girlfriend Meridith, to have breakfast with Bryn. They arrived in town just last night, so it was to be a very quick visit, unfortunately.

Caillie slept in quite late too, but was up and showered by the time that they all arrived back at the house. Meridith was very sweet with Riku, sitting on the floor going through his tin box of insects and snakes, and talking to him about them all ... which were their favourites, which would make a better pet, etc. It was great to see Mark again and to meet Meridith. Riku showed them his art work, stored on Doug's computer, and the time went by quickly. They left after we took what has become our traditional "photo with guests", and Riku gets right into his usual position on the top of the sofa.

Riku and Caillie had time to watch two short episodes of "Casper", and Riku had the third part of his breakfast upstairs. I packed up his snacks and a sandwich for the plane, Bryn lightened Riku's very heavy backpack to make room for them, and to make that pack lighter too!

I was feeling very sad as I realized that, with every thing we did, it was going to be the last time, for a long time. Riku told me not to get rid of any more toys as he will be back, and I got lots of impulsive extra hugs and kisses too. In the night, when he was lying there, he said very quietly, "I don't want to go home." I don't think he was saying it to me, but this morning he was very excited at the prospect of seeing Mirei and his mommy. That made me feel much better! He said Mirei would be very glad to have him home again, as she really likes the cat game he plays with her; he is the cat, and Mirei is his "mommy".

So, of course the time came too quickly to leave the house. Caillie and I sat with him in the back seat. Riku pointed out the hill that he didn't climb with his daddy this trip; it was on his list, and so I promised it would be on the list for next summer. We chatted all the way to the airport, just a ten minute drive, so not long at all, and said our goodbyes at the curb. He's very excited about this trip alone with his Dad, watching movies, and being on their way home.

It was a hot day again, so I was ready to just sit and put my feet up when we got home. Caillie worked on the last of three letters to a friend, and we went off to mail that for today's pickup. Not much later, we had decided to do Ghiradelli Square, for a hot fudge sundae, as well as walk to Pier 39 to see the sea lions.

First came the hot fudge sundae! We shared it, though Caillie could have eaten a whole one on her own, so I put down my spoon and let her finish it off. The whole area was just packed and we were dodging folks all the way to Pier 39.

Caillie was pretty tuckered out from a too late bedtime last night, and was happy to head home after the long walk back to the Square.

A plain cheese pizza from Amici's was on Caillie's list too, and so Doug and I placed our order, and went to pick it up, while Caillie had some computer time on her own here. The pizza was delicious, and she and I had two games of cards after we finished, as well as our tea. Caillie is drinking herbal tea, which is great, as I seldom use it and she is enjoying it so much.

Bed early for Caillie tonight; she read until 9:30, I wrote just a few emails ... I'm so far behind now, I can't begin to catch up, and chose my blog photos too.

I'm tired, emotionally and physically, and am going off to bed to read my wonderful hard-to-put-down book "Too Many Men"; it's not a light and easy book, but a page turner for certain, and I am reading much later than I should be these nights!

I hope Riku is sleeping soundly on the plane, along with his Dad. They've been flying now for nine hours and must be getting close to Korea, where they will wait for their connection to Osaka.

29 August 2009

Another Day Being Tourists

The plan for today, after our two gorgeous days of hot, clear weather, was to walk the Golden Gate Bridge with Caillie, something we do each time she is here. It's on her list! :) However, living on the west coast means that the weather changes constantly and quickly and so we woke to low-hanging fog today, and it was still hanging about at noon.

So we had a lazy morning at home, all of us happy not to be dashing out immediately. Caillie has been busy with my craft drawer since she arrived, making colourful cards, writing letters to a friend, and to Gregg's mom, but also just poking about with all the paper, stickers, and soft wires, and lots of glue and tape too! She sat at the table as busy as could be, while I got at the laundry, got a couple of emails out, and cleared things away.

When it was clear that we weren't going to walk the bridge, which is not fun at all when it's covered with fog (cold and with no view), we changed plans, and decided to go to see Andy Goldsworthy's "The Spire", and decide from there what to do next. Caillie saw "Fault Line" last year so she has two of Goldsworthy's pieces under her belt now. She keeps a tiny journal she started when she was just three years old, on her trip to visit us in England, and has kept it up each trip with us since.

Doug suggested we go to the Pet Cemetery in the Presidio.. At first Caillie wasn't sure about this, but she enjoyed it very much. It's very touching as there are sweet dedications to beloved pets, just tiny and not elaborate, but heart-felt for sure, to the pets of soldiers and officers at the base, dating mostly from WW1 and WWII. The site is in pretty sad condition; the Boy Scouts used to take care of it, but haven't for a few years, and now just recently many trees have been cut down to make room for bridge repairs, and it all looks quite neglected. We wandered about reading out the inscriptions on the markers to each other, and looking at the photos of some of the pets.

From there we went to the Palace of Fine Arts, as Caillie had not been there before. It's a lovely spot, with lots of photo opportunities, so we spent perhaps a half hour there just wandering about. I hope to do this with Kelly, Gregg, and the four children when they come in March.

Chinatown is also on Caillie's to-do list, and so we had Doug drop us off a block from the "dragon gate" that officially marks this area. We had such a good time, laughing our way up one side of the street and down the other, warning each other not to look at the hanging barbecued ducks in the windows. Ugh! Our plan had been to have lunch in Chinatown, and we checked out a few menus, but nothing really appealed to us. It might have had something to do with those dead ducks. We went in and out of a few stores, as Caillie thought she might find something fun to take home as a souvenir, but nothing clicked, and we were ready for lunch.

Caillie had a yen for sushi, and if we had had more time, we would have taken a bus to Japantown, but instead walked to the big shopping centre at the cable car turnaround point on Market Street. There was only one very expensive sushi restaurant there, so we walked over to Macy's, checked out their food area, and hit the jackpot! Caillie ordered two plates, one a California roll and one cucumber. There were twelve pieces in all and she enjoyed every bite of it. I had my favourite Boudin sourdough roll filled with tuna salad; it has been many months since I've had one, and it was delicious.

We lucked out by catching a bus within three minutes of standing at the bus stop at Union Square, transferred to the #19 bus to Potrero Hill within five minutes, and got home about 6 P.M. I was tired and ready for a hot cup of tea, but also had lots to do, and managed to do both. Caillie is trying hard to finish up a library book, and she spent some time on the computer too. Our evenings fly by quickly!

An hour and a half later, Doug and I drove to El Toro to pick up a burrito for Caillie's dinner. We made another stop at Taqueria Can-Cn on Mission Street to pick one up for Doug (he is no longer an El Toro fan), and I had some fruit for dinner, still full from my late lunch. Caillie and I went upstairs to watch a movie she had Doug rent for us from Netflix, an oldie called "Bringing up Baby", with Katherine Hepburn and Cary Grant. It was just delightful; I think she had seen it a few years ago with either her dad or mom, and thought I would enjoy it too. I did, although we stopped it at 9 o'clock so that Caillie could get to bed, read, and have her light out by 9:30.

I am just starting to panic at all that needs doing before leaving for Toronto on Wednesday. We're off to Santa Cruz tomorrow, and that gives me one day to organize the packing, trying not to forget all the extras I want to take with me, getting through as many emails as I can, as I may not have a computer for a month, and a dozen other little jobs that must be done before we leave. Bed too late at midnight, and I read too long after that too! Who do I see about getting more hours in my day?

30 August 2009

Santa Cruz!

We were hoping to do Santa Cruz with both Caillie and Riku, but the timing just didn't work out (Riku had swimming lessons booked, and friends to see), so yesterday we set off with Caillie for one of the last items on her to-do list.

Doug and I had two errands to do first. Doug had an appointment at 9 A.M. and, as a result, is now signed up for Medicare, which means he will have health insurance coverage here as of October! Yahoo! I went to get a much needed hair cut but learned that my gal doesn't start work on Mondays until noon, so that has to wait until tomorrow, my last day at home for a month.

The day was perfect for our trip south, not too hot, and not too cool, but just right! We stopped, as has now become our tradition, in Capitola, a tiny town just outside Santa Cruz, for lunch at "Dharma's", a vegetarian restaurant. Caillie knew without looking at the menu that she would be having her favourite burrito there, I had a wonderful white bean and basil soup, a huge and delicious side salad, and a serving of brown rice. It was all delicious. Doug had a breakfast burrito, one of his favourite dishes at this spot.

We spent a lovely couple of hours there, first walking out to the end of the wharf to check out the view and the sea lions. There were a few in the usual spot under the wharf, but today we saw lots of them along the south side of the wharf as well. And, much to our delight, found some very laid back sea lions basking and rocking gently in someone's catamaran!

Caillie was only interested in doing the big roller coaster ride, as she and her family will be here during March break next year. She decided she would rather go on the other rides with them, and so she and her grandfather went on two roller coaster rides together. They were both disappointed in the first one, but had a great time, as always, on the one that gives them a long classic high ride, rather than the quick bends and shorter dips on the first ride. I prefer neither!

We bought some salt water taffy too, as that was the second thing Caillie wanted in Santa Cruz. It's now available at Pier 39, but she waited until she could get it where she always has. I noticed the open bag beside her bed while she was reading tonight.

After arriving home, we had a quick dinner made up of things in the fridge that won't keep, and then Caillie and I went upstairs to eat while watching the end of "Bringing up Baby". It ended happily, of course, with the protaganists in love. I have suggested to Doug that he watch it on his own before sending it back to Netflix. Such fun to see Katherine Hepburn and Cary Grant oh-so-many years ago.

I had to do a Creative Memories order to buy extra sheets for my albums ... the company is changing the size of their albums(!), and so Caillie, with pen and paper in hand, went with me from spot to spot, helping me count how many pages and protectors I need for them all. It was a biggish job and went much faster with my secretary close by.

Bryn wrote to let me know that Riku had awakened happily at 7:30 this morning, despite awaking a few times during the night. He walked to school on his own, and when Bryn showed up a little later on, after biking there with extra school supplies for Riku, he was happily engaged in conversation with his classmates. Mirei starts back at her French Immersion program tomorrow, although is protesting, saying she would rather go to the Japanese preschool program she attended for a session this summer.

She and Riku are happy to be back in each other's company, and she is making them all laugh with a new trick ... turning her tongue upside down and then sticking it out! What a girl! In just one month and three days I will have her with me for a couple of weeks.

The evening passed too quickly as always, and I went off to bed at 11 P.M. tonight. All the fresh air and walking today made me too tired to stay up too late. Just as well ... tomorrow will be a very busy day, packing up for a month away.
