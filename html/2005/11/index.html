Caterpiller Power!

Celeste and Melinda had just left when I came downstairs at 7:30 this morning and Doug left fifteen minutes later. I answered a few emails, sorted through some gifts in the cedar chest, and had time to read the paper and eat before Riku arrived at 9 a.m. Bryn said he was a sick boy, and he was... coughing and sniffly and pale. Later in the morning he was running a slight fever too. Before that though he sat and played with playdo at the table, ate a little toast and "bacon", drank a small glass of juice and was ready for a wee nap at 10:30. He slept for only forty-five minutes which was fine, as I would rather he had a longer nap in the afternoon.

Doug and I decided to give Riku one of his big birthday gifts early, as he is leaving for Japan just two days later. The big truck we had picked out for him was a huge hit! It took he and I quite a long time to get it separated from the packaging, and so he pretended we were "workers" and we were "working hard" at it all. We really were! I had to find a screw driver as there were screws holding down the wheels, as well as lots of plastic wiring holding the front end down onto cardboard.

The next hour was spent with this very loud machine, working away in the kitchen. It backs up, runs forward, has gears to dump the back end and bring it up again, there is music, the truck vibrates, it reminds you to wear your hard hat! and the thing Riku loves best, I think, is when the right button pushed comes out with "caterpiller power!" He says it every time with lots of emphasis on the "power". This was a great gift for him and Bryn got a big kick out of it too, when he came to pick Riku up at 6:15. In fact, he realized I hadn't released the wheels and we actually saw it moving backwards and foreward.

We had a little quiet tv time, a rare event for us; this might have been our second time watching tv ever together here! I put on my children's tape from Hawaii; Riku kept asking me for the "polar bear" and I have no idea what he meant. I asked Bryn and he didn't know either so my guess it is something that he sees at home with Akane. Riku liked the whale and the owl on the kekei tape, and I certainly enjoyed watching it again. Bryn and Caillie were crazy about this tape when they were younger and it brought back lots of good memories.

Susan called and I had a little chat with Amelia, who was going to a swim session this afternoon, and told me she had gone on the trapeze yesterday. She asked if Riku was napping and I told her he was sick; she asked twice more about him. We were hoping to have some playground time together today but Riku needs to stay put and not expend a lot of energy. Amelia sounded so sweet and it was lovely to hear her little voice.

Riku had a nice long nap in the early afternoon after eating a good-size portion of a homemade noodle soup with lots of vegies in it. He woke at an hour and twenty minutes, I asked him if he was ready to get up or if he would like to sleep a little big more. His answer "I would like to sleep a little bit more", he rolled over, pulled his cotton blanket up around him, and was asleep for another forty minutes.

He ate some more soup, and we went upstairs to read some books, sitting together on the bed. He was pretty warm still, his hands so hot, and he was happy to snuggle up and read all about jelly fish, a book from Kelly. He perked up after that, and Bryn found us upstairs, sitting on the floor with the truck and the blocks, filling it up and dumping it over and over.

They left at 6:30, Celeste and Melinda arrived home at 6:45, and Doug a half hour later. We had quite an adventure trying to go out for dinner, Celeste's and Melinda's treat. We set off to Luna, an upscale Mexican restaurant Doug and Dave ate at last week; Doug had raved about it, and Celeste and Melinda decided we should try it too. After driving downtown, we found out it was closed for a private party. Off to the outer Mission to go to "Mom is Cookin'" and found the sign gone and the restaurant closed completely! We've been going there for twenty years and it is gone! Back to the Mission District for a burrito at La Papalote.. it was fine, but just... too salty and with an uninteresting marinated tofu.

Doug booked me for my up-coming almost-two month trip. I fly from here on the 29th of November for a flight to Osaka, leave there (hopefully with Mieri Elizabeth having made an early arrival!), on the 20th of December, and flying back to SF on January the 17th.

Raining tonight... first serious rain of the season.

Bed at 11:30...

3 November 2005

The End of Another Week... Already!

I have no idea where the days went to this week, but fly by they did! I asked if I could have Riku today; he wasn't feeling up to par yesterday and I quite enjoy having the time with him at home, after all the half-days at nursery school. He will be off to Japan in less than two weeks, so the more time with him now, the better.

This may have been the first day ever that Riku did not "work" at his table in the kitchen, drawing and "writing" letters. Instead he chose the blue Play-doh, asking me to make a jellyfish! He seemed quite pleased with it and didn't break it up quite as quickly as he usually does, a good sign! Maybe he liked it!

He wanted to "throw the ball!" again today, so we went in the hallway, he sat on the bottom step while we rolled it back and forth. We do try to make him repeat wee Bryn's famous-in-the-fmaily phrase "this is not a bouncing ball, this is a rolling ball" but so far no luck! Yesterday he would put his hands up to his head the second I released the ball and I had him cupping his hands for it. Today we perfected it, as he didn't cover his head once! Just for fun, I covered mine and he said "No Nana, not do it like that!". Fast learner... and teacher too!

While I got him a little snack ready he went in to play catch with Grandpa. It was hilarious; Riku was laughing so hard as Doug pretended the ball was down his front, and would then put it down Riku's overalls too. Soon Riku was putting it down the front of Doug's robe, and laughing that wonderful delighted laugh as Doug pretended to search for it, finally letting it drop from his robe onto the floor. I managed to get this on video tape.

Riku was not coughing a lot, but when he did, it was a hard and nagging cough. I worry about pneumonia coming back, as I know it's likely to return once you have had it.

This was the second day I set the timer for Riku to warn him of his naptime. Today he asked for it! He said "put timer on; when it goes ring ring ring, it is naptime." My word, this is easy! I used to just tell him "ten more minutes", but it was easy to let it slide longer or forget when the time was up. He only slept an hour, which was fine, as I thought he would more easily get to sleep this afternoon, but it didn't happen and I should have encouraged him to stay in bed longer this morning...

He ate well, though, and did lots of juice and water drinking, so perhaps this flu/cold whatever will disappear quickly. We read some stories in the living room, working our way through some of the classics... "Little Red Riding Hood", "The Three Little Pigs" and the story of the runaway gingerbread man. These are in a Spanish language book, but it's easy enough to follow the stories with the great illustrations they have.

He had trouble breathing when lying down for his nap this afternoon, so I decided to put him in the stroller, hoping the fresh air would help him drift off. It didn't, but we walked for an hour, and he lay back commenting occasionally on what he was seeing. He was at least relaxing and having some quiet time, and I had a little walk too. Just on the off chance that Akane might be home, I rang their bell, and much to my surprise she was there! It was 5 p.m. and Riku went off upstairs with his mommy.

Doug and I were babysitting Amelia tonight, while Susan and Kevin went off to a movie, so we left the house at 6:20. Amelia was just delightful. Happy to see us after her first not-sure reaction, and then sat on the floor chatting, while building with Riku's blocks. She was a happy little camper, and it was such fun being with her. Doug and I sang a little made-up song about spiders crawling on first me, until she realized it was all in fun, and then on her! Before her bath at 7:30 she ate a container of yogurt and we shared some chips and guacamole too.

Of course I love bathtime with the children; Amelia was amazing as she puts her head way back in the water and stayed like that, telling me how strong she was, until I took a photo of her. She is strong! But she is gentle too, and bathed her little water baby, washing her hair, carefully keeping the water out of the baby's eyes.

Amelia loves getting lotion on her, and I laugh when I think about her turning over, butt in the air, so that I can put it on her back, and then flopping over to get her tummy taken care of too. She just lays back and enjoys the experience, lifting one arm and one leg at a time for the cream. She is going to love massages one day, just like her Mom and her Nana! Caillie is very much like this too, and was from the time she was a little girl like Amelia.

PJs, teeth brushed, three stories, and then Amelia told me it was bedtime! She put her head down, closed her eyes, and listened to me singing to her, while stroking her forehead. Minutes later she was gently snoring away. Ahh, that was easy, I thought! But within a half hour she was awake and calling, and then awake and coming to the door of her room. She didn't cry but asked for her mommy and daddy, and was successful at keeping herself awake, despite the deep breathing and blowing out all the air that I was encouraging! She works hard keeping those eyes open! Susan and Kevin walked in about ten p.m. and Susan took over. Kevin said the movie was great and they were happy to have seen it.

It was just 10:15 when we walked in the door, so I suggested we continue watching the (DVD) movie Doug had started over there. Off we go...

Riku quote, sent by Bryn tonight: he was reading his dictionary (or "decorative" as he calls it)

Grandpa's Girl and Tuck-In with Riku

Amelia arrived just after 8:30 this morning and was with me until 5:30; the day flew by. Susan told me that when she told Amelia she was coming to my house today, the first thing she said was "Play-doh!" And so we went right upstairs, after waving goodbye to Kevin, and I got the "green one" as requested. Amelia is happy to eat while playing and finished off her eggs, "sausage", and toast. She puttered about happily, sitting at the little kitchen table, perusing the new Hallowe'en books, and pushing the bumblebee around the house.

But mostly she wanted to spend as much time with Doug as she could manage today. At one point, she wandered into the living room, relocated the chicken sitting on a child's chair, carried the chair carefully into the dining room, and lined it up so that she was sitting right beside Doug at his desk. Of course she wanted some attention too, so exclaimed over the kaleidoscope, which she can pronounce perfectly!, and gave him big hugs around his neck too. What a girl!

Amelia didn't come empty handed today; Susan had sent over a bag full of goodies for us... gorgeous yellow flowers, a container of Belgium chocolate pudding, a jar of Trader Joe's mayonnaise which got "best-of" reviews, a package of "bacon" for Riku, as she knew I was out of it, and a package of Lucca's spinach filled ravioli, which we had for a delicious fast dinner tonight. That girl... how she spoils us!

Amelia has a cold, with a cough too, and we knew she was a tired girl. Even though she settled in very nicely, she napped for only 40 minutes in total, and that was that. However, she was happy and I didn't try to get her back to sleep, a quite pointless endeavour at the best of times.

Lunch time and Amelia once again carried her chair, into the kitchen this time, placing it right beside Doug's chair as he ate his lunch! I put her into her own chair while she ate, but she sat right beside him until it was ready. After lunch she managed a little snuggle with him too. Then followed... painting, more books, some time upstairs on her own, until it was time for an afternoon nap. Again she settled in very nicely but awoke forty-five minutes later, happy as could be.

Off we went outside with some snacks, and two brooms. Amelia was very helpful, moving Riku's four trucks to a new spot, after dumping the rain water out of them, so that we could sweep under them. It was perfect outside for this; the day was cool but not cold, and I did a great clean up back there, putting away the three deck chairs in the storage area at the side of the house.

Some funny Amelia tales today... she ruffled my hair quite vigourously whilst proclaiming "pretty hair, Nana!" and then saying "big" after it was all puffed up! Something happened in the laundry room while she was playing with the various plastic toys, and Doug and I both heard her say "oh, my goodness!" Amelia gave lots of spontaneous hugs, not at all unusual, kissed me on the shoulder today as I put her sandals on, sang happily away to herself, paid attention to the children's songs on the cd, and repeats a chorus, and sings "ABCD..." so very well, for a just-turned-two year old. I managed to get it on video tape today and must do more taping of both children.

She remembered she had enjoyed the "I like you" man on the computer an d asked for it by that name, and then had a turn at knocking the little bears down from their stack, another computer game she likes. Now that she is using the mouse herself for this, she looks quite the professional!

Susan came by at 5:15, and of course we had to show her some of Amelia's favourite things we did today... we had played a matching game, which Amelia did so easily, with six cards, three pairs. So I added another pair and that was easy for her too. Susan hadn't thought she was ready for this yet, but she is more than ready, and I'm sure they will be playing it at home. Amelia also showed her mom the "I like you!" man on the computer and her prowess with the bears too.

I quickly made dinner, and we left the house at 6:15 to babysit Riku while Akane and Bryn went out to a movie; Riku and Bryn were playing with an elaborate train set when we walked in, and Riku was happy to let Grandpa take over when Bryn left. Bryn told Riku to tell me "just do the dinosaur puzzle first" when told him it was bedtime. He is doing so well at the puzzles, and ones that I find difficult! but by the time he was ready for bed it was too late for puzzles. I had played with the trains with him for a bit, we both drew in the living room for a while, read a story while he ate a piece of toast, and by then it was pj, teeth brushing and tuck in time. Despite his cough and congested breathing, or perhaps because of it, he was asleep within ten minutes easily, at 7:45. Akane had told me he had napped for only an hour this afternoon so I wasn't surprised at all then he dropped off so quickly.

Akane and Bryn arrived home at 9:10 and off we went home. We had thunder and lightning earlier in the evening, a rare event here in San Francisco, but it ended fairly quickly.

Doug and I home at 9:30; up we went to watch a taped show and of course I fell asleep. I will catch up tomorrow on how it ended but for now it is bedtime... almost midnight. Too late!

A Day at Home and a Wee Visitor too...

Since Doug and I took the afternoon off yesterday, the plan was to stay at home today and pretend it was a weekday. So, I got the ironing done whilst listening to NPR; I heard the funniest story, read by a woman author, Aggie Tippery, who teaches writing classes in Minnesota. I laughed out loud several times and thought how much Fay would have loved her sense of humour.

Later Doug looked her up on the Net, as I would have ordered her book but alas, it is out of print. Doug had gone to his Tai Chi class at 10 a.m. and then got home and went right to work on the computer. I wrapped birthday and Christmas gifts, tidied up a little in the kitchen, answered a couple of emails, and had Doug order three books for me from Amazon.

Bryn and Riku were out running errrands this morning; Bryn had called saying they would drop by around 1 p.m. as he planned to wrap Riku's birthday gifts here while Riku had his nap.

He was a tired boy, quite pale, but needed a little lunch first. He ate quite a bit of leftover noodle and veg' soup, half an English muffin, and some juice, but really protested giving in to his nap. It was so obvious that it was just what he needed, but he was overtired and not co-operative. I put his cotton blanket in the dryer for a minute, tucked his favourite stuffed dog in his arms, and held him until he quietened down. And then he slept for three and a half hours! He woke very briefly, less than a minute each time, three times during this period but I just "hushed" him, and left the room.

Riku is sooo much better today. It was a pleasure to hear such even breathing and not struggling to breathe through a clogged nose. However, the crying brought on some coughing which was hard to see, but he didn't cough at all once he fell asleep.

While he was napping I made two carrot cakes and had those both baked by the time Riku woke. I will ice them in the morning and shall make a single layer chocolate cake as well, for the family party to be held here tomorrow night at 6 p.m.

He awoke slowly and I lay with him for about ten minutes, as he slowly came round to opening those eyes all the way. Before his nap, Riku had told me he wanted to play with the Play-Doh so he sat at the table eating a snack of strawberries and another half of an English muffin.

Bryn came in from picking up Akane and a grocery store run, as Riku was finishing up with the Play-Doh, and told me a funny story. Riku was reluctant to sit on the toilet today but I promised him a little surprise if he agreed to try. He did and was successful and very happy with the small cement truck I gave him. That little truck and his big "Caterpiller" truck kept him busy for a while too.

They went off at 6 p.m. and Riku will be back over early tomorrow morning so his parents can prepare for his party at 2 p.m. Doug and I had a delicious dinner, which took only twenty minutes to prepare. We bought a new Quorn product yesterday at Rainbow, a "cutlet" with Gruyere cheese, and I baked a sweet potato in the microwave, heated up creamed corn for Doug, and quickly made myself a small side dish of sauteed chard with pine nuts, and a little balsamic vinegar, which took all of five minutes to cook.

Off we went upstairs to watch the first three hours of our newest Netflix DVD "Angels in America". I took up a very large bowl of fresh walnuts to shell, a gift from Celeste last week. I have three big bowls to crack and this seemed like the perfect opportunity. I got through one and a half bowls tonight, and shall finish off the rest tomorrow night. They take up far less room like this and I shall put them in plastic bags in the fridge for banana breads over the next year! I am the only one in this household who likes walnuts, but Susan happily took some home with her last week.

"Angels in America" is just wonderful with a great cast of actors... Meryl Streep and Al Pacino amongst them. It was easy to stay awake for this, although it helped that I was busy with the walnuts I'm sure.

Doug and I went through some photos, managing to delete well over half of some taken at the music festival in early October. We were so far from the stage that we didn't get any shots of the singers, but we did get lots of good ones of Riku! Once I annotate those they will be up for viewing.

It was served with a green salad, a delicious roll, a chocolate chip cookie, and I finished off with hot tea. Our choice was "English" or "green".

I dozed off and on while watching two movies, "Must Love Dogs" and "Bruce Almighty". They helped pass the time but I was glad I hadn't paid to see them in a theatre. Having the extra seat beside me really helped with fatigue as I could stretch out and take up as much space as I chose to, without being aware of a passenger beside me. The time went by very quickly, and at 7 p.m. we were served a snack of sun chips, a tiny Toblerone bar, an almond cookie, and beverage service. I drank lots of water on the plane and had several cups of very strong tea with lots of milk.

It was strange to look out the window at 7:30 and see a brilliant blue sky with puffy white clouds. Of course I still had my watch set to SF time, so was used to it being dark by that time. A third movie kept me partially awake for a bit longer, although I also finished off the second of my magazines; "Bewitched" wasn't enough to hold my attention without a second diversion.

At 11 p.m. we were served a mini meal; I was glad it was hot and even though it was delicious it consisted of more sweet potatoes, this time mixed with white potatoes and baked. It came with a boring white bagel, cream cheese, applesauce, and two cookies, all of which I saved for Riku. I had noticed that at both lunch and dinner the non-vegetarian meals had come with a fruit bowl, and so I asked if there were any extras. The very thoughtful attendent brought me two of them, consisting of cantaloup, honeydew melon, and grapes. I ate both of them and then packed the two containers another attendent brought to me.

By 11:30 the sky had changed to the most beautiful pink with streaks, and it continued for the longest time. The pilot announced at 11:30 that we were passing over Mt. Fuji, and I was fortunate enough to be on the right side of the plane for this. I snapped a couple of photos, but by that time it was dark enough that they may not have turned out well. Hard to tell while they're still in the camera.

We landed at 11:55 p.m. S.F. time, just five minutes short of an eleven hour flight. Somehow we cut an hour and fifteen minutes off the original estimated time of arrival. It was such a good trip for me; I wasn't exhausted or nearly as anxious to get off the plane as I have been on the past two trips. That extra stetching space really made a difference.

My bags took a long time to come down the conveyer belt, but finally I went through customs and out to the big hallway to spot Bryn and Riku sitting on a bench waiting for me. It was so good to see them, and Riku was just so sweet. He gave me huge hugs, touched my face, put our noses together, and then more hugs. A few minutes later he said out of the blue, "I missed you Nana." and I told him that I sure had missed him too.

The train to Sakai-shi was waiting but we had time to get the suitcases on board, my two carry-ons stowed above us and the stroller folded up before it left. Riku immediately wanted the snacks I had told him I had with me, and he ate most of the applesauce, he and his dad each had a cookie although Riku insisted I have one half of his. We sang songs and chatted and looked at the lights outside, and before I knew it we were getting organized to get off for home. All looked very familiar and, after starting down the hallway to 2905, we could smell the tomato sauce Akane had prepared for our pasta dinner.

Akane is looking "out front" and big with this baby, but her doctor told her two days earlier that it wouldn't be any time soon. Not what we wanted to hear! The couple of hours to bedtime dashed by... our delicious dinner which included a green salad, some time admiring Riku's new fire station, which he put together on his own, with just a bit of help from Bryn, and then tuck-in time. While sitting at the table I told Riku Amelia had put some Play-Do in her mouth (I had told him I brought some with me for him) and Riku's response was "Oh, that was not good. What Auntie Susan say?" That was a new expression from him and we got quite a chuckle out of it.

The plan from now on is to have Riku sleep on his own in the small middle bedroom; he has been sleeping with his dad in the tatami room up to now, which is to be my room. However, we shall see how it goes. I tucked Riku into his bed, but he woke at the 45 minute mark and then again a half hour later. By that time it was close to 10:15, Akane and Bryn went off to bed and I decided I would do the same. And thus ends my first night in Japan.
