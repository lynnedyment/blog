My San Francisco Girl

Walking Amelia means discovering new places in the city; I've been to Golden Gate Park many times but now access is close and immediate, so we're exploring. I had three hours out with Amelia this morning, it was another beautiful day, and so we walked and walked... through a beautiful dahlia garden (or Lady-in-the-Lighthouse, as Gram called them), and through the most gorgeous rose garden, where I stopped many times to smell the roses. From there I discovered a wonderful playground, perfect for young children, complete with huge sandbox areas and smaller pint-sized equipment, just perfect for Riku right now, and soon to be perfect for Amelia too. Further along, much further, was Stow Lake, so I walked around that one and a half times, deciding that next spring, when the Dyment/Tilston crew arrives we will rent paddle boats and see the amazing waterfall up close. Amelia slept for an hour and a half and woke up smiling, as always.

Late this afternoon we had another shorter walk, and on the way home Amelia surprised me with a very unexpected loud, very loud!, squeal... sheer pleasure, so I imitated her and we exchanged squeals until she decided to go back to her loud and wet raspberries.

Just before I left her with her mom tonight she surprised us both with a new skill; Susan had just installed a new light plate in her room, Amelia was admiring it and Susan held her close to see and touch it... and she turned it on, and then off! Red letter day at the Doering/Ellis household, and Amelia was as pleased with herself as we were... big grins and claps all 'round!
