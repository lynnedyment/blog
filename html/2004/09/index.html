Last Day with Riku... for Too Long!

Bryn dropped off my smiley little guy this morning so that his Mommy could get all her packing done and get a good sleep tonight, for their long long trip to Japan tomorrow. I was delighted to have this day with him, as all the laundry is done, the house is looking just fine, and all I really have to do is pack.

I made Riku a full portion of four grain cereal and I finished up my hot chocolate; Riku enjoys looking at magazines and/or the paper while he has breakfast, so it's a very pleasant half hour together. Riku wanted to stay there when he finished, so I gave him the button box to play with on the table. Today he had a big flat plastic container for them too, and spend his time transferring them from the box to the container and back again. Once again he handed over any buttons with thread in the holes for me to "cut?".

Nap time followed, and Riku headed off to turn on the "ocean" sound in his room while I shut the blinds. He is more than happy to crawl up into his bed at naptime, as he knows exactly what the routine is. I hold him in my arms and he asks for his animals... the monkey, two dogs, and two mice. Always. There is no room for more than these; his arms are full as he drifts off to sleep. I take all but the monkey and one dog from him before I put him down, although often I tuck one under his leg or foot for comfort.

Forty minutes later, on the dot as usual, he awoke and while I made some puddings, banana bread, and granola, Riku wandered in and out of the garden. Once again I put out his snacks and his chair for him; it is really sweet to see him learning how to boost himself up into the chair that is just a tad too high for him. He perseveres though, and is successful each time now. He walked the beam again today and I videotaped him doing so, for posterity.

I had glasses to pick up so decided that Riku could nap in his stroller while I ran errands. We were out for three hours and walked a very long distance, a perfect thing to do the day before a long plane trip. It was hot on the walk; I told Riku we were going home and he asked first for "water?" and I said "sure" knowing he wanted to play in the yard, and next came "hose?", not quite so clearly as "water", but understandable. I bent down to tell him yes, and he told a little story by saying "trees?" and pointing both hands held together upwards. Yes, by all means he could water the trees, and then he put his hands on his head, grinned and nodded, and yes, I too remembered the last time when he managed to get himself all wet. Communication is improving every single day now. Riku was out in just his diaper and rubber sandals today and looked adorable, watering everything in sight.

Tub time came next and a hair wash for both Robin and Riku. It's much easier for Riku to have his hair washed if we do Robin's first and it's fun too, as Riku does the rinsing. Next came a big dinner once again of whole wheat pasta with spinach, mushrooms, and tomatoes, followed by a big portion of homemade applesauce and half a cookie. I think Riku is going through a growth spurt, as he is happy to eat something fairly hearty every two or three hours.

Akane, Bryn, and Brent came over at 7 p.m., just as the brownies were coming out of the oven, to take us out for dinner at a taqueria we all like a lot and rarely go to; Bryn's treat tonight. It was very nice not to think about what I was going to eat tonight; the day was busy enough. Our food was delicious; Riku is very fond of tortilla chips and had a couple of bites of a quesadilla too. That tummy is very round these days!

It was very difficult saying goodbye to Riku; I can't imagine not seeing him for six weeks and he will change so much. He knows he is going on a big plane with his mommy tomorrow but he doesn't know how far away it is taking him from us.

And here I sit writing, and I should be upstairs packing. I'm not going to make my midnight deadline as, instead of finishing up the kitchen work, I phoned two friends to say goodbye and then went out at 10:30 p.m to sweep the patio! That made so much sense too, as I won't see it for a month! The cool air felt good though, and as soon as I get the dishes finished up I shall go upstairs and pack.

Susan and Amelia will be here to pick me up at 8:30 a.m. for my 10:20 flight to Buffalo. This worked out very nicely indeed as I get to see Amelia again too; she just might be walking the next time I see her! In fact, I could bet on it.

An Amazing Co-Incidence and the Rest of My Day

Just after finishing a breakfast of perfectly poached eggs, sauteed Ontario tomatoes, and whole wheat Italian toast, here at Gerry and Lionel's this morning, the phone rang. It was a cousin of Lionel's from Mississauga calling to chat and to catch up on summer news. At one point Gerry handed the phone over to me, as she had told this gal that her cousin Lynne was visiting from San Francisco. Since she has a son in SF, she was curious as to where I lived. Sooo, we chatted a bit, her son lives on Delores Street, she occasionally visits so we arranged that the next time she was there she would give me a call and we could spend some time together. Her son works, and has no family there, so she spends her days wandering about on her own. I asked where her son works and was told at Sony downtown. Hmmm, interesting, I told her, as I knew two fellows his age who also worked at Sony and suggested she get a piece of paper and pen and write the names down as perhaps her son knew them. The first was Sean Coady and the second Brent Williams. Brent Williams she asked? Yes. Well, Brent was her son!!!!!

I'm telling you, we were all just astounded at this incredible co-incidence! Jackie Williams mother and Lionel's mother were sisters! She couldn't believe that Bryn Dyment was MY son as she has met him and been outside my house and on and on we went. I told her we had had a picnic together last Sunday and promised to send her photos via email, and I sent a second album too, in which Akane and Brent are in the kitchen cooking. Is this amazing????

Following this astounding event, I drove over to Uncle Frank's and spent three and a half hours with him. We were both busy, he with photo related computer jobs while I did some sorting for him.

At 6 p.m. Gerry, Lionel, and I arrived at Marie and Graham's for a barbecue dinner... two types of macaroni salad, a really delicious potato salad, baked beans, pork chops for everyone else, and a very tasty spinach filled "cutlet" for me.

Graham brought out a big box of chocolate covered ice cream on a stick for part of our dessert, which really hit the spot.

It was a lovely night for sitting outside; the day had been hot and humid but the back yard was just perfectly comfortable until the mosquitos started to bite. In we went for a cup of tea and banana bread for those who wanted extra dessert, and then we headed for home, watched some t.v. while I soaked my feet in a foot bath and scrubbed away at my feet. Ahhh, very relaxing and now I should head off to bed.

The rest of the day and evening passed quickly... big snack outside for all of the children, after a short burst of rain earlier... piano practice for Bry' and Myk', then Myk' and Caillie did one of Caillie's birthday crafts... piano lessons for Bryn and Caillie, quite a few games of "Guess Who" with both Bryn and Caillie (Myk' and Bry' went off with their mom at 5:45 p.m.),and finally a late, light dinner of Gregg's homemade chicken soup and sourdough toast for the others, while I ate an egg and toast.

Bryn hurt his knee today during the school soccer try-outs today, and we are hoping it will be healed enough by tomorrow that he can try again tomorrow.

At bedtime, while Kelly read to Caillie, I read Bryn two chapters of a book about grizzlies I bought for his birthday last year; however, upon reaching the point where the farmer finds one of his bulls injured, takes his rifle and searches until he finds the mother grizzly and her four cubs, Bryn decided that was a very good point to stop. He wasn't going to risk hearing something sad before going to sleep. What a wise boy this is (I looked ahead!). I read a few pages from another book while he waited for his mom to come down and tuck him in.

Chatted with Carol on the phone, wrote some emails... and off I go to bed at 10 p.m.... or soon!

16 September 2004

Happy Birthday Caillie

Caillie turned seven today; she had birthday pancakes and a Happy Birthday song to acknowledge her special day, before heading off to school.

Kelly and I drove the Bryn and Caillie to school, and then Gregg to work, where I had a tour of his lovely office; Daniel Lanois lives right across the street from this old renovated stable and then a residence, before being converted to an office building. Interior brick walls, large open spaces, and a deck make this one of the most appealing offices I have seen.

Kel' and I thought we would look for loot bag goodies in Chinatown but alas, nothing opened until 11 a.m., not even the bakery where we walked to buy tofu buns while waiting for the regular retail stores to open. Finally we managed to find some great containers for Bryn's party and bowls for the girls, as well as items to put in them.

Lunch at La Commensal was a real treat. I bought a huge plate of food and brought more than two thirds of it back home for extra meals. I have a hard time not choosing every vegie dish on the buffet table.

The dollar store was next where we finished the loot bags, then a quick trip to the grocery store for snacks for the party, and finally home. It was chilly today so at least we weren't rushing around and walking in the humidity of the last few days.

Myk' and Bry' were here with us until 5:30, and after piano practice there was time for Bry' to beat me in a game of "Guess Who" and another game which was new to me... can't recall the name now. I had hoped to have had dinner with Carol tonight but she wasn't able to make it, and Kelly and Gregg had dinner plans with friends as well, which were also cancelled. So, it was a quiet night at home for all of us; we watched some t.v., had some take-out food for dinner in front of the t.v. and I turned in after the two hour Oprah biography show. It was cold and I was happy to be in bed snuggled up reading.

17 September 2004

Two Birthday Parties

Bryn's early birthday party was held at Hans' today whilst Caillie's was held at Kelly's and Gregg's, both from 2 p.m.-4:30. This morning, Gregg set up a stage on the back deck for the "Rock Star" event for Caillie, complete with backdrop, a poster, and a white board with the names of the "stars". Backstage was a karaoke machine and lots of cd's. I made chocolate cupcakes, Kelly and I put together the loot tumblers and bowls and they looked great, and all was ready for eight little girls by 2 p.m.

At Kelly's, the girls came in, some got right into the makeup while others chose some special clothes to wear. Meanwhile, at Bryn's party, he was being decorated with whipped cream and toppings, on his chest and face, which the guests dug into after they finished the job! I missed out seeing this but Hans did show me a few photos... weird stuff!!!! :) Bryn was beaming though and obviously enjoyed it all; he loves a party even if he is the "cake"!

The rock show started off a little quietly as the girls took some time to warm up but eventually they did and by the end of the last session most of the girls were on stage as back up singers and dancers. Kelly made a tape and it will be lots of fun to see that next week when I am back in Toronto.

Things were winding down nicely by 4:15 so we got everything cleaned up and organized in time to leave the house at 6 p.m.... me to the bus terminal for a 6:30 bus to Niagara Falls and Kelly and Gregg to a dinner party and club night in Oakville.

Gerry, Lionel, Cheryl, and Kenny were there to pick me up at 8 p.m. Tea and tidbits at Tim Horton's, home to make a couple of phone calls, and off we went to bed at 11 p.m.

18 September 2004

A Relaxing Sunday in NFO

What a lovely day this was! Gerry made what has become our traditional Sunday breakfast... perfect poached eggs, whole grain toast, and fresh tomatoes heated up to spoon over the toast... and a pot of tea.

I had a long leisurely chat with Candy, and rolled some of the fifty plus pounds of pennies of Uncle Frank's.

At 1 p.m. we left with a neighbour and good friend of Gerry's and Lionel's, Nancy, to attend a play at the Shaw Festival. We all very much enjoyed "Ah, Wilderness" by Eugene O'Neill. It was funny, touching, and very well acted; the tickets were only $10, as unsold seats are available to family and friends of the actors a day or two before it plays. Gerry knew the 12 year old boy in the play, so that made it even more interesting. I always forget how much I enjoy live theatre until I attend a play; I must do so more often.

It was a perfect fall day, crisp but sunny, and we drove past Tricia Romance's beautiful home in Niagara-on-theLake before heading home on the backroads through Virgil and past where Becky and Wayne lived.

The three of us were very hungry by this point so we had pizza at Boston Pizza for dinner, rented "Calendar Girls", came home, made a pot of tea, and just relaxed for the rest of the evening. I have seen the movie before so sat and rolled some more pennies, getting lots done.

My day ended with an unexpected and very pleasant phone call from Bryn!... the first time I have spoken with him since I left S.F. We caught up on some Riku/Amelia/apartment/job news, and now I'm off for another early night.

19 September 2004

Glorious Autumn Day

I have had the most amazing weather since arriving in Ontario and today was an example of a perfect fall day. It started off quite cool and was cool overnight, but by early afternoon the sun was hot and the day as sunny and gorgeous as could be.

I had some time with Beth in the morning; also saw Tammy and Elizabeth when they came to pick Beth up... computer problems needing attention. Drove to UF's and made fried egg sandwiches and a pot of tea for lunch. We had a very productive afternoon, clearing away boxes of craft materials, another of appliances, and a big box of gifts.

I left at 5:15 and drove to Ian's for a three hour visit. His new home is really lovely, comfortable and the tidiest and most well organized home I have ever been in. There is a wall of family photos, a well stocked bookcase, a place for everything and everything in its place. Mom would have been very proud of this son she raised. It was such a pleasure to sit there and catch up on all that has gone one since January; he filled me in on his final chemo sessions, he weighs more than he has since high school and he looks just wonderful. He made me a perfect cup of tea and had two slices of zucchini and walnut bread beside it for a snack for me.

From Ian's I drove to Sheldon's; Donna was upstairs in their room so we went down to the rec' room where Shel' showed me their new bathroom, complete with a steam/shower and a huge jet tub. What a great job the two of them have done renovating their home. We had a two hour visit, also catching up on our news with each other. It was fun exchanging grandchildren stories with him. It's not often I have a one-on-one visit with my number two brother, and I really enjoyed the time with him.

Home at Gerry's and Lionel's at 11:15... bed too late, but maybe tomorrow...

20 September 2004

Last Night in Niagara Falls... again!

This will be short and sweet as I am too tired to think straight. It was a very busy day, packed every minute with one more little job I wanted to get to... unpacked the car from last night, drove to WalMart to copy two photos of Uncle Harry, bought new jeans, more penny rollers, cards, then drove to Beth's to deliver goodies (which she was delighted with)and squeezed in two games of cards too!, and then had a relaxing lunch at "John's" with Uncle Frank.

Back at his house we emptied his big freezer, and then I puttered around doing a few easy little jobs... made up some of Auntie Rita's rhubarb, another of black currants she picked last summer, and left three dessert dishes of custard and fruit for UF to snack on while I'm gone and more to make up himself. It is such a pleasure to cook for Uncle Frank, especially when I know how much he enjoys these little extra treats.

At 5 p.m. we went to Ian's to drop off the frozen bits... met Lisa, who was charming and friendly, Ian showed Uncle Frank through his new abode, and then we met up with Vicki, Mitchel, and Alanna!, who (along with David, of course) are living just a few doors down from Ian while their new house is being completed. I took a photo of the children, so eventually it will be up in an album. They were very sweet and friendly, even though they don't know me from Adam.

Leaving there we drove to St. Catherines for a shared plate of eggplant parmigiana at "Lena Linguine's". UF liked it very much too!

Had a few minutes chatting with Gerry before she went off to bed... and now I plan to do the same thing, before (once again) it is midnight before my light goes out.

21 September 2004

Back to Toronto...

Caught the 11:15 a.m. bus back to Toronto this morning, after spending most of the morning sorting through all the bags, suitcases, etc. in my room, in preparation for leaving in a big hurry in another week and a bit. Oh, also bought some delicious tofu rolls from the Chinese restaurant opposite the bus terminal for my lunch on the bus; their food is delicious and I enjoyed every bite of it.

Once in Toronto, I stored my big bag in a locker and did some birthday shopping for Kelly downtown. Took the subway home in time to meet the children at 3:30 at school. Had some time to chat with Kelly, which was lovely, while the children played outside and/or practiced the piano.

After Bryant left with his mom (Myk' was at a friend's), Bryn and I caught the subway downtown for dinner at Movenpic... cheap date... pizza for him and a shared plate of rosti between us.

Quiet evening at home, watched a little t.v., did a bit of laundry, answered some emails, and read all of Akane's latest blog entries which I couldn't access from Gerry's.

Such a pleasure to see Riku's little face on the blogs. I'm missing both Riku and Amelia a lot this week. Three weeks is a long time away from those ever-changing little ones.

22 September 2004

Off to Las Vegas!

... Kelly and Gregg that is! They left at 6 a.m. today and just as Kelly predicted I didn't get back to sleep. That was fine though as I had my shower over with early and was all set when the children wakened. It was a busy day, filled with the usual walks to and from school (just Bryn and Caillie home for lunch), some time throwing the ball to Manitou to tire him out for his naps, and some general work around the house.

I did have a long chat with Vicki this morning and then a longer one with Gail tonight. Vicki will be with me tomorrow morning for a visit and Gail sounds just awful; that poor girl is in obvious pain and has been told to take sixteen Tylenols a day for pain. Surgery in three plus weeks which will be a very long time indeed with the pain she is having tonight.

Walked Caillie to piano, while Bryn was at his final soccer try-out; he made the school team! and we are all delighted for him. He plans to try out for badminton as well. No, Bryn Sr., I don't think they have ping pong at the school.

Bryant and Myk' were here after school for a couple of hours; snacks, piano practice, and a trip to the piano teacher's house with Caillie, after which their mom picked them up.

I'm more than ready for bed tonight...

23 September 2004

Off to Bed...

after a lovely, but very busy day. That seems to be the only kind there are here, and in the Falls too! As a result the time is flying by faster than I ever imagined it would; Bryn called this afternoon and reminded me that I would be home a week today! Hard to imagine! So much still to do, so little time!

Today was again unseasonably hot at 29C. Vicki arrived just a few minutes after I walked the children to school, and went with me to the alley while Manitou had his fifteen minute "catch the ball" session. It was nice and cool in the basement so we went down there to chat while I did the ironing and got the last load of washing in.

Bryn and Caillie hadn't seen Vicki for quite a long time, possibly two years, but she looked familiar to them and gave her big hugs when we met them at school at 11:30. We all had grilled cheese sandwiches for lunch, and then drove the children to school... we were off to pick up Cath's gorgeous mixed breed dog from her grooming session, did some shopping at a hardware store and dropped the dog off at Cath's and Mike's home in the city.

All four children came home with me, and we quickly organized Bryn and Bryant for their 4 p.m. pickup to Jacob's birthday party. The girls spent an hour or so playing dress-up and came down looking very glamourous and willing to pose for a photo. We had dinner at home instead of going out for pizza, and watched a movie... "Annie" that was in the house. I think we were all too hot to think about walking out again.

The boys came home at 8 p.m., watched the end of the movie with us, and off they all went to bed, a tired group, at 9 p.m. First however, I lined them up on the side of the tub and washed eight very dirty feet.... actually, six, as Myk's were amazingly clean, thanks to runners and socks on her feet all day. Caillie definetely won the dirty feet award tonight but Bryant came in a close second.

Kelly and Gregg called to talk to the children; they just loved, loved, loved "O", the big show in Las Vegas, but were wondering if they were going to find enough to do for the next two full days, not being gamblers. Maybe one trip to Las Vegas will be enough for them.

10:40 and I'm off to bed. Bryant and Bryn have requested an 8 a.m. wake-up call to clean their room! I think Bry' is the one who is super keen but Brynnie will happily cooperate.

24 September 2004

What a Grand Day!

I awoke at 6:45 this morning and had my emails read and a shower before the boys woke up, a little earlier than their intended 8 a.m. awaking but not by much. True to their promises last night they got right to cleaning their room, which really meant sorting it out, (putting all the Lego pieces in the right container etc.), and doing a really wonderful job on it. This took a grand total of twenty-five minutes of steady work and I promised to do the vacuuming and dusting.

At 9 a.m. we all sat down for breakfast together and then went out on the deck to try to recreate the photo of the four of them last summer, with Manitou, who was just a pup. Well, he was much more co-operative last year, as this year we could not pursuade him to lie down in front of them. Last year he sat on Bry's lap, and this year he could have stretched out on all four of them.

I "interviewed" Bryant, our second annual attempt at this. I think it will be fun for all of us to see these interviews years from now. Unfortunately, my camera battery needed charging so Bry' was the one and only one done today.

Myk' and Bryant left with their mom at 10 a.m. and at noon Bryn, Caillie, and I were at Ontario Place. It was a perfect day for it, with 24C temps and some cloud cover. And what a fun day it was; it was the most relaxing day of my visit as we just "hung" out there, doing whatever struck the children's fancy (which included talking me into a log ride! Yikes!). I survived, much to my surprise, but it wasn't my favourite part of the day. Knowing that a forty foot drop is coming after that long climb up does not make me relax and enjoy it one bit.

We ordered a large cheese pizza for lunch, and all had soft drinks with it (they came with the pizza, so what could I do but sit back and enjoy it). We found a table with a big umbrella and ate while the children planned what rides they would go on next.

They enjoyed the big climbing gym several times and then we went on to play miniature golf, which Caillie is so good at! She had two holes in one during the first game and another during our second game.

Both of them loved the "driving school," complete with lights, stop signs, and lines on the roads, which many children completely ignored. As a result, there were a few three car collisions at intersections... lots of fun to watch.

The maze was great fun too; the children went through the first time on their own and then wanted to show it all to me too. It was just terrific and very well done.

Disappointingly, we had saved the water slides and events until after the first golf game, just to find that they closed the water park down at 4:30! Bryn had really been looking forward to it but reconciled himself to another game of golf, and a cup of ice cream before heading for the shuttle to Union Station. Another surprise was the early closing at 6 p.m. as we thought it would be fun to see an IMAX movie, free with our tickets. However...

Manitou was very happy to see us, so while Bryn practiced the piano, Caillie and I took him out to the alley to chase a ball for twenty minutes, along with his friend Jake.

None of us was hungry so we ate lightly... yogurt, some sourdough toast, and a pear, while we watched "Flubber" with Robin Williams. It gave me a chance to finish up the mending while sitting and relaxing at the end of the day with the children. Bedtime at 10 p.m. for two happy and relaxed children... and 11:30 for their Nana.

P.S. Kelly called just as we were coming in the door... 100F and steaming hot on the sidewalks.
