A Relaxing Sunday in NFO

What a lovely day this was! Gerry made what has become our traditional Sunday breakfast... perfect poached eggs, whole grain toast, and fresh tomatoes heated up to spoon over the toast... and a pot of tea.

I had a long leisurely chat with Candy, and rolled some of the fifty plus pounds of pennies of Uncle Frank's.

At 1 p.m. we left with a neighbour and good friend of Gerry's and Lionel's, Nancy, to attend a play at the Shaw Festival. We all very much enjoyed "Ah, Wilderness" by Eugene O'Neill. It was funny, touching, and very well acted; the tickets were only $10, as unsold seats are available to family and friends of the actors a day or two before it plays. Gerry knew the 12 year old boy in the play, so that made it even more interesting. I always forget how much I enjoy live theatre until I attend a play; I must do so more often.

It was a perfect fall day, crisp but sunny, and we drove past Tricia Romance's beautiful home in Niagara-on-theLake before heading home on the backroads through Virgil and past where Becky and Wayne lived.

The three of us were very hungry by this point so we had pizza at Boston Pizza for dinner, rented "Calendar Girls", came home, made a pot of tea, and just relaxed for the rest of the evening. I have seen the movie before so sat and rolled some more pennies, getting lots done.

My day ended with an unexpected and very pleasant phone call from Bryn!... the first time I have spoken with him since I left S.F. We caught up on some Riku/Amelia/apartment/job news, and now I'm off for another early night.

19 September 2004
