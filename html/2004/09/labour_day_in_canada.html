Labour Day in Canada

I had a long relaxing morning at Gerry and Lionel's today. After a very light breakfast, in anticipation of a buffet lunch at the new casino, I helped Gerry enter some email addresses in her computer, wrote a few emails, and made a few phone calls. I was trying hard to get in touch with my brothers, to no avail, although I did reach Wendy. She and Glenn were on their way to London to take Steph's boyfriend to school; too bad as they could have joined Uncle Frank, Gerry, Lionel, their friend Doreen, Beth, Paul, and I for the brunch. We picked Uncle Frank up and were in line for brunch by noon hour. This was Uncle Frank's first visit to the new casino.

Lunch was great. There is a very large selection of food and it was very well done with plenty to choose from. I had a big plate of various salads and finished off with a big bowl of delicious strawberries, another of mixed fruit, and a very tiny slice of cherry pie. We took our time and left just two hours after arriving.

I drove out to Amy and Jake's for a long visit with Amy. Jake was off to Hamilton for the annual Labour Day football game between Hamilton and Toronto. Amy and I spent from 3 p.m. until 11 p.m. catching up on all our news and eating all the various and delicious food Amy had prepared... a tomato and boccocino salad, a big mixed vegetable salad, an eggplant, tomato, and onion casserole, followed by a small dish of vanilla ice cream, strawberries and chocolate sauce... and a big pot of tea. Such a very very relaxing day and evening this was.

On the way home I drove by Uncle Frank's to see if his lights were on. The office and rec' room lights were still lit and I was very tempted to drop in but... it was 11:15 so I drove on by. Just as well as he had found a nice big stack of photos he knew I would enjoy looking at, and I would have been there until 2 a.m.! He has promised to put them aside, smoking... as he put it, until I am back in town next week. I was happy to know it was safe to call him so late and able to say goodbye until next week; I am leaving for Toronto a day earlier than I planned.

Only one and a half more days until I see my Toronto grandchildren!

6 September 2004
