Summer Day in the City

It was 90F (about 33C) here in San Francisco yesterday, and not much cooler today. Doug and I walked Amelia from 11 a.m. until 1:30 p.m. in Golden Gate Park, and Amelia slept for all but twenty minutes of this walk. Susan and Kevin finally managed to see "The Weeping Camel"; I didn't want them to miss it.

It was lovely and cool under the big redwoods, especially along the pathways around Stow Lake. Today we once again saw the egret, but along with it, we saw a log lined with turtles basking in the sun... what a treat! There were some ripened blackberries, thanks to the high temps the last couple of days, so we stopped for about twenty minutes to get our fill of them. When it was time to meet up with Susan and Kevin, Amelia was still sleeping and so Susan walked her for another hour and five minutes, a record nap for Amelia!

Before Doug and I went to see the film "Hero" in the afternoon, we needed to stop for a bite to eat. Doug checked his list of "restaurants we should try sometime" and came up with a winner... "Reggae Runnins", conveniently located just a few blocks from Susan and Kevin's. It is run by Sister Sarah and Ras Wadadah and the food was as excellent as their names are interesting. We shared a big bowl of the daily special, barbecued tofu, with green salad, brown rice, and a most delicious sambosa, a lentil and veggie filled pastry. We will be going back there to try another of their all-vegetarian menu.
