Sunday Afternoon in Kyoto

Home for lunch and then it was time for a fun and favoured once-a-year activity ... colouring eggs. I had taken a kit along with me; they had just started putting out the Easter goodies two days before I left for Japan, so I lucked out. The children spread paper on the table, and we got right to work, colouring ten Easter eggs. Riku and Mirei delighted in the colours and took their time dipping and re-dipping; they all turned out beautifully!

Mirei got a new hair-do today; she had her hair pulled back on the sides, and looks adorable. She spent a lot of time playing with a doll house that Akane brought down from her bedroom, and what a time she had with the tiny furniture; Akane joined her for a while too.

Later on, Mirei decided I too needed a new look and stood behind me on the sofa pushing and combing my hair this way and that. I listened to Riku practicing his piano above us, realizing that I have just two more days with the children before winging my way back to San Francisco. I hope the next few months fly by as quickly as this one did!
