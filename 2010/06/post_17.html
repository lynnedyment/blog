A Visit to Stow Lake with Isobel and Roger

After a lovely and very tasty dinner last night with Isobel and Roger at "Basil Thai", our current favourite Thai restaurant here in the city, we learned that they had part of the day free today. So, we picked them up at their hotel at 10 A.M. and went off for a little tour of the city. We went to the top of the DeYoung Museum in Golden Gate park for the lovely view of the city from that vantage point, then drove along the Great Highway for a look at Ocean Beach. It was a lovely day and we stopped in at Louis' Diner at the top of the old Sutro Baths ruins, and had a great view while we ate a tasty lunch.

Our last stop of the day was a stroll around Stow Lake; I used to walk in this area so often with Amelia and Finn, and visit there with all the children over the years, and I miss it. It's a lovely spot for a stroll, and I must get there more this summer with Riku and Mirei.

Isobel and Roger enjoyed it all, and at 2 P.M. we dropped them back at their hotel for the beginning of their tour of San Francisco by bus, and then a train trip tomorrow to Seattle, heading for Alaska a few days later.

We have visited with them in their home in England, and met up with them two years ago in Las Vegas, so it was fun to spend some time with them again.
